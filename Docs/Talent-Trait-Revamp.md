# GOAT Lite — Talent & Trait Revamp (design report)

**Status:** design ✅ (rev. 2026-06-23 — Umut's review pass applied: bright-register rename, seconds-not-turns, Guardian T3 remade, paladin DR generalised, Archer anti-heal, safe-pick variety, Necromancer deferred). Execution still deferred. Feeds roadmap **C.2** (per-spec talent trees). Companions: `ROADMAP.md`, `Class-Tools-System.md` (spec identities), `MMO-Nostalgia-Reference.md` (Necromancer proposal + naming voice).

> Generated via a multi-agent investigation — WoW (retail Dragonflight/War Within + Classic 51-point) talent-design research → per-spec dossier → parallel tree designs → cross-cut synthesis — then revised per Umut's review. Goal: talents that are **cool and actually matter** — every tier a real playstyle fork, not a stat stick.

## Design contract
- **10 active specs.** Each spec = **15 nodes** = **5 tiers of 3 mutually-exclusive choices** (pick 1 of 3 per tier). The Necromancer's **Bonecaller** (DPS) and **Pallbinder** (healer) are **💤 deferred** — their full trees are kept at the end of this doc (under the deferred banner) for when necro returns; they are *not* in the active 10-spec scope and their gothic names are intentionally left un-revamped until then.
- Each tier is a real **playstyle fork** — AoE↔single-target · survival/defensive↔output · self-sustain↔raw-damage · burst↔sustained · control/utility↔damage · **lean-into↔branch-from the spec's class-tool**. The 3 options are power-equal and win in *different* content; "+5% vs +10%" is banned.
- **Naming register — bright Warcraft-style class-fantasy, IP-safe.** Names are re-flavoured per spec by class school (Fury / Holy / Frost / Arcane / Shadow / Nature / Marksman / …), heroic and classic-MMO in tone — **never the heavy gothic drowned-barrow vocabulary** (barrow/marrow/carrion/drowned/toll/grave/hollow/pall), **and never a verbatim or near-verbatim real WoW spell/talent/resource name** (the public repo + Steam build is a hard pre-launch IP gate — see memory `goatlite-ip-legal-blockers`). The satire stays in the meta/QA wrapper — never in a spell name. *(The rename was machine-audited for cross-spec collisions and WoW-verbatim leakage; residual pre-existing ability-name IP-debt is listed in the synthesis §3.)*
- **Seconds, never turns (player-facing).** The engine runs **continuous seconds**; a "turn" is only an internal authoring quantum = **3 seconds** (`secondsPerTurn = 3`, verified in `engine.ts`). Every duration/cooldown in the player-facing prose below is in **seconds / m:ss**, and the UI (skill tooltips, the talent picker, the replay log) MUST render seconds/minutes and **never** the word "turn." Only the backticked engine-effect tags keep `cooldownTurns`/`durationTurns` field names (the implementation unit). *(Implementation seam: the picker/tooltips still need a turn→second display pass — fold into C.2 wiring.)*
- **Balance-aware:** the combat regime is going **timer-bound** (P.5c), so talents favour **active throughput / utility / typed-or-conditional defensives** over generic flat survival (the anti-generic-EHP rule the C.11 audit demanded). **Exception (Umut's call):** the **paladin** mitigation tools — Crusader's party damage-reduction and Cleric's absorbs — are now **general** (soak *any* damage), **not** DoT-typed; but they stay **timed cooldown windows you predict and press**, so they're still "a button," not flat always-on EHP.
- Every node is tagged with its **engine effect** — existing vocab (`maxHpPct`/`dmgPct`/`intakePct`/`critPct`/`onlyIf`) or a **NEW** hook. The consolidated NEW-hook list (the engine work this revamp implies) is in the **synthesis** at the end.

> **Sequencing — two distinct senses (the confusing part, untangled):**
> **(1) The engineering keystone** (the *first* implementation milestone): nearly every spec tags nodes "NEW: onlyIf condition X," which looks like ~11 separate engine features. It isn't. The engine **already** has a rich condition evaluator (`condHolds()` in `combat.ts`) that the *talent* path simply doesn't use — talents go through a separate, impoverished switch that knows only 3 conditions and gates only damage. The keystone is **one unification**: route talents through `condHolds()`, widen the talent `onlyIf` enum to the full set, and let conditions gate the **intake** (defense) and **crit** channels too. That single change unblocks conditional defense/crit for **11 of 12 specs**; the per-spec "NEW condition" bullets are *consumers* of it, not separate work. Build it before any per-spec node.
> **(2) The roadmap order:** this whole revamp lands **before P.5c** (the global regime tuning). Talents are sim power inputs, so calibrating the regime first then adding the trees = double-tuning the regime + every dungeon read. (Already ✅ Resolved in `ROADMAP.md` Open Decisions.)

## WoW talent-design patterns (the inspiration)

A field guide for porting WoW's talent design into the auto-battler. Every example name below is illustrative and re-skinnable to the bright Warcraft-style register; the mechanics map to the engine's real fields (`damageType` Physical/Magic, dispel typing Magic/Curse vs Nature/Poison, per-spec resources like Rampage/Burn/Chill/Poison/HoT-stacks, bands front/back, `onlyIf` conditions, `cooldownTurns`). The satire stays in the QA wrapper — never in a spell name.

---

### 1. What makes a talent MEANINGFUL (not a stat stick)

A talent earns its slot when picking it **changes a decision you make during the run**, not just a number on a sheet. The four tests:

1. **It changes behaviour, not just output.** "+8% damage" is invisible at the table; "+22% damage *while 3+ enemies are up*" makes you read the pull. The engine already does this with `onlyIf` (`enemiesAtLeast`, `targetHpBelowPct`, `targetBand`). The *condition* is the design — the percentage is just tuning. (This is exactly the Dragonflight pivot: the revamp's stated goal was **player agency over build + meaningful rewards**, achieved by gating power behind situational triggers and choice nodes rather than flat passives.)

2. **It has an opportunity cost.** WoW's octagonal **choice nodes** give you *one of two* — you cannot have both. Taking the cleave answer means giving up the single-target answer. A talent with no cost (a free passive everyone takes) is not a choice, it's a tax. Your `talents.json` already encodes this shape: each node is a 2–3 option pick where the `"default": true` "balanced" middle option is the *deliberately weaker safe pick* you trade up from.

3. **It expresses an identity / fantasy.** Classic's 51-point capstones (Mortal Strike, Bloodthirst, Shield Slam, Shadowform) were **playstyle-defining**: reaching the bottom of a tree changed *how the class plays*, and the cost was the rest of the tree. The capstone is the punchline of a whole build. Re-skin this as a deep-commitment final node that re-keys a spec's rotation (e.g. a Pyromancer node that turns Burn from DoT into burst).

4. **It interacts with the OTHER systems you already have.** The best WoW talents reach sideways — Convection (cheaper Lightning), or talents that make your dispel also buff, your interrupt also slow. Your `mass-dispel` already does this (cleansed allies deal +10%). A meaningful talent in this game should hook the **problem taxonomy** (P1–P12): it should improve how a spec answers its dungeon problem, not just pad EHP — because the C.11 audit proved **generic EHP was the disease**.

The litmus test, in one line: *if every viable build takes it, it's not a talent — it's base kit.* (Fix: either bake it in, or give it a real rival on the same node.)

---

### 2. The recurring meaningful-choice ARCHETYPES (this-or-that shapes)

These are the reusable "shapes" — the skeleton of a choice node. Pick a shape, then re-skin the names per spec. Each is a genuine trade with no dominant pick across all content:

- **Cleave vs. Execute** — wide damage when 3+ are up *vs.* amplified damage on a low-HP target. (Trash week vs. boss week.) Engine: `enemiesAtLeast:3` vs `targetHpBelowPct`.
- **Single-target vs. AoE** — your `node-2` (Focused Strike vs. Cleaving Blow). The purest dial.
- **Proactive-absorb vs. reactive-burst** — pre-shield the predicted hit *vs.* top the spike *after* it lands. This is the **Crusader vs. Cleric P5 read** as a talent: prevention vs. reaction.
- **Sustained tempo vs. burst spike** — steady always-on output *vs.* a windowed cooldown that front-loads. (Your `node-3` Steady Hand vs. Overload.)
- **Survival vs. throughput** — +max HP *vs.* conditional damage (your `node-1`). The eternal tank/DPS lever.
- **Mobility/reach vs. control** — gain a reach/dive primitive *vs.* gain a CC. (Maps to P11 reach vs. P8 lock.)
- **Single-target reliability vs. spread/contagion** — apply a strong DoT to one *vs.* spread a weaker DoT to many. (Pyromancer Conflagration shape.)
- **Duration vs. magnitude** — a longer, gentler effect *vs.* a short, intense one. (HoT that outlasts the drowning tick vs. a burst bloom — the **Lifebinder P6 vs. P5** identity.)
- **Resource generation vs. resource spending** — build the stack faster *vs.* make spends hit harder. (Rampage / Burn / Poison economies.)
- **Typed-tool specialisation** — your dispel/shield/school becomes better *but narrower* (Magic-only vs. Nature-only; Physical-school vs. Magic-school). Turns "a cleanse" into "the *right* cleanse" — the anti-generic-EHP weapon.
- **Self vs. party** — a buff that only helps you *vs.* a weaker version that helps the group. (The Archer recut deliberately took the self-only side of this; a talent could offer the party side back at a cost.)
- **Conditional spike vs. unconditional floor** — big payoff *if a condition holds* vs. a smaller guaranteed amount. (The "gamble vs. safe" node — your `"default"` middle options are the safe floor.)
- **On-kill / on-crit chain vs. steady value** — snowball mechanics (reset a cooldown on kill/crit, à la Massacre→Blood Frenzy, Shadow Step→Camouflage) *vs.* a flat reliable bonus. Rewards execution and reading the fight.

---

### 3. The ANTI-PATTERNS to avoid

These are the failure modes WoW spent two decades fixing — and the ones your own C.11 audit flagged. Treat each as a hard "no":

- **The boring passive %-node.** "+5% damage / +5% HP" with no condition. Invisible, mandatory, un-fun. *Fix:* attach a trigger (`onlyIf`) so the number teaches a read, or delete it and bake it into base stats.
- **The mandatory pick (a tax, not a choice).** A node where one option is so far ahead that 100% of builds take it. The other options are decoration. *This is the most common sin.* *Fix:* if a node has an obvious answer, it isn't a node — either remove the losers or buff them into genuine rivals.
- **The false choice (illusory branch).** Two options that do almost the same thing, or one strictly dominates the other in all content. The player *feels* like they chose but didn't. *Fix:* the two sides must win in *different content* (the cleave side wins trash weeks, the execute side wins boss weeks).
- **Generic-EHP creep — the cardinal sin here.** The C.11 audit proved stacked party-DR / shields / off-heals scaled survivability against *everything*, so "bring more survival" beat "bring the counter." A talent that adds undifferentiated EHP (flat party DR, a bigger generic shield) is the disease. *Fix:* any defensive talent must be **typed or keyed** — rot-only DR, immunity to *one* event, a shield that only stops the *predicted* hit. Useless against the wrong problem by construction.
- **The trap option.** A talent that looks exciting but is mathematically never correct. Wastes the player's attention and erodes trust. *Fix:* if it's never right, it's a bug — cut it or fix it.
- **Build-locking complexity / mandatory long chains.** Classic's 31-point-deep prerequisite chains forced cookie-cutter routes. A talent buried behind five filler nodes isn't a choice, it's a toll road. *Fix:* keep prerequisite depth shallow; let capstones be reachable by more than one path.
- **The "do my whole rotation for me" passive.** Removes decisions instead of adding them. *Fix:* talents should add a decision (a new condition to watch), not automate one away.
- **Power without identity.** A raw throughput cooldown that any spec could have. *Fix:* tie every notable talent to the spec's signature verb (grip, cancel, anti-heal, HoT-coverage) so the choice reinforces *what this class is for*.

### 4. Iconic talent IDEAS by role

*These are illustrative inspiration only — they sketch the flavor and the choice-shape, not the shipping nodes. The finalized, balanced talents live in the per-spec trees below.*

Each entry: an in-world name + the WoW pattern it borrows + the engine hook + the choice it creates. Names are bright class-fantasy, matched to the established voice.

#### TANK (Guardian / Crusader / Mystic)

- **Sunder Grip** *(Drag-to-the-Dust upgrade — reach/threat).* Your pull also roots the dragged caster for 3s. *Choice vs.* **Bulwark Oath**: instead, your taunt also grants the gripped target's nearest squishy −15% damage-taken. (Reposition-offence vs. peel-defence — the P11/P7 fork.)
- **Thornguard** *(Shield Wall reflect-build).* Your block-wall reflects +50% as Physical. *Choice vs.* **Bastion Aegis**: the wall instead grants 1 charge of full immunity to a flagged ally (P7 negate). (Thorns vs. transfer.)
- **The Mountain Holds** *(Mystic avoidance capstone — Unmoving Mountain line).* Each parry has a chance to reset Earthen Sweep. *Choice vs.* **Stillwater Calm**: parries instead heal the lowest ally (lifesteal-as-group-sustain, the P6 peel side). (Selfish uptime vs. group sustain — never both.)
- **Warden's Burden** *(deep capstone, identity-flipping).* While you hold hard threat, the back band cannot be targeted at all — but your own damage is halved. The wall-or-warden commitment node.

#### HEALER (Cleric / Lifebinder)

- **Lantern of Dawn** *(reactive-burst identity — Cleric P5).* Your instant heal on a target below 30% also leaves an absorb equal to 50% overheal. *Choice vs.* **Steadfast Vigil**: instead, your heals on full-HP allies pre-load a small shield (proactive). (Reactive triage vs. proactive prevention — the P5 shape as one node.)
- **Cleansing Litany** *(typed-dispel specialisation — anti-generic).* Your cleanse strips +1 debuff and grants the cleansed ally +10% power *but only removes Nature/Poison* (Lifebinder). The Cleric mirror, **Sanctified Word**, does the same for Magic/Curse. (Forces the *right* dispel — P4. Re-skin of your live `mass-dispel`/`unbinding-word` split.)
- **Evergreen Renewal** *(duration-vs-magnitude — Lifebinder P6).* Your rolling HoT gains +3s and outlasts the spreading tick, *but cannot crit*. *Choice vs.* **Sudden Bloom**: HoTs bloom for a burst on expiry but are short (loses to a sustained tick). (The P6-coverage vs. P5-spike identity baked into one octagon — the design says Lifebinder must be *weak at the spike by choice*.)
- **Crowned in Light** *(deep healer capstone, smite-atonement).* While any ally is below 30%, your Magic damage heals the party for 50% — turning the healer into an off-school answer to the armour wall (P3) at the cost of pure throughput.

#### MELEE DPS (Berserker / Assassin)

- **Riving Wound** *(Mortal Strike — the iconic healing-reduction execute; already named in the design).* Your strike applies −50% incoming healing for 6s. *Choice vs.* **Reaving Sweep**: instead, hit 2 adjacent for full damage (anti-heal single-target vs. cleave — the P9 vs. P2 fork; Riving Wound is the **only** answer to a self-healing boss).
- **Steeled Patience** *(Cold Blood crit-chain).* After a crit, your next finisher is a guaranteed crit. *Choice vs.* **Bloodglut**: crits instead heal you 3% max HP (snowball burst vs. self-sustain).
- **Shadow Lunge** *(reach/dive primitive — Shadowstep, already in the voice).* Your opener can reach the back band and deals +30% there. *Choice vs.* **Throatcutter**: instead, +100% vs. targets below 20% (reach vs. raw execute — P11 vs. P12).
- **Rampant Slaughter** *(deep capstone, resource-flip).* At max Fury, your execute resets its own cooldown on kill (the Massacre→snowball loop) — converts the spec into a chain-killing soft-enrage answer (P12).

#### RANGED PHYSICAL DPS (Archer)

- **Hunter's Quarry** *(armor-pen / debuff-amp — Mark identity).* Your Mark also makes the target take +30% from *your* shots specifically (selfish focus-fire). *Choice vs.* **Hunter's Largesse**: the Mark instead amps the *whole party's* damage on that target by +15% (the self-vs-party node — the Archer deliberately defaults selfish, this buys back the support side at a personal-DPS cost).
- **Arcing Volley** *(reach specialisation — P11).* Your arcing shot can pick off the sheltered caster and hits 2 in the back band. *Choice vs.* **Piercing Bolt**: instead, a heavier single aimed shot with +50% execute (back-line reach vs. single-target ceiling).
- **Steady Focus** *(burst-window cooldown — already in the kit).* Your aim window also grants +20% crit damage. *Choice vs.* **Unerring Patience**: steady passive +8% crit/power with no window (spike vs. tempo — the P12 enrage fork).
- **The Last Arrow** *(deep capstone).* Below 35% target HP your basic shot fires twice — a pure execute-phase identity for the soft-enrage race.

#### CASTER DPS (Pyromancer / Arcanist)

- **Flame Vents** *(Pyromancer detonate-build — burst identity).* Detonating 4+ Burn stacks grants +25% power (your live detonation cooldown) AND stuns the target if it had Burn — turning the detonation into a P1 *delay* / P8 *lock*. *Choice vs.* **Creeping Embers**: Burn instead spreads to 2 adjacent on every cast (burst-detonation vs. contagion-AoE — the P1/P8 vs. P3 armour-wall fork).
- **Spreading Blaze** *(spread/contagion specialisation — Conflagration line).* Your spread carries *all* stacks and refreshes duration. *Choice vs.* **Smoldering Ash**: instead, each stack ticks +20% harder but cannot spread (wide vs. tall DoT).
- **Frostbind** *(interrupt/lockdown — Arcanist P1 identity; Counterspell pattern).* Your interrupt also adds +6s to the target's next cast (your live interrupt) AND chills it. *Choice vs.* **Ringing Silence**: instead, your silence becomes mass (all enemies) but shorter — the P1-cancel vs. P8-lock fork, the cleanest single-target kick vs. the add-wave lockdown.
- **Frostbound Capstone — Frostwarden** *(deep control capstone).* Your first CC each fight lasts +3s and, while a target is frozen/silenced, your Magic damage to it is +30% — rewarding the lockdown identity with a damage payoff (P1+P8 into P3).

# The 10 active talent trees

*Five tiers of three mutually-exclusive choices per spec. (The two Necromancer trees — Bonecaller / Pallbinder — are deferred; see the banner after Mystic.)*

---

## Guardian — Tank (warrior)

*The immovable iron sentinel. Holds hard threat so the back-line is never free-cast on, drags the sheltered caster onto his shield, and steps avoidable death off his allies — a punishing bulwark who turns every blow he soaks into damage, reflect, and rallying iron.*

### Tier 1 — The Reactive Engine (what powers the bruiser loop: vengeance, thorns, or a heavier anchor swing)
- **Mounting Fury** — Each time an enemy strikes you, your next Vengeful Riposte gains +35% damage (stacking up to 3). The more the pull beats on you, the harder you answer. *Tradeoff:* serves the reactive-bruiser / single-target build — your output is gated behind *being hit*, so it goes quiet when the healer is keeping you topped or the pull is small. `NEW: on-self-hit, grant a stacking outgoing-dmg buff to the next Vengeful Riposte cast (self-hit-counted dmg stacks).`
- **Bristling Bulwark** — Your Spiked Aegis reflects to *every* melee attacker in the front band, not just the one who hit you, and the reflect scales +50%. *Tradeoff:* the AoE / passive-cleave answer — turns a packed pull into free damage with zero buttons, but does nothing on a single boss and never spikes. `NEW: widen thorns-reflect to all front-band melee attackers + reflectPct scalar (multi-target thorns).`
- **Tempered Edge** — +18% damage while 2 or fewer enemies remain; baked into your basic swing, no trigger to manage. *Tradeoff:* the unconditional single-target floor — reliable boss-week throughput that asks nothing of you, but it can't snowball off incoming hits like Mounting Fury or sweep a pack like Bristling Bulwark. `dmgPct: 18, onlyIf: { type: "enemiesAtMost", value: 2 }`

### Tier 2 — The Grip Fork (lean into Hook and Haul → P11, or trade the reach for a brutal punish on whatever you've already caught)
- **Iron Tether** — When you grip a sheltered back-line caster to the front (Hook and Haul), the target is also rooted for 3s, unable to retreat or be re-sheltered. *Tradeoff:* the dedicated P11 answer — locks the dragged caster in your face so the melee can delete it, but spends your whole identity on reach and gives no help against a telegraph or a hard hit. `NEW: grip also applies a 1-turn root (control: stun-equivalent, no-reposition) to the gripped target.`
- **Twin-Chain Haul** — Hook and Haul now drags *two* back-line enemies forward at once instead of one. *Tradeoff:* the AoE-reach build — collapses an entire sheltered back line into melee range for a cleave kill, but the grip no longer roots, so a single priority caster can simply walk back out. `NEW: grip target count 1 → 2 (multi-target grip).`
- **Brand the Caught** — Skip the reach entirely: instead of gripping, your next strike on any target already in melee range deals +28% and applies a Rending Cut. *Tradeoff:* the punisher who gives up the signature tool — pure front-line damage for fights with no sheltered caster, but you have *no answer to P11* and the back line free-casts. `NEW: convert the grip slot into a single-target dmg+Bleed strike (on-demand bleed strike, no reposition).`

### Tier 3 — The Sentinel's Voice (the warband-utility fork: a party-wide rallying shout, an iron-tight single-target threat lock, or a crowd-scattering war cry)
- **Banner of Valor** — A 60s rallying shout: the whole party gains +12% damage and a small absorb for 12s. *Tradeoff:* the group-buff build — a flat power swing for the entire pull that shines on coordinated burst windows, but it's a shared cooldown that does nothing for your own threat and is wasted if the timing is off. `NEW: 60s cooldown party-wide buff — applyStatus party { dmgPct: 12, shieldAbsorb } for durationTurns 4 (rallying-shout party buff).`
- **Unbreaking Glare** — A hard single-target taunt on a short 18s cooldown that *locks* threat: the target cannot switch off you for 9s no matter how much the party out-threats it. *Tradeoff:* the threat-tyrant build — guarantees a loose runner or swap-target snaps to you and stays, the cleanest answer to a threat-sloppy pull, but it's pure control with no damage and helps only one enemy at a time. `NEW: single-target hard-taunt, cooldownTurns 6, applies a 3-turn threat-lock (taunt cannot be overridden) to the target (locked-taunt control).`
- **Daunting Roar** — A 30s war cry that staggers every enemy in the front band: −20% enemy damage for 6s and a brief interrupt on any mid-cast. *Tradeoff:* the panic-button build — buys the group a breather across a whole pack and clips an incoming caster, but it's a reactive defensive tool that adds no throughput and no lasting threat. `NEW: 30s cooldown front-band debuff — applyStatus enemies-front { dmgPct: -20 } for durationTurns 2 + interrupt active casts (AoE soften + interrupt war cry).`

### Tier 4 — Active Mitigation (how the wall holds: reflect the spike, share it, or pre-empt it — never just flat DR)
- **Riposting Aegis** — Iron Bulwark now reflects the blocked damage back at +90% as Physical, turning your defensive cooldown into a burst window. *Tradeoff:* the thorns / reactive-damage build — the more they hit the wall the more they bleed, but it only pays out *while you're being focused*, and offers the party nothing. `NEW: raise Iron Bulwark reflectPct and route reflected damage to the attacker as a damage event (reflect-on-block scalar).`
- **Sheltering Ward** — Iron Bulwark instead converts into a single charge of full immunity you can hand to a flagged ally before a known hit. *Tradeoff:* the proactive-prevention / utility build — the cleanest answer to a predicted one-shot on the back-line, but you lose all the personal reflect and self-block. `NEW: Iron Bulwark becomes a transferable single-event immunity granted to one ally (immunity transfer).`
- **Resolute Footing** — When an enemy ability would hit you while you are *not* at full HP, you take −22% from it — active because you must let yourself drop below full to arm it. *Tradeoff:* the self-sustain bruiser's floor — conditional personal DR that smooths every incoming spike once the fight is rolling, but it's selfish and does nothing at full HP or for allies. `NEW: conditional self-intake reduction keyed to "self below max HP" (below-full self-DR).`

### Tier 5 — The Sentinel's Oath (the deep identity capstone: total threat-lock, lifesteal-anchor, or a soft-enrage cleave finisher)
- **Sentinel's Burden** — While you hold hard threat (Challenge active), the back band cannot be targeted at all — but your own outgoing damage is halved. *Tradeoff:* the absolute threat-anchor build — the wall-or-warden commitment that makes the back-line untouchable (the ultimate answer to free-cast pressure), paid for with your own damage; useless on fights where the threat is already on you. `NEW: while self has hard-taunt up, set the entire back band untargetable + self dmgPct −50% (threat-lock untargetable aura).`
- **Valor Repaid** — Each time you are struck, heal the lowest-HP ally for a share of the blow; while you hold threat this makes you the group's sustain anchor. *Tradeoff:* the self-sustain / group-bruiser build — converts the punishment you soak into party healing (eases a healer-light comp), but every point you give back is a point you didn't put into reflect or damage. `NEW: on-self-hit, heal the lowest-HP ally for a fraction of the damage taken (damage-to-ally-lifesteal).`
- **The Line Breaks** — +24% damage while 3 or more enemies are up, and your kills refresh the grip so you can immediately drag the next sheltered caster — a chain-collapse finisher for trash weeks. *Tradeoff:* the AoE-cleave capstone — turns the Guardian into a pack-shredding threat machine that snowballs through a wide pull, but it does nothing against a single boss and abandons the personal-survival capstones. `dmgPct: 24, onlyIf: { type: "enemiesAtLeast", value: 3 }` `NEW: on-kill, reset the grip cooldown (kill-resets-grip chain).`

**Builds:** (1) **Reactive Reflect-Bruiser** — Mounting Fury → Brand the Caught → Daunting Roar → Riposting Aegis → Valor Repaid: single-target punisher that turns being hit into damage and self-sustain, with a war cry to weather the spikes; (2) **Threat-Anchor Warden** — Tempered Edge → Iron Tether → Unbreaking Glare → Sheltering Ward → Sentinel's Burden: the no-free-cast lockdown that drags, hard-taunts, immunizes, and seals off the back-line; (3) **AoE Collapse Guardian** — Bristling Bulwark → Twin-Chain Haul → Banner of Valor → Riposting Aegis → The Line Breaks: trash-week pack-shredder that hauls back-lines into a thorns-and-cleave grinder behind a party damage banner; (4) **Selfish Survival Wall** — Tempered Edge → Brand the Caught → Daunting Roar → Resolute Footing → Valor Repaid: the unkillable solo anchor for healer-light comps.

## Berserker — DPS (Warrior)

*The physical executioner — the only anti-heal in the game. Pay your own blood to snowball Fury, rive open wounds nothing can close, and delete the dying.*

### Tier 1 — The Price of Wrath (how you pay for power)
The Berserker's core verb is self-damage-as-fuel. This tier decides what that blood buys.
- **Edge of Ruin** — Your Fury stacks hit harder the lower your own HP: while you are below 40% HP, +18% damage. *Tradeoff:* the snowball/burst payoff for a player who lets Savage Frenzy and Wrathful Frenzy bleed them low and rides the edge; gives up the safety floor entirely (you are squishiest exactly when this is live). `NEW: onlyIf condition selfHpBelowPct gating dmgPct (the engine's onlyIf currently keys only targetHpBelow / enemiesAtLeast / enemiesAtMost — a self-HP gate is new). +18% dmgPct under selfHpBelowPct:40.`
- **Lifeblood Renewal** — Your crit-lifesteal is doubled (crits heal 6% max HP instead of 3%) and you gain +12% max HP. *Tradeoff:* the self-sustain bruiser floor — turns the spec into a fight-forever attrition build that needs no healer babysitting; gives up the raw damage of its siblings for a fatter, self-topping body. `maxHpPct:12 (FREE) + NEW: scalar override on Battle Trance crit-lifesteal healHpPct 3→6.`
- **Warbound Cadence** — A modest, identity pick: every third strike adds a small Fury surge (+1 stack) on top of its hit, so your ramp never stalls. *Tradeoff:* the deliberately-safe balanced pick — no HP gamble and no condition to read, just a steady extra trickle of Fury that smooths out slow openers; lower ceiling than either specialist, but it always turns over. `NEW: every-third-strike on-hit Fury-stack rider (+1 stack) — a cadence counter on the rampage resource generator (no periodic resource-gain channel in talents today).`

### Tier 2 — The Shape of the Swing (where your damage lands)
The eternal cleave-vs-single-target dial, tuned to the Berserker's Steel Cyclone/Sweeping Fury AoE vs Reaping Blow execute.
- **Reaving Sweep** — +24% damage while 3 or more enemies are up. *Tradeoff:* the trash-week AoE build's backbone, multiplying Steel Cyclone/Sweeping Fury into packs; dead weight on a lone boss. `dmgPct:24, onlyIf enemiesAtLeast:3 (FREE).`
- **Throat-Finder** — +22% damage while 2 or fewer enemies remain. *Tradeoff:* the single-target / boss-week answer that fattens every hit during a duel; near-useless mid-pull when the room is full. `dmgPct:22, onlyIf enemiesAtMost:2 (FREE).`
- **Even Temper** — +11% damage, always. *Tradeoff:* the safe generalist that never whiffs on the week's content but never spikes; you give up the high ceiling of committing to one shape. `dmgPct:11 (FREE).`

### Tier 3 — The Wound That Will Not Close (lean into the class-tool, or branch out)
This is the signature fork: do you become *the* anti-heal, or specialize your snowball elsewhere? Riving Wound is the only answer to a self-healing boss.
- **Riving Wound** — Your Reaping Blow (and Steel Cyclone at 5 Fury) brands the target with −50% incoming healing for 6s. *Tradeoff:* the P9 solver and the spec's identity capstone-early — the ONLY tool that beats a self-healing/empowered boss no purge can touch, and a top answer to P2 Magic-Resist Walls and P12 soft-enrage; gives up pure personal throughput for a fight-defining debuff your party can't get anywhere else. `NEW: talent grants an enemy-debuff rider (apply 'riven-wound' status, -50% incoming healing) onto a flagged ability — no debuff-on-enemy / healingReduction field exists in TalentOptionSchema today; this is an ability-style effect promoted into a talent.`
- **Rending Frenzy** — When you apply or extend a Bleed, it ticks for +35% and lasts +3s. *Tradeoff:* the bleed-DoT build — turns Steel Cyclone's pack-wide Bleed and Sweeping Fury's extensions into the primary damage source for sustained AoE attrition; gives up the anti-heal entirely (you cannot answer a self-healing boss). `NEW: talent modifier on the 'bleed' status — +35% tick magnitude and +1 base duration applied at status-application time (no per-status tuning channel in talents today).`
- **Hungering Onslaught** — +20% damage while your focus target is below 30% HP, and Reaping Blow's kill no longer resets only Savage Frenzy — it also refunds 2 Fury. *Tradeoff:* the execute-snowball build that branches AWAY from the anti-heal to chase the chain-kill loop; pure greed that does nothing against a healing boss or before targets drop low. `dmgPct:20, onlyIf targetHpBelowPct:30 (FREE) + NEW: on-kill Fury refund rider (+2 stacks) added to Reaping Blow's reset-on-kill handler.`

### Tier 4 — Surviving the Bleed-Out (active/conditional defense, never flat DR)
The Berserker pays HP to fight; this tier decides how it keeps from paying with its life — all keyed or active, none generic flat DR.
- **Battle Resolve** — While below 35% HP, you take −20% damage. *Tradeoff:* a conditional safety net that only arms when the self-damage build is at the cliff edge — exactly the Edge of Ruin partner; does nothing while healthy, so it buys zero ceiling for a topped-up Berserker. `NEW: conditional intakePct gated by selfHpBelowPct:35 (intakePct is currently applied unconditionally — gating it by self-HP is new).`
- **Crimson Tithe** — Your Fury spends (Savage Frenzy, Wrathful Frenzy) heal you for 8% max HP instead of damaging you. *Tradeoff:* the self-sustain bruiser's engine — flips the spec's blood-cost into blood-gain so it can spam its ramps risk-free; gives up the low-HP damage spikes the other builds weaponize (you're never low on purpose). `NEW: invert the hpPct resourceCost on Fury-spending abilities into a heal (no resource/heal-on-spend channel in talents).`
- **Reckless Disregard** — Your major Wrathful Frenzy lasts +3s and grants +8% crit, but its HP cost rises to 15%. *Tradeoff:* the all-in burst build's payoff — a longer, deadlier offensive window at a steeper blood price; pure offense with no defensive value at all (the anti-survival pick on the defense tier). `NEW: per-ability duration/cost override on Wrathful Frenzy (+1 turn, +8% crit, cost 10%→15%); critPct on the buff is new as a talent rider.`

### Tier 5 — The Last Stroke (your finisher identity)
The capstone tier — each option re-keys the kill phase for a different build.
- **Rampant Slaughter** — At 4+ Fury, a Reaping Blow kill resets its OWN cooldown. *Tradeoff:* the chain-killing soft-enrage answer (P12) — strings executes back-to-back across a dying pack, the AoE/execute build's dream; worthless on a single durable boss where nothing dies to reset it. `NEW: self-stack-gated (rampage>=4) reset-cooldown-on-kill rider on Reaping Blow targeting its own cooldown.`
- **Crimson Crescendo** — Each Fury stack now grants +6% damage (up from Battle Trance's +4%) and your stacks no longer decay between casts. *Tradeoff:* the sustained snowball ceiling — rewards a player who never drops the rhythm with the highest steady output in the spec; gives up the kill-phase explosiveness of its siblings for a fat, ever-climbing baseline. `NEW: scalar override on Battle Trance rampage-damage-bonus perStackPct 4→6 + a no-decay flag on the rampage resource.`
- **Headsman's Certainty** — Reaping Blow's execute threshold rises to 35% HP and its below-threshold multiplier climbs from x2 to x2.5. *Tradeoff:* the pure single-target executioner — deletes bosses far earlier and harder in the kill window, the Throat-Finder/Riving Wound boss build's finisher; offers nothing in AoE and nothing until the target is dying. `NEW: per-ability override on Reaping Blow's execute modifier (threshold 20→35, multiplier 2→2.5).`

**Builds:** *Bloodbound Executioner* (Throat-Finder + Riving Wound + Headsman's Certainty — the boss-deleting anti-heal that solves P9/P2/P12); *Bleeding Reaper* (Reaving Sweep + Rending Frenzy + Rampant Slaughter — Bleed-fueled AoE trash-clear and chain-kills); *Edge-Walker* (Edge of Ruin + Reckless Disregard + Battle Resolve — low-HP all-in burst gambler); *Undying Bruiser* (Lifeblood Renewal + Crimson Tithe + Crimson Crescendo — self-topping attrition body that needs no healer).

## Crusader — Tank (paladin)

*The transferable-aegis warden: a radiant front-liner who wins by spending a single, perfectly-timed negation on the right ally at the right beat. Every build is a different answer to "which incoming event do I refuse to let happen?"*

### Tier 1 — The Oath's Reach (who the aegis is FOR)
The Crusader's identity question first: does your protection follow the most fragile ally, the focused ally, or come back to harden yourself?

- **Light of the Sworn** — While Aegis of Valor / Sacred Bastion is active on an ally, that ally's incoming damage is also reduced -18% for the same window (a lingering "afterglow" once the hard negate is spent). *Tradeoff:* serves the **predictive-peel build** — your single-target aegis now also softens the aftermath, the best answer to a one-target-at-a-time spike chain (P5/P7); gives up the self-survival and party-wide options entirely. `NEW: when a single-ally peel/immunity status is active on a target, apply a typed intakePct -18% to that same target for the status duration (peel-linked intake debuff, not a flat aura).`
- **Goldwrought Vigil** — Holy Strike's +5% armour buff is doubled to +10%, lasts 6s instead of 3s, and stacks up to 2 — your basic attack now maintains a small rolling DR on whichever ally is taking the heat. *Tradeoff:* the **active-uptime build** — you babysit one ally with your auto-rhythm instead of a big cooldown; gives up the burst-negate amplification and the self-wall. `NEW: extend buff-nearest-ally mechanic to amountPct 10, durationTurns 2, maxStacks 2 (armour buff that rolls off the basic).`
- **The Bulwark Holds** — Stalwart Defender's passive armour also grants you -10% damage taken while you hold a hard taunt; drop threat and it lapses. *Tradeoff:* the **selfish-wall build** — turns you into a conventional unkillable anchor *as long as you're actively tanking*, but spends nothing on the party and dies the instant you're not the boss's target. `NEW: conditional intakePct -10% gated on selfHasStatus 'hard-taunt-active' (self-only, threat-keyed — not a flat passive).`

### Tier 2 — The Sheltering Light (the sustained-window save vs the burst answer)
The cardinal anti-generic-EHP fork: a timed party-wide general DR you must *predict and press*, versus a sharper single-event tool — neither is an always-on EHP stat-stick.

- **Bulwark Litany** — Rally Cry additionally drops a party-wide -12% damage-taken canopy (against *any* damage) for its window — a cooldown-gated, timed general save you press just before a heavy stretch. *Tradeoff:* the **heavy-window / sustained-pressure build** — the broad party-DR cooldown that smooths a punishing stretch when you read it coming; it is window-locked, so a mistimed press protects nothing and it adds zero throughput. `NEW: typed intakePct -12% to ALL incoming damage for the party, active only for the Rally Cry window (cooldown-gated party-DR cooldown, not an always-on aura).`
- **Stillvalor Aegis** — Sacred Bastion gains a second charge but each charge is single-ally and the cooldown lengthens; you can negate two separate predicted events per its window instead of one big one. *Tradeoff:* the **multi-spike build** — two surgical immunity-peels for a fight with two scary beats (P5 On-the-Hour Spike + a second mechanic); gives up the broad party-DR window and any throughput. `NEW: Sacred Bastion → 2 charges, single-ally immunity-peel each, longer cooldown (charge-based negate cooldown).`
- **Daybreak Cadence** — Rally Cry's +15% power becomes +20% and also grants the party +6% crit for the window. *Tradeoff:* the **throughput / timer-race build** — you lean *away* from the aegis fantasy to make the party kill faster, the pick when the dungeon is timer-bound rather than survival-bound; gives up both the party-DR window and the spike tools. `dmgPct +5 on the existing party power buff plus critPct +6 to the party for the buff window (party offensive cooldown).`

### Tier 3 — The Aegis's Discipline (how Radiant Aegis behaves)
Radiant Aegis is gated to one charge per predictable window — this tier decides what that charge *means*.

- **Dawnforged Bulwark** — Radiant Aegis's absorb is +50% larger and, while it holds, that ally cannot be critically struck. *Tradeoff:* the **anti-spike-shield build** — the surest pre-block for a known big hit (the proactive side of the P5 prevention-vs-reaction read); a fat single shield that does nothing once it pops, useless against a stream of small ticks. `NEW: shield base/scale +50% and a "no-crit-taken while shielded" flag on the shield status.`
- **Spreading Benediction** — Radiant Aegis splits across the **two lowest-HP allies** at 60% size each instead of one ally at full size. *Tradeoff:* the **raid-damage / AoE-pressure build** — covers a two-target soak or a spread mechanic where the whole back line is bleeding; loses the ability to fully save one focused ally. `NEW: shield targets the 2 lowest-HP allies at 0.6x magnitude each (split-shield distribution).`
- **Radiant Reprisal** — When Radiant Aegis expires or is consumed, it detonates for Holy damage to all enemies scaling off the absorbed amount. *Tradeoff:* the **damage-tank / timer build** — turns a defensive cooldown into a threat-and-damage swing, the pick when you must contribute to the kill clock; the shield itself is unchanged (no extra survival), so it's the weakest *defensive* of the three. `NEW: on shield consume/expire, deal Holy AoE = shieldAbsorbed × factor to all enemies (shield-detonation).`

### Tier 4 — The Redirect's Edge (what Guardian's Oath becomes)
Guardian's Oath (100% redirect to self at -20%) is the Crusader's other transfer tool. This tier is the personal-defensive vs. offence-from-defence vs. control fork.

- **Steadfast Forbearance** — While Guardian's Oath is active, the redirected damage you take is reduced an extra -25% (on top of the base -20%) and you cannot be reduced below 1 HP for the duration. *Tradeoff:* the **hard-peel / save-the-ally build** — you can eat a lethal hit *for* an ally and live, the ultimate "I take this so you don't" tool; spends your whole defensive budget on one redirect window and adds no damage. `NEW: while redirect-damage status is active on self, additional intakePct -25% on redirected damage + a death-immunity (min 1 HP) flag for the duration.`
- **Oathbound Reprisal** — While Guardian's Oath is active you deal +30% damage (you're shrugging off blows and swinging back). *Tradeoff:* the **damage-tank build** — converts your peel cooldown into a personal burst window, great on a timer; you take the redirected hits at only the base -20% with no safety net, so a misjudged redirect can kill you. `NEW: dmgPct +30% gated on selfHasStatus 'redirect-active' (conditional self-damage buff while the redirect runs).`
- **Reckoning of the Warden** — When Guardian's Oath ends, the enemy that dealt the most redirected damage is dazed for 3s (you turn the soaked punishment back as a stagger). *Tradeoff:* the **control / mechanic-delay build** — a built-in mini-interrupt/tempo tool to buy the party a beat (P1/P8 flavour); the redirect stays plain -20% with no extra mitigation or damage, so it's the softest pick if you just need to live. `NEW: on redirect-damage expiry, applyStatus 'daze' 1 turn to the enemy that contributed the most redirected damage (on-expiry crowd-control).`

### Tier 5 — Capstone: The Last Bastion (what your MAJOR is FOR)
The deep commitment node — Sacred Bastion (the single-ally immunity-peel) is re-keyed into one of three identities. This is the build's punchline.

- **Aegis of the Dawnsworn** — Sacred Bastion becomes a TRUE immunity: the chosen ally fully negates the next incoming event of any size for the window (a clean, total Eruption-negate / On-the-Hour-Spike refusal), and its cooldown is shortened. *Tradeoff:* the **predictive-negate capstone** — the purest expression of the Crusader fantasy and the definitive P7/P5 answer; it is single-ally and binary, so a missed prediction wastes your whole major and it gives nothing to a sustained-pressure week. `NEW: Sacred Bastion → single-ally full event-immunity (negate next incoming event entirely) with reduced cooldown (one-charge predictive immunity major).`
- **Vault of the Dawn** — Sacred Bastion instead drops a -40% damage-taken dome (against *any* damage) over the **whole party** for its window — the disaster button you press to predict and eat a heavy stretch. *Tradeoff:* the **timed party-save capstone** — lets a Crusader hard-carry a punishing-pressure week by reading the heavy window and pressing the dome, the strongest broad save in the kit; it is a cooldown-gated window, so a wrong-timed press buys nothing and it does no damage. `NEW: Sacred Bastion → party-wide typed intakePct -40% to ALL incoming damage, active only for the cooldown window (cooldown-gated general party dome major, not an always-on aura).`
- **Reliquary of Radiance** — Sacred Bastion shields the ally (0.5×maxHp) AND, while that shield holds, the whole party deals +20% damage. *Tradeoff:* the **damage-tank / timer capstone** — one cooldown that both peels *and* swings the kill clock, the pick for a timer-bound clear; the peel is "just" a shield (no immunity, no broad DR window), so it's the weakest *defensive* major and rewards aggression over prediction. `NEW: Sacred Bastion shield (0.5×maxHp single ally) + while that shield is active grant party dmgPct +20% (shield-linked party damage major).`

**Builds:** (1) **Predictive Peeler** — Light of the Sworn → Stillvalor Aegis → Dawnforged Bulwark → Steadfast Forbearance → Aegis of the Dawnsworn: the definitive single-event-negate Crusader for spike/Eruption weeks (P5/P7). (2) **Light-Warden / Pressure Carry** — Goldwrought Vigil → Bulwark Litany → Spreading Benediction → Steadfast Forbearance → Vault of the Dawn: the timed party-DR answer for sustained-pressure weeks (P6). (3) **Radiant Anchor / Damage-Tank** — The Bulwark Holds → Daybreak Cadence → Radiant Reprisal → Oathbound Reprisal → Reliquary of Radiance: a selfish, timer-racing tank that contributes to the kill clock. (4) **Warden of Tempo / Control** — Goldwrought Vigil → Stillvalor Aegis → Spreading Benediction → Reckoning of the Warden → Aegis of the Dawnsworn: a peel-and-stagger build that buys the party beats against mechanics (P1/P8 flavour).

## Cleric — Healer (paladin)

*Identity: the reactive-burst + Magic/Curse-dispel healer who heals by dealing damage (atonement). The instant spike-top — heals the spike AFTER it lands, banks overheal as a shield, and answers the Armour-Wall with off-school radiant damage. Weak at sustained decay by design; throughput is capped so a second healer buys safety, not ceiling.*

### Tier 1 — The Atonement Shape (how your damage heals)
- **Choir of Dawn** — Radiance and Searing Ray heal the lowest-HP ally for +20% of the damage dealt (atonement healPctOfDamage 50→70%), but you cannot atonement-heal the same ally twice in a row — it always rotates to the *next* lowest. *Tradeoff:* serves the **damage-while-healing / off-school** build — biggest passive throughput floor, spread across the party — but loses the ability to pour all your radiant healing into the one person who is actually dying. `NEW: atonement healPctOfDamage override + "rotate target, exclude last-healed ally" targeting flag on atonement-heal/atonement-on-dot.`
- **Kindled Offering** — your Burn DoT (Searing Ray) ticks +30% harder and its atonement healing is doubled, but Radiance's direct atonement is removed (basic attacks no longer heal). *Tradeoff:* the **DoT-leaning sustain** answer — converts you into a fire-and-forget atonement engine that wants Searing Ray on cooldown, the closest the Cleric gets to decay-coverage — but gives up the instant, every-cast trickle of Radiance healing. `NEW: per-status DoT magnitude multiplier + atonement-on-dot healPctOfDotDamage override + flag to disable atonement-heal on a named ability (radiance).`
- **Piercing Ray** — Radiance deals +15% Magic damage but no longer heals at all; instead every Radiance reduces Mending Light's cooldown by 3s. *Tradeoff:* the **reactive-spike enabler** — you stop caring about passive trickle and weaponise your filler into faster access to your instant spike heal, leaning hard into the burst identity — but you forfeit all atonement value while topped, so a calm pull heals nothing. `dmgPct: 15` (on radiance) `+ NEW: "on-cast of ability X reduce cooldown of ability Y by N turns" hook + disable atonement-heal flag.`

### Tier 2 — The Spike (your signature reactive top)
- **Sanctuary Bloom** — Mending Light cast on an ally below 30% HP leaves a general absorb equal to 60% of its overheal that soaks any incoming damage. *Tradeoff:* the **pure reactive-triage** pick — the cleanest answer to On-the-Hour Spike (P5): you let the hit land, then top + bank a shield against the *next* big swing — but it only blooms when you catch someone already low, so it does nothing while the party is healthy and demands you hold Mending Light for the spike. `NEW: conditional overheal→shield magnitude (general absorb, no consumption-type restriction).`
- **Steadfast Grace** — Mending Light cast on a full-HP ally pre-loads a small proactive general absorb (overheal→shield works while topped, not just when healing damage). *Tradeoff:* the **prevention / Crusader-mirror** pick — lets the Cleric pre-shield a predicted hit instead of reacting, smoothing predictable damage — but the shield is smaller than Sanctuary Bloom's reactive bloom and wastes a cast when nobody is hurt. `NEW: allow overheal-to-shield to generate a general shield on full-HP targets (proactive shield, magnitude scaled).`
- **Greater Mending** — Dawnward's below-30% bonus rises from +50% to +90% and refunds 3s of its cooldown if it lands on a target below 30%. *Tradeoff:* the **emergency single-target ceiling** — your hardest non-major top gets much bigger and can chain in a crisis — but it is a slow cooldown heal, not an instant, so it loses the race against a true one-shot spike that Mending Light would catch. `NEW: per-ability targetHpBelowPct heal multiplier override + "refund N cooldown turns if target below pct" hook.`

### Tier 3 — The Cleanse (your typed dispel niche, P4)
- **Radiant Absolution** — Cleansing Light strips +1 Magic/Curse debuff (3 total) and its cleansed-ally +damage buff rises to +18% for 9s. *Tradeoff:* the **lean-into-the-tool** pick — the definitive Spreading-Curse (P4) answer that turns a heavy dispel into a party-wide offensive swing — but it is still locked to Magic/Curse, so a Nature/Poison fight leaves it dead, and it's a cooldown you must time to the curse wave. `NEW: cleanse count override + cleansed-ally buff amountPct/duration override on a named ability.`
- **Purifying Hymn** — Cleansing Light additionally cleanses 1 Nature/Poison debuff from each ally (covers all four dispel types), but the post-cleanse damage buff is removed. *Tradeoff:* the **generalist insurance** pick — the only way the Cleric touches Nature/Poison, broadening her dispel to cover any debuff wave — but she gives up the signature offensive payoff, becoming a pure purifier instead of a purifier-who-buffs. `NEW: add extra dispelTypes coverage (Nature/Poison) to a named cleanse + remove its conditional buff effect.`
- **Solar Verdict** — Cleansing Light no longer cleanses; instead it deals Magic damage to all enemies scaled on power, and that damage feeds atonement (heals the lowest ally for 50%). *Tradeoff:* the **damage-pivot / armour-wall** pick — converts your utility cooldown into an AoE off-school nuke + group heal, brutal on trash and great vs P3 armour walls — but you throw away your dispel entirely, so a curse fight will overwhelm you. `NEW: replace cleanse with an AoE damage effect (damageType Magic, scaleStat power, pattern all-enemies) that triggers atonement-heal.`

### Tier 4 — Off-School Damage (the radiant-atonement / P3 axis)
- **Crowned in Light** — while any ally is below 30% HP, your Magic damage heals the whole party for 50% of the damage dealt (atonement goes from single lowest-ally to party-wide during the emergency). *Tradeoff:* the **deep atonement capstone-feel** pick — in a crisis the Cleric becomes an off-school damage-as-group-healing answer to the Armour Wall (P3), enormous AoE healing while you smite the pack — but only when someone is *already* low, so it does nothing for prevention and pure throughput is capped. `NEW: conditional atonement target-mode swap (lowest-ally → party) gated by "any ally below pct"; needs a new onlyIf scope (ally-state, not focus-target).`
- **Zealous Devotion** — +18% Magic damage while 3 or more enemies are up; with three foes your atonement healing is enough to off-tank decay through pure radiant damage. *Tradeoff:* the **AoE damage-while-healing** pick — leans on the wide-pull math so radiant spam both clears trash and trickle-heals the party — but the bonus evaporates on a single boss, leaving you a weak solo-target healer. `dmgPct: 18, onlyIf { type: "enemiesAtLeast", value: 3 }` (FREE).
- **Final Light** — +20% Magic damage to your focus target while it is below 30% HP, and Light's Clarity's atonement-amp also applies to *your* damage there. *Tradeoff:* the **single-target execute** pick — turns the kill window into a burst of both damage and the largest atonement heals of the fight, ideal for a boss soft-enrage — but offers nothing on full-HP targets or wide pulls. `dmgPct: 20, onlyIf { type: "targetHpBelowPct", value: 30 }` (FREE) `+ NEW: extend atonement-amp to self-damage under the same condition.`

### Tier 5 — The Major: Light's Salvation Recut (your defining cooldown)
- **Light's Salvation** — a strong instant single-target reactive burst heal (no party-shield half); on a target below 30% HP it heals for +50% and leaves a general absorb that soaks any incoming damage. *Tradeoff:* the **purest spike-top** pick — the recut signature: the single biggest instant save in the game for catching the On-the-Hour one-shot (P5) — but it touches only one ally, so a party-wide pulse can still drop two people at once. `NEW: single-target reactive heal with conditional below-30% multiplier + conditional general absorb (recut of lights-salvation: remove party-pattern shield, swap to side:ally pattern:single).`
- **Solar Reckoning** — Light's Salvation becomes an instant party-wide Magic nuke that feeds full atonement (heals everyone for 50% of damage dealt) and refreshes Burn on every enemy. *Tradeoff:* the **AoE damage-as-healing burst** pick — the Solar/Crowned build's payoff cooldown: a single button that nukes the pack, blankets it in DoT, and bursts the whole party up at once — but it heals *nothing* if there are no enemies in range to strike, so it whiffs on a positioning check. `NEW: major that deals AoE Magic damage (scaleStat power), triggers party-wide atonement-heal, and reapplies a named DoT to all enemies.`
- **Dawnpeal** — Light's Salvation instantly strips ALL Magic/Curse debuffs from every ally, grants the party +15% damage for 9s, and heals each cleansed ally for a moderate burst. *Tradeoff:* the **utility / hard-reset** pick — the dispel-build's panic button that wipes an entire curse wave (P4) and swings the party offensive in one cast — but its raw healing is the smallest of the three majors, so it's wasted if there's no debuff to clear. `NEW: major combining full-coverage cleanse (count = all, dispelTypes Magic/Curse) + per-cleansed-ally heal burst + party power buff for N turns.`

**Builds:** (1) **Reactive Spike-Top** — Piercing Ray → Sanctuary Bloom → Radiant Absolution → Final Light → Light's Salvation: the instant single-target save build. (2) **Atonement Off-Healer (P3 armour-wall)** — Choir of Dawn → Greater Mending → Solar Verdict → Crowned in Light → Solar Reckoning: heal the party purely by smiting the pack. (3) **Curse-Janitor / Utility** — Kindled Offering → Steadfast Grace → Purifying Hymn → Zealous Devotion → Dawnpeal: the dispel-and-prevent generalist for debuff-heavy weeks. (4) **DoT-Sustain hybrid** — Kindled Offering → Greater Mending → Radiant Absolution → Zealous Devotion → Solar Reckoning: the closest the Cleric comes to covering sustained decay.

## Assassin — DPS (rogue)

*The melee priority-delete and the only melee reach — a stealth-and-poison rogue who slips through shadow, chains crits, and executes one target into the dark.*

### Tier 1 — The Opener (what your stealth strike is FOR)
- **Opening Cut** — Your stealth-empowered opener (Duskstep / Shade Step follow-up) deals +25% to a target above 80% HP, but nothing extra once it's wounded. *Tradeoff:* the pure burst-window build — front-loads the kill on a fresh priority add (P8) and rewards reading which mob to delete first; gives up the execute carry of Throatcut and the reach of Shadowleap. `NEW: dmgPct gated by targetHpAbovePct (new onlyIf type — current enum only has targetHpBelowPct).`
- **Shadowleap** — Your opener can land on the BACK band and deals +30% there (folds the dive into the stealth-open). *Tradeoff:* the dive/reach build — the melee-only answer to the Sheltered Caster (P11); pays for it with no above/below-HP amplifier, so it under-burns when the back caster is already dead. `NEW: the reach-on-opener relies on the dive primitive (ability special); the +30%-vs-back is dmgPct gated by a targetBand onlyIf (new condition type).`
- **Throatcut** — Your opener deals +30% to a target below 35% HP instead. *Tradeoff:* the execute build — converts the cheap stealth strike into a second finisher for the soft-enrage race (P12); loses the fresh-target alpha and the back-line reach. `dmgPct + onlyIf{targetHpBelowPct:35}.`

### Tier 2 — Single-target vs. the wave (priority-delete vs. clearing the room)
- **Singular Malice** — +22% damage while 2 or fewer enemies remain. *Tradeoff:* the boss/priority-kill build — leans all the way into single-target erase for the Dangerous Add (P8) and bosses; dead weight on a packed trash pull. `dmgPct + onlyIf{enemiesAtMost:2}.`
- **Whispering Edge** — Your strikes spill 35% of their damage onto one adjacent enemy while 3+ are up. *Tradeoff:* the only AoE answer in the tree — keeps the Assassin from drowning on trash weeks (P2/wide pulls); a flat downgrade in any 1-2 target window where the spec wants to focus. `NEW: cleave-splash on basic/finisher hits (splashPct + splashCount on the spec's attacks), gated to enemiesAtLeast:3.`
- **Twin Daggers** — Your off-hand follows every strike for a small extra hit, +9% damage at all times, no condition. *Tradeoff:* the safe floor — never wrong, never exciting; the steady, poison-and-target-count-agnostic pick for a player who just wants reliable uptime. `dmgPct.`

### Tier 3 — The Poison economy (build the stacks vs. spend them harder)
- **Weeping Venom** — Your poison applications land +1 extra stack and last longer; poison ticks +20% harder. *Tradeoff:* the sustained-DoT build — ramps damage across a long single-target fight and feeds a fatter Carve; weak in the first few seconds before stacks exist, so it loses burst openers. `NEW: poison apply grants +1 stack & +1 duration, and a poison-DoT magnitude multiplier (status-tick scaling — no talent field today).`
- **Venom Glut** — Each poison stack on your target adds +4% to your finisher (Carve) damage, on top of its base per-stack bonus. *Tradeoff:* the burst-payoff build — turns the poison ramp into one explosive spend instead of steady ticks; does nothing if you don't reach the finisher window, punishing sloppy play. `NEW: finisher gains +X% per stack of poison on target (per-stack damage scaling keyed to a status count — abilities have perStackOf, talents do not).`
- **Silent Patience** — +6% damage and +5% crit chance, always. *Tradeoff:* the generalist poison-agnostic pick — steady value that ignores the stack economy entirely; gives up both the long-fight ramp and the finisher spike. `dmgPct + critPct.`

### Tier 4 — The crit-chain (snowball execution vs. raw conversion vs. staying alive)
- **Bladed Cascade** — When you crit, your next strike is a guaranteed crit (chaining off Killer's Focus and Shade Step's resets). *Tradeoff:* the snowball build — the highest skill-ceiling, highest-ceiling damage line that rewards keeping the crit chain alive and Duskstep resetting; offers zero defense and falls off if the chain breaks. `NEW: on-crit "guarantee next crit" flag (status-driven crit guarantee — no talent crit-state field exists).`
- **Lethal Precision** — +12% crit chance and +20% crit damage, flat. *Tradeoff:* the reliable-crit build — converts the spec's crit synergies into raw, unconditional throughput without needing a chain to hold; gives up the explosive snowball ceiling of Bladed Cascade. `NEW: critPct exists; the +20% crit DAMAGE has no talent field today (critMult is a buff stat, not a talent effect).`
- **Predator's Vigor** — Your crits heal you for 4% of your max HP. *Tradeoff:* the self-sustain bruiser line — buys the lone-melee Assassin enough lifesteal to survive a dive onto the back band without a healer babysitting it; pure-offense builds rightly skip it as a damage loss. `NEW: heal-on-crit (lifesteal keyed to crit events — no talent healing/lifesteal field).`

### Tier 5 — The capstone (re-key the spec: chain-killer vs. assassin's mark vs. ghost)
- **Endless Slaughter** — When you kill an enemy, your finisher's cooldown resets and your next strike is empowered. *Tradeoff:* the soft-enrage chain-kill capstone (P12) — converts the spec into a wave-erasing executioner that snowballs through low-HP mobs; useless on a single fat boss where there's nothing to kill mid-pull. `NEW: reset-finisher-cooldown-on-kill + empower-next (on-kill hook — no talent kill-trigger field).`
- **Sever the Thread** — Your opener and finisher gain full back-band reach AND deal +40% to the sheltered back caster; while a back-band enemy lives, your single-target damage to it is +15%. *Tradeoff:* the dedicated dive capstone (P11) — makes the Assassin the definitive melee answer to the Sheltered Caster, erasing the protected mob the rest of the party can't touch; pours everything into reach and is flat on a fight with no back line. `NEW: grant dive-reach to multiple abilities + a band-keyed damage amp (targetBand onlyIf + reach primitive — neither is a talent field).`
- **Fade to Black** — After scoring a kill, you re-enter stealth automatically (one free empowered opener) and take -50% damage for 3s. *Tradeoff:* the survival/execution capstone — lets a healer-light comp run the Assassin as a self-resetting ghost that fades between kills; the conditional, kill-gated defensive (not flat DR) keeps it timer-regime-safe, but it sacrifices the raw chain throughput of Endless Slaughter. `NEW: on-kill re-stealth + a 1-turn keyed intake reduction (on-kill status grant — no talent status/defensive-window field).`

**Builds:** (1) **Dive Assassin** — Shadowleap → Singular Malice → Venom Glut → Bladed Cascade → Sever the Thread: the melee P11 specialist who erases the back caster. (2) **Execute Chain-Killer** — Throatcut → Whispering Edge → Weeping Venom → Bladed Cascade → Endless Slaughter: a P12 soft-enrage wave-deleter. (3) **Alpha Burster** — Opening Cut → Singular Malice → Venom Glut → Lethal Precision → Sever the Thread: the priority-delete P8 specialist. (4) **Lone-Knife Bruiser** — Throatcut → Twin Daggers → Silent Patience → Predator's Vigor → Fade to Black: the self-sustaining, healer-light dive survivor.

## Archer — DPS (rogue), Back line

*Identity: a selfish ranged precision sharpshooter who Marks a target, reaches over the front rank to pick the sheltered back-line caster, and steadies their aim to focus-fire it dead. Every tier asks which problem the bow answers: the reach pick, the resist-wall ceiling, the execute race, or the trash hail.*

### Tier 1 — The Mark's Purpose (what your Mark is FOR)
This fork re-keys the spec's signature debuff: is the Mark a selfish focus-fire amp, a wide opener, or a steadier line?
- **Killing Mark** — While your focus target is below 50% HP, your shots hit +14% harder (the Mark becomes a personal focus-fire kill stamp). *Tradeoff:* serves the single-target / executioner build; pure selfish ceiling, gives up any AoE or survival the siblings offer. `dmgPct: 14, onlyIf: { type: "targetHpBelowPct", value: 50 }`
- **Hunting Marks** — +16% damage while 3 or more enemies are up (you spray Marks across the pack instead of one). *Tradeoff:* the AoE / trash-clear opener; dead weight on a lone boss, sacrifices the execute payoff. `dmgPct: 16, onlyIf: { type: "enemiesAtLeast", value: 3 }`
- **Steady Mark** — +8% damage at all times (a level, always-on draw). *Tradeoff:* the safe balanced floor — wins in no single situation but never goes dark; gives up the higher conditional ceilings on both sides. `dmgPct: 8`

### Tier 2 — The Reach (lean INTO the signature class-tool, or branch away)
The Archer's Phase-P niche is NATIVE RANGED REACH — picking off the sheltered back-line caster. This tier decides whether you double down on it or trade it for raw single-target ceiling.
- **Hawkeye's Volley** — Hawkeye's Reach can pick off TWO sheltered targets in the back band and its back-band bonus rises to +60%. *Tradeoff:* the dedicated reach / P11 back-line-assassination build; the strongest answer to the protected caster, but wasted when there is no back rank to reach. `NEW: per-ability override — extend hawkeyes-reach targeting to pattern "adjacent" count 2 within band "back", and raise its existing targetBand:"back" multiplyDamage from 1.3 to 1.6 (no current talent field touches a specific ability's targeting/band modifier).`
- **Heavy Draw** — Hawkeye's Reach becomes a single heavy aimed shot: drop the reach, gain +50% damage versus targets below 35% HP (a second execute hammer). *Tradeoff:* the single-target ceiling build that abandons the reach niche entirely — best raw boss DPS, worst answer to a back-line problem. `NEW: per-ability override — strip hawkeyes-reach's targetBand back modifier and add a modifier when targetHpBelowPct 35 → multiplyDamage 1.5 (talents cannot rewrite an ability's modifier set).`
- **Patient Line** — +12% damage while 2 or fewer enemies remain (you favour the patient line over the crowd, reach intact but unimproved). *Tradeoff:* the generalist boss-lean middle — keeps your base reach shot and rewards thinning the pack, but improves neither the reach nor the execute as sharply as its siblings. `dmgPct: 12, onlyIf: { type: "enemiesAtMost", value: 2 }`

### Tier 3 — The Aim Window (burst spike vs the kill race vs the wounding shot)
Shapes how your damage is delivered — sharpened for the soft-enrage execute, smoothed into a precise spike, or barbed to keep a self-healing target's wounds from closing.
- **Serrated Shot** — Your shots on the focus target apply a barbed wound: the target takes 40% less healing while Marked (a serrated arrowhead that stops the wound closing). *Tradeoff:* makes the Archer a RANGED answer to the self-healing boss (P9) alongside the Berserker — your only anti-heal pick, but it spends a tier slot that buys no raw output of its own. `NEW: while the focus target is Marked, apply a healingReceivedPct: -40 debuff to it (no current talent field reduces a target's incoming healing — requires a heal-intake hook in combat.ts).`
- **Falling Aim** — +18% damage while your focus target is below 30% HP (you save your steadiest shots for the kill). *Tradeoff:* the burst / soft-enrage executioner spike — huge on the kill, invisible until the target is bloodied. `dmgPct: 18, onlyIf: { type: "targetHpBelowPct", value: 30 }`
- **Steady Sight** — A flat +6% crit chance at all times that sharpens your aim window (passive precision that always contributes). *Tradeoff:* the sustained-tempo / crit floor — reliable every shot, but no conditional spike when it matters most. `critPct: 6`

### Tier 4 — Keeping the Line (active/conditional survival, never flat passive DR)
A back-line DPS still eats spillover. This fork is your one defensive choice — all three are keyed or conditional, none is a flat damage sponge.
- **Hawkstride** — While 3 or more enemies are up, you take 14% less damage (you read the pull and stay out of the hail). *Tradeoff:* the trash-survival / AoE-build defensive — keeps you alive through pack pulls, but does nothing on a lone boss where you most want output. `intakePct: -14` *(applied with an enemiesAtLeast:3 gate)* `NEW: extend onlyIf to gate intakePct, not only dmgPct (resolveTalents currently applies intakePct unconditionally; conditional intake requires a combat.ts change).`
- **Set Feet** — While your focus target is below 35% HP, you take 12% less damage (you can plant your feet for the kill without dying to the enrage). *Tradeoff:* the executioner-build defensive — survives the soft-enrage race, but offers nothing during the long opening of a fight. `intakePct: -12` *(applied with a targetHpBelowPct:35 gate)* `NEW: same conditional-intake hook as above (gate intakePct by onlyIf).`
- **Roll Aside** — The first time you would drop below 35% HP, you dodge clear and take 60% less damage for 6s (a once-per-fight evasive that buys the healer a window). *Tradeoff:* the always-available survival pick — a single keyed escape rather than a standing buffer, so it saves you once but cannot soak repeated spillover. `NEW: one-shot trigger — on first crossing below 35% HP, apply intakePct: -60 for durationTurns 2 (a hp-threshold-triggered self-buff; no talent field arms a one-time conditional defensive).`

### Tier 5 — The Last Shot (capstone — the build's punchline)
The commitment node that re-keys your endgame: pure execute, the trash-clearing storm, or a focus-fire amp that finally reaches back to the party.
- **The Last Shot** — While your focus target is below 35% HP, your damage spikes +28% (every shot becomes a finisher in the kill window). *Tradeoff:* the single-target / soft-enrage capstone — the highest boss-and-execute ceiling in the spec, but flatly dead until the target is bleeding. `dmgPct: 28, onlyIf: { type: "targetHpBelowPct", value: 35 }`
- **Culling Storm** — While 3 or more enemies are up, +24% damage AND your Arrow Storm / Falling Volley strike a second time at 50% (the pack-clearing crescendo). *Tradeoff:* the AoE trash-clear capstone — owns multi-pull weeks, but every point is wasted on a single target. `dmgPct: 24, onlyIf: { type: "enemiesAtLeast", value: 3 }` plus `NEW: per-ability rider — Arrow Storm and Falling Volley deal a second 50% all-enemy hit (a "repeat-strike at pct" mechanic; no talent field repeats an ability's hit).`
- **Hunter's Largesse** — Your Mark also amplifies the WHOLE party's damage on that target by +12% (a second damage-taken increase on the Marked enemy), at the cost of −6% to your own output. *Tradeoff:* buys back the support side the recut removed — turns the selfish Archer into a focus-fire enabler for the group, surrendering personal ceiling for party throughput; the only co-op capstone, useless solo. `NEW: amplify the Mark status's damageTaken magnitude by +12 (party-facing, distinct from the self-only dmgPct knob) AND apply a self −6% dmgPct (dmgPct: -6 is expressible; the Mark-magnitude buff is NOT — no talent field edits a status's statMods).`

**Builds:** (1) **Back-line Executioner** — Killing Mark → Heavy Draw → Falling Aim → Set Feet → The Last Shot: single-target P2 resist-wall / P8 kill / P12 soft-enrage; (2) **Reach Sharpshooter** — Killing Mark/Steady → Hawkeye's Volley → Steady Sight → Set Feet → The Last Shot: the P11 back-line-caster answer; (3) **Wound-Stopper (anti-heal)** — Killing Mark → Patient Line → Serrated Shot → Set Feet → The Last Shot: the ranged P9 self-healing-boss answer; (4) **Arrow Hailer (AoE)** — Hunting Marks → Patient Line → Steady Sight → Hawkstride → Culling Storm: trash-clear; (5) **Party Focus-Fire Marker** — Steady Mark → Patient Line → Steady Sight → Roll Aside → Hunter's Largesse: a teamwide kill-target enabler.

## Pyromancer — DPS (mage)

*Identity:* the burn-stack AoE detonator — paint the pack in Burn, then explode it. Every tier asks the same question a different way: do you stack WIDE (contagion across the pack), tall (fewer targets, hotter ticks), or do you trade the slow-burn for a controlled detonation that delays a cast and locks an add. The signature fork is contagion-AoE (P3 armour wall) vs. detonation-control (P1 delay / P8 lock).

### Tier 1 — The Ember's Nature (how Burn behaves on the pack)
- **Creeping Embers** — Every offensive cast also spreads 1 Burn stack to an enemy adjacent to your target. *Tradeoff:* the contagion / P3-armour-wall build — Burn blankets the whole pack with no extra casts, so Emberfall/Emberstorm detonations hit everyone. Gives up the focused single-target ramp and the control payoff of the other two. `NEW: on every offensive cast, apply 1 stack of the 'burn' status to one enemy adjacent to the primary target (auto-spread rider).`
- **Smothering Ash** — Burn cannot spread, but each of your Burn stacks ticks +25% harder and lasts +3s. *Tradeoff:* the tall single-target / boss build (P3 against a lone armoured target, P12 ramp) — fewer targets but a far hotter bar. Gives up pack coverage; weak when 4+ are up. `NEW: increase Burn DoT tick magnitude by +25% and Burn duration by +1 turn for Burn applied by this caster; suppress all Burn spread mechanics.`
- **Kindled Patience** — Burn you apply starts at 2 stacks instead of 1 the first time it lands on a fresh target. *Tradeoff:* the balanced ramp pick — faster initial stacking on a clean pull without committing to spread or tall ticks, but no ceiling on either axis; the safe middle. `NEW: when applying Burn to a target with no existing Burn, apply +1 additional initial stack (first-application bonus only).`

### Tier 2 — The Detonation (what an explosion is for)
- **Pyre-Vents** — Detonating 4+ Burn stacks grants +25% power for 6s (your inferno payoff) AND, if the detonated target had Burn, stuns it for 3s. *Tradeoff:* the detonation-control build — turns Emberfall/Emberstorm into a P1 cast-*delay* and P8 add-*lock* while still spiking your damage. Gives up raw splash width; you must set up the stack before it pays. `NEW: on self-detonation of >=4 Burn stacks, apply 'stun' (1 turn) to detonated targets that had Burn, in addition to the existing +25% power buff.`
- **Cinderburst** — Your detonations splash to +1 additional adjacent target and deal +20% detonation damage. *Tradeoff:* the AoE-payoff build — the widest possible explosion across a stacked pack (P3 trash, P12 burst), no control attached. Gives up the stun (no P1/P8) and the execute focus. `NEW: increase detonation splashCount by +1 and detonation damage by +20% (Emberfall / Emberstorm / Skyfall detonate channel).`
- **Stoked Furnace** — Detonations refund 3s of Emberfall's cooldown per 4 stacks consumed. *Tradeoff:* the tempo / sustained-cycle build — detonate more often for steady throughput rather than one big bloom; serves a long boss timer. Gives up the burst spike and the control of its siblings. `NEW: on self-detonation, reduce Emberfall's remaining cooldown by floor(stacksConsumed / 4) turns.`

### Tier 3 — Where the Fire Reaches (range profile vs. the pack)
- **Far-Cast Ember** — +18% damage while 3 or more enemies are up; your Burn application range extends to the back band, letting you light the sheltered caster. *Tradeoff:* the reach / P11 + AoE build — answers the back-line caster AND the trash wall at once. Gives up single-target ceiling; the bonus evaporates on a lone boss. `dmgPct: 18, onlyIf { enemiesAtLeast: 3 }` plus `NEW: this caster's Burn-applying abilities may target the back band (reach flag for P11).`
- **Focused Pyre** — +20% damage when 2 or fewer enemies remain. *Tradeoff:* the single-target / boss build (P3 lone armour wall, P12) — pure ceiling on the kill target. Gives up all pack/reach value; dead weight on heavy trash. `dmgPct: 20, onlyIf { enemiesAtMost: 2 }`
- **Banked Heat** — +6% damage per consecutive cast on the same target this rotation (resets if you swap targets), capped at +18%. *Tradeoff:* the patience floor — a modest tempo reward for parking on one target rather than chasing the pull, but the ramp resets the moment you pivot, so it never tops the dedicated forks. Gives up the high end of both reach and burst. `NEW: grant +6% damage per consecutive offensive cast on the same primary target this encounter, max +18%; reset the stack to 0 on any target swap (focus-stacking floor).`

### Tier 4 — Ward of the Flame (active/conditional defense, not a flat wall)
- **Ashen Veil** — When you detonate 4+ Burn stacks, you take −30% damage for 6s. *Tradeoff:* the active-mitigation defensive — survival is *earned* by playing the detonation rotation, so it never beats reading the fight; pairs with any detonate build. Gives up the throughput of Emberglass and the burst of Searing Resolve. `NEW: on self-detonation of >=4 Burn stacks, apply a 2-turn self status granting intakePct −30% (conditional, detonation-gated mitigation).`
- **Searing Resolve** — +14% damage while your focus target is below 30% HP. *Tradeoff:* the no-defense execute build — pure offense for the soft-enrage race (P12), trusting the timer to end before you're hit. Gives up all mitigation. `dmgPct: 14, onlyIf { targetHpBelowPct: 30 }`
- **Emberglass** — −8% damage taken and +4% damage. *Tradeoff:* the balanced floor — a thin always-on cushion plus a little output, for a player who wants neither a rotation-gated ward nor a glass-cannon gamble. Gives up the high end of both siblings. `intakePct: -8, dmgPct: 4`

### Tier 5 — The Last Coal (capstone — re-keys the build)
- **Conflagrant Bloom** — While 3 or more enemies are up, +16% damage; capstone: your Burn spreads from any detonated target to ALL surviving enemies in the pack. *Tradeoff:* the contagion-AoE capstone — converts the whole pack into one shared Burn pool that every detonation feeds; the definitive P3 trash-melter. Gives up single-target and control entirely. `dmgPct: 16, onlyIf { enemiesAtLeast: 3 }` plus `NEW: on self-detonation, spread the pre-detonation Burn stacks of the detonated target to ALL living enemies (full-pack contagion capstone).`
- **Crown of Cinders** — +18% damage while your focus target is below 35% HP; capstone: detonating Burn on a target below 35% HP refunds half the stacks consumed, letting you re-detonate the execute target. *Tradeoff:* the burst/execute capstone (P12 soft-enrage) — a chain-detonation kill engine on the boss. Gives up all pack coverage and control. `dmgPct: 18, onlyIf { targetHpBelowPct: 35 }` plus `NEW: when detonating Burn on a target below 35% HP, refund floor(stacksConsumed / 2) Burn stacks to that target (execute-phase stack refund).`
- **Warden of Embers** — capstone: your first detonation-stun each fight lasts +3s, and while a target is stunned or Burn-locked your Magic damage to it is +30%. *Tradeoff:* the control capstone — pays off the Pyre-Vents delay/lock identity (P1 + P8) with a damage spike on the locked target, fusing control into throughput. Requires the Pyre-Vents path to function; gives up raw AoE and execute ceiling. `NEW: extend the first detonation-stun of each encounter by +1 turn; while a target has the 'stun' status, this caster's Magic damage to it is +30% (control-into-damage capstone).`

**Builds:** (1) Contagion AoE-melter — Creeping Embers → Cinderburst → Far-Cast Ember → Ashen Veil → Conflagrant Bloom (P3 armour wall + P11 reach across a pack). (2) Detonation-control — Kindled Patience → Pyre-Vents → Banked Heat → Ashen Veil → Warden of Embers (P1 cast-delay + P8 add-lock with a payoff). (3) Tall single-target / execute — Smothering Ash → Stoked Furnace/Cinderburst → Focused Pyre → Searing Resolve → Crown of Cinders (P3 lone wall + P12 soft-enrage). (4) Tempo sustained — Kindled Patience → Stoked Furnace → Banked Heat → Emberglass → Conflagrant Bloom (long-timer steady detonation cycle).

## Arcanist — DPS (mage)

*The lockdown caster. You don't out-damage the pull, you shut it up — chill the swing out of a sword, snap the cast out of a throat, then collect interest on everything you've frozen in place.*

### Tier 1 — The Opening Read (how you choose your first target)
- **Silence the Spire** — Your single-target Silence and Spellbreak deal +18% damage, and your damage to any silenced or interrupted enemy is +12%. *Tradeoff:* the focused-lockdown build — pays off when one Dangerous-Caster must die first, but does nothing on a silent trash wave; gives up the wide chill of its siblings. `NEW: per-talent dmgPct conditioned on a new onlyIf type 'targetHasStatus' (silence); plus a flat dmgPct rider on the spec's two lockdown abilities (talent-scoped ability-buff hook).`
- **Glaciate** — Your Arcane Bolt's bonus against CC'd targets rises from +30% to +50%, and Chilled targets (not just stunned/frozen) now count as CC'd for that bonus. *Tradeoff:* the sustained-amplify build — every bolt into your own chill snowballs, but it's all pressure and no hard stop; gives up the burst interrupt-damage of Silence the Spire. `NEW: extend Arcane Bolt's 'targetHasStatus: cc' modifier to include 'chill' and raise its multiplier (talent-scoped ability-modifier override).`
- **Glittering Frost** — +14% damage while 3 or more enemies are up. *Tradeoff:* the trash-wave floor — unconditional-feeling cleave for add packs, but flat and identity-light; gives up both the lockdown synergy and the chill-amplify of its siblings. `dmgPct: 14, onlyIf { enemiesAtLeast: 3 } (EXISTING).`

### Tier 2 — The Lock (what you do when the adds arrive — P8)
- **Hushing Frost** — Glacial Burst also Silences every enemy it hits for 3s (6s with Frost Attunement). *Tradeoff:* the add-lock build — turns your one AoE into a true mass shut-up for a Dangerous-Add wave, but adds nothing to single-target boss work; gives up the freeze-stacking and the wider chill of its siblings. `NEW: add an applyStatus(silence) effect to Glacial Burst when this talent is owned (talent-scoped ability-effect injection).`
- **Deepening Cold** — Arcane Bolt applies 1 extra Chill stack (so a single bolt arms Glacial Burst's Freeze), and your Freeze chance on 2+ Chill rises from 30% to 70%. *Tradeoff:* the freeze-lock build — reliably hard-CCs a whole pack with one Glacial Burst, but needs setup time and a Burst off cooldown; gives up the instant silence of Hushing Frost and the chill-throughput of Rimebrand. `NEW: +1 stack on Arcane Bolt's chill application + override Glacial Burst's freeze chancePct (talent-scoped status/param edits).`
- **Rimebrand** — Each enemy you Chill takes +10% magic damage while Chilled (a Mark-like rider, magnitude set on apply). *Tradeoff:* the chill-as-damage build — converts your spammy slow into raw pack damage with no CC commitment, but never actually stops a cast or add; gives up both lockdown forks above. `NEW: applyStatus that adds a damageTaken% mod keyed to Chill, or a new 'damageTaken-while-chilled' status rider.`

### Tier 3 — The Barrier's Purpose (your shield was gutted — pick its new soul)
- **Warded Mind** — While shielded by Rune Barrier, you (and the rider's allies) are immune to incoming CC AND your spell haste is +15% — the lockdown caster who can't be locked down. *Tradeoff:* the uptime/control build — guarantees your interrupt lands through enemy CC and speeds your rotation, but is pure utility with no direct damage; gives up the offensive barrier of its siblings. `NEW: tie the existing 'cc-immunity-while-shielded' rider to a haste buff while the shield holds (talent-scoped buff on a status condition).`
- **Shattering Aegis** — Rune Barrier's break/expiry AoE deals +60% damage and Chills everyone it hits. *Tradeoff:* the burst-detonation build — folds your defensive into a pack-clearing bomb that re-arms your Chill engine, but the shield is now a delivery system you'll pop early; gives up the protective uptime of Warded Mind. `NEW: scale up the existing 'barrier-break-aoe' magnitude and add a chill applyStatus to it (talent-scoped ability-param edit).`
- **Frostward Veil** — When Rune Barrier expires it leaves you (only) at -10% damage taken for 6s; while it holds you cannot be interrupted. *Tradeoff:* the self-survival timer-build — a small, *keyed* personal buffer for the back line under a soft-enrage, never a generic party wall; gives up the party CC-immunity and the burst of its siblings. `intakePct (self) keyed to a post-expiry window — NEW: a timed self intakePct buff triggered by an ability event (existing intake channel, new trigger).`

### Tier 4 — The Engine (what fuels your sustained throughput)
- **Cold Focus** — +6% crit chance, and your crits against Chilled or frozen targets deal +25% bonus crit damage. *Tradeoff:* the crit-snowball build — rewards bolting into your own CC for spiky payoffs, but flat-lines against CC-immune bosses; gives up the always-on tempo of Resonant Cadence. `critPct: 6 (EXISTING) + NEW: bonus crit-damage multiplier conditioned on targetHasStatus(chill/freeze).`
- **Resonant Cadence** — +8% damage always, and every fourth Arcane Bolt refunds a slice of its cast time, smoothing your tempo. *Tradeoff:* the reliable tempo floor — the safe pick that works on every target including CC-immune ones, but its ceiling sits below the conditional siblings; gives up the chill-keyed crit of Cold Focus and the execute spike of Winter's Hunger. `dmgPct: 8 (EXISTING) + NEW: small periodic cast-time refund on every Nth Arcane Bolt (talent-scoped cadence rider).`
- **Winter's Hunger** — +20% damage while your focus target is below 35% HP. *Tradeoff:* the execute build — closes kills fast in the soft-enrage race (P12), but is dead weight at full HP; gives up the chill-keyed crit of Cold Focus and the unconditional floor of Resonant Cadence. `dmgPct: 20, onlyIf { targetHpBelowPct: 35 } (EXISTING).`

### Tier 5 — The Capstone (commit to who you are)
- **Winterwarden** — Your first CC each fight lasts +3s, and while a target is frozen, silenced, OR stunned, your Magic damage to it is +30%. *Tradeoff:* the control-capstone — the lockdown identity's punchline, converting every hard-CC into a damage window (P1+P8 → P3), but worthless against an unCCable target and reliant on landing the lock; gives up the unconditional output of its siblings. `NEW: stack onto Frost Attunement's 'first-cc-duration-extension' + a dmgPct conditioned on targetHasStatus(freeze/silence/stun) — new onlyIf type.`
- **The Long Winter** — Spellbreak's cooldown drops from 9s to 6s and its interrupt now adds +9s (was +6s) to the target's next cast; killing an interrupted or silenced enemy refunds 3s off Spellbreak. *Tradeoff:* the interrupt-tyrant build — the cleanest possible P1 cancel, denying caster after caster on a tight loop, but it's all denial and adds no raw damage; gives up Winterwarden's CC-damage payoff and the AoE ceiling of Glacial Cataclysm. `NEW: talent-scoped cooldown reduction + interrupt-param edit + on-kill cooldown refund (new on-kill cooldown hook).`
- **Glacial Cataclysm** — Glacial Burst's cooldown drops by 3s and it deals +35% damage; if it Freezes 2 or more enemies, its cooldown resets. *Tradeoff:* the AoE-capstone — chains Bursts across back-to-back trash packs for an unrivaled clear, but needs bodies on the floor to snowball and offers little single-target; gives up the boss-killing focus of its two siblings. `NEW: talent-scoped Glacial Burst cd reduction + dmgPct, plus a cooldown-reset triggered by N freeze applications (new on-effect cooldown-reset hook).`

**Builds:** (1) **Add-Lock controller** — Hushing Frost + Warded Mind + Glacial Cataclysm: shut down and freeze Dangerous-Add waves (P8). (2) **Interrupt-tyrant denier** — Silence the Spire + Warded Mind + The Long Winter: the premier P1 single-caster cancel that can't be peeled off. (3) **Frost-amp executioner** — Glaciate/Deepening Cold + Cold Focus + Winterwarden: freeze a target and dump amplified Magic burst into it (P3). (4) **Trash-shredder** — Glittering Frost + Rimebrand + Shattering Aegis + Glacial Cataclysm: chill-everything, no-hard-CC AoE pressure.

## Lifebinder — Healer (sage)

*The proactive HoT-coverage and Nature-dispel healer: she pre-blankets the party in rolling growth so the heals are already ticking when the floor opens up. Strong over time and against creeping plague; deliberately a beat slow on the single hard spike.*

### Tier 1 — The Grove's Reach (how wide does the green grow?)
- **Verdant Spread** — Your rolling HoTs (Renewing Bloom/Wildbloom/Nature's Breath) tick for +18% while 3 or more enemies are up, but lose that bonus on a near-cleared pull. *Tradeoff:* the trash-week / wide-coverage build — maximum coverage HPS when the room is full; gives up the focused output of the boss-week sibling and the personal floor of the survival sibling. `dmgPct: 18, onlyIf { enemiesAtLeast: 3 }` (folds into the healing output channel — FREE).
- **Last Light** — +16% healing (and damage) while the focus enemy is below 40% HP — your green surges as the pull dies. *Tradeoff:* the boss-week / execute-window build, pairing with the burst forks below; weak during the long full-room stretch where Verdant Spread shines. `dmgPct: 16, onlyIf { targetHpBelowPct: 40 }` (FREE).
- **Quick Bloom** — Once per fight, instantly refresh every active HoT to full duration — a free re-blanket you bank for the moment the floor opens. *Tradeoff:* the safety pick for new/under-geared rosters — a panic button that buys a fresh layer of coverage with no upkeep, but it's a single charge that does nothing for raw throughput, so it falls off once the timer (not survival) is the wall. `NEW: once-per-fight active that refreshes all of the bearer's active HoTs to full duration (no HoT-refresh active or per-fight charge exists in talents).`

### Tier 2 — The Cleansing Word (lean into the typed dispel, or branch toward output)
- **Cleansing Litany** — Your Nature/Poison cleanse strips +1 debuff and grants each cleansed ally +10% power for 6s. *Tradeoff:* the anti-Weltering-Plague specialist — turns the Mire's P4 answer into a party damage swing; useless on a pull with nothing to dispel, where the other two siblings always work. `NEW: talent that modifies the lifebinder's cleanse ability — +1 cleanse count and an on-cleanse power buff (no talent field exists for cleanse-count or post-cleanse buffs today).`
- **Spring's Haste** — Your cleanse comes off cooldown faster (9s to 6s), so the plague never escalates past you. *Tradeoff:* the throughput-of-utility pick — more dispel uptime against fast-stacking decay, but no power swing and no extra strip; loses to Cleansing Litany on a single big curse and to Verdant Surge on a clean pull. `NEW: talent that reduces a specific ability's cooldownTurns (talents cannot touch ability cooldowns today).`
- **Verdant Surge** — +10% healing output, always. You walk away from the dispel's depth for a flat, unconditional green floor. *Tradeoff:* the generalist / clean-week pick — the safe choice when the affix isn't plague; gives up every dispel upgrade, so the Mire run punishes it. `dmgPct: 10` (FREE).

### Tier 3 — Duration vs. Magnitude (the P5/P6 identity, baked into one octagon)
- **Evergreen Renewal** — Your rolling HoTs gain +3s of duration and tick +10% harder, but can never crit — a longer, gentler green that OUTLASTS the heaviest sustained pressure. *Tradeoff:* the P6 coverage build — the pre-blanket that survives the long grind; explicitly surrenders the spike (no crit, no burst) so it loses the single P5 hammer by design. `NEW: talent that extends HoT duration by +1 tick AND adds a flat HoT-magnitude bonus AND suppresses crit on HoTs (no duration, HoT-specific magnitude, or crit-suppression field exists in talents).`
- **Sudden Bloom** — Your Wildbloom blooms for +35% burst on trigger/expire, and +15% crit while the focus enemy is below 50% HP — short, intense green for the kill window. *Tradeoff:* the burst-triage build, buying back a piece of the P5 spike the spec normally lacks; the HoTs are shorter and gamble on the bloom landing on time, so they're worse at the sustained tick. `critPct: 15` is FREE but conditional crit needs `NEW: gate critPct behind onlyIf { targetHpBelowPct } (onlyIf currently gates ONLY dmgPct), plus NEW: talent that amplifies Wildbloom's bloom-heal magnitude.`
- **Even Tending** — +7% healing and +5% crit, unconditionally. Neither longest nor sharpest — a steady middle that asks nothing of your timing. *Tradeoff:* the low-skill-floor balanced pick; never the best at coverage OR at the spike, so it's the deliberately-weak safe fork you trade up from. `dmgPct: 7, critPct: 5` (FREE).

### Tier 4 — Vital Surge's Tribute (what does consuming the garden buy?)
- **Verdant Reclamation** — When Vital Surge consumes 3+ HoT stacks, it also strips 1 Nature/Poison debuff from the target. *Tradeoff:* the burst-and-cleanse fusion for the plague fight — folds emergency triage and a dispel into one button; wasted when there's nothing to cleanse, where the damage sibling always pays out. `NEW: talent that adds a conditional cleanse rider to a specific ability, gated on consumed-HoT-stacks (no cleanse rider or self-stack condition in talents).`
- **Thornward Vigor** — +14% damage while 3 or more enemies are up — your nature-bolt and bloom backlash actually hurt the pack, letting you contribute to the trash race while healing. *Tradeoff:* the damage-while-healing / fast-clear build for over-geared weeks; pure offence, so it adds nothing on a single-target boss or a dispel-heavy room. `dmgPct: 14, onlyIf { enemiesAtLeast: 3 }` (FREE).
- **Sheltering Bloom** — −10% damage taken for any ally currently carrying one of your HoTs. *Tradeoff:* the active-mitigation coverage pick — keyed to your own pre-blanket so it's only EHP where you've already planted, never undifferentiated party DR; gives up Vital Surge's cleanse and the trash damage, and does nothing on an un-HoTed ally caught flat-footed. `NEW: keyed intakePct that applies only to allies who currently have a Lifebinder HoT (talent intakePct is uniform-self today and cannot read ally HoT status).`

### Tier 5 — The Deep Commitment (the capstone fork that re-keys the build)
- **Verdant Reckoning** — While any ally is below 30% HP, your Magic damage also heals the party for 50% of the damage dealt — an off-school answer to the armour wall (P3) that turns triage into smite-atonement. *Tradeoff:* the damage-dealing-healer capstone for armour weeks; demands you spend GCDs on damage instead of pure heals, so it loses raw throughput exactly when the group is already low. `NEW: smite-atonement mechanic gated on lowest-ally-HP (no damage-to-party-heal conversion or ally-HP condition in talents).`
- **Endless Spring** — Your rolling HoTs no longer expire on their own while the bearer is below 50% HP — the green keeps tending the wounded until they're safe. *Tradeoff:* the ultimate P6 coverage capstone, the answer to sustained pressure that never lets up; pure prevention, it does nothing for a healthy party and cannot save a one-shot spike. `NEW: HoT-persistence mechanic — suppress HoT expiry while the bearer is below an HP threshold (no HoT-lifetime control or bearer-HP condition in talents).`
- **Gale Severance** — Your enemy purge (P9) also rips a buff from every adjacent enemy and you gain +12% crit for 6s after a purge. *Tradeoff:* the utility/anti-buff capstone — leans the spec into offensive dispel for buff-heavy packs and rewards it with a personal crit window; the weakest pick on a fight with nothing to purge, where the two healing capstones always work. `critPct: 12` is FREE as a flat passive, but the purge-AoE and on-purge trigger need `NEW: talent that adds an enemy buff-strip rider + an on-purge self-crit-buff to the lifebinder's purge ability.`

**Builds:** (1) **Rolling-HoT sustained coverage** — Verdant Spread into Evergreen Renewal into Endless Spring: the pure P6 pre-blanket that outlasts the grind. (2) **Plague-cleanse Mire specialist** — Cleansing Litany into Verdant Reclamation, with Spring's Haste uptime: the P4 Weltering-Plague answer with a party power swing. (3) **Burst-triage / off-school smiter** — Last Light into Sudden Bloom into Verdant Reckoning: buys back a slice of the P5 spike and pays armour-wall (P3) weeks in damage-as-healing. (4) **Damage-while-healing fast-clear** — Verdant Spread into Thornward Vigor into Gale Severance: a contributor on over-geared trash and buff-heavy purge weeks.

## Mystic — Tank (Spirit-Warden)

*The control tank: you don't raise a wall, you stand in the pack and turn its blows back on it — parry, counter, kill the cast, stun the add, drain it dry while you steady your breath. Survival here is earned with timing, never bought with a slab of EHP.*

### Tier 1 — The Counter (what your parry is FOR)
The Mystic's whole defense is avoidance + counter. This tier decides what a successful parry pays out — selfish drain-back, group sustain, or a faster control loop.

- **Spirit Drinker** — While your Stone Stance is up, your counter-hits steal +50% more life back to you, and parrying refunds a sliver of Zen Strike's cooldown. *Tradeoff:* serves the lone lifesteal-bruiser build (stand-and-drink solo survival); gives up any group benefit and any extra control the siblings offer. `NEW: on-parry, reduce a named ability's remaining cooldown by N turns + scale the existing lifesteal mechanic's pct by the talent.`
- **Flowing Calm** — Each parry also heals the lowest-HP ally for a small amount (your counter becomes a trickle of party sustain). *Tradeoff:* the soft-support tank — props up a thin healer through trash, but the heal is small and you keep none of the self-drain; loses Spirit Drinker's solo wall and Stone Cadence's tempo. `NEW: on-parry heal-lowest-ally hook (extends the existing on-parry-heal mechanic's target to lowest-hp-ally).`
- **Stone Cadence** — Every parry shaves 3s off Earthen Sweep's cooldown; the more they swing, the more you sweep. *Tradeoff:* the control engine — turns a busy pack into near-permanent AoE stun uptime; gives up the self-heal of Spirit Drinker and the group heal of Flowing Calm, betting that locking the pack down beats out-sustaining it. `NEW: on-parry, reduce Earthen Sweep cooldown by N turns (parry→CC reset loop).`

### Tier 2 — Single Lock vs. Sweeping Lock (your control's shape)
The Mystic answers P8 (add lock) with Earthen Sweep and P1 (kill-cast) with Zen Strike. This tier is where your CC commits to a shape — one hard answer, or wide soft pressure.

- **Iron Focus** — Zen Strike's interrupt also leaves the silenced target unable to cast for an extra 3s (the kick lands harder and buys real time). *Tradeoff:* the dedicated P1 cancel — best on dangerous-cast bosses where one clean kick matters; gives up the AoE reach of the other two against trash waves. `NEW: interrupt also applies a 1-turn silence status to the interrupted target.`
- **Gathering Storm** — Earthen Sweep hits the whole front band instead of just 2 adjacent, and its stun catches everything it touches. *Tradeoff:* the P8 add-wave answer — locks an entire pack at once; gives up Iron Focus's surgical kick-extend and Resonant Chime's payoff, and does nothing extra to a single hard caster. `NEW: widen Earthen Sweep targeting pattern from adjacent/2 to all-front-band.`
- **Resonant Chime** — Enemies you stun take +20% damage from the party while stunned (your lockdown becomes the party's kill window). *Tradeoff:* the control-into-throughput bridge — makes the DPS's job easier on every Earthen Sweep; needs allies to capitalize, and gives up the raw single-target lock (Iron Focus) and the full-pack lock (Gathering Storm). `NEW: stunned enemies take +X% damage (a vulnerability debuff applied alongside the existing stun).`

### Tier 3 — Active Mitigation (how you survive the spike, without a wall)
Pure timer-bound design: no flat %DR brick here. Each option is an *active* or *conditional* survival lever tied to your stance/health, never an always-on slab.

- **Rooted Stance** — While your Stone Stance is active, you take meaningfully less damage; the instant it drops, so does the mitigation. *Tradeoff:* rewards perfect stance uptime — strong for the pilot who plays the cooldown, dead weight in the gaps; gives up the always-on cushion of Steadying Brew and the burst-recovery of Rising Breath. `NEW: conditional intakePct that applies ONLY while a named self-buff (stance) is active (current intakePct is unconditional).`
- **Steadying Brew** — A short, on-demand draught: drink to soften incoming damage for a brief window, then it's spent — a small active cushion you pay for with timing, not a passive slab. *Tradeoff:* the safety pick for an unsteady pilot or a thin healer; deliberately modest in both size and uptime, but it's an active button rather than free EHP; gives up Rooted Stance's high ceiling and Rising Breath's clutch save. `NEW: short cooldownTurns active that applies a brief-duration intakePct buff on cast (a small, spec-flavored brew button, not unconditional %DR).`
- **Rising Breath** — When you drop below 35% HP, your next stance grants a burst of self-regen on top of its parry. *Tradeoff:* the comeback lever — saves you from the spike that would have killed you, then idles when healthy; gives up Rooted Stance's sustained mitigation and Steadying Brew's reliability, betting on the one moment that matters. `NEW: onlyIf self-HP-below threshold → on next stance cast, add a regen-HoT (new self-low-HP condition + stance-keyed regen).`

### Tier 4 — The Peel (Spirit Tether's identity — P6 drain answer)
Spirit Tether is the Mystic's one reach into ally survival, deliberately capped so it can't become generic off-heal EHP. This tier shapes that single peel — bigger soak, broader reach, or duration-vs-magnitude.

- **Shared Burden** — Spirit Tether redirects more of the ally's damage onto you and the HoT outlasts the draining tick (a long, gentle save). *Tradeoff:* the P6 sustained-drain answer — the *right* tool against a wither/poison tick that grinds an ally; gives up Severing Tether's emergency magnitude and Twin Spirits' coverage. `NEW: scale damage-redirect's redirectPct + extend the spirit-link-hot duration via the talent.`
- **Severing Tether** — Spirit Tether's HoT front-loads into a short, intense burst (a hard spike-soak now, gone fast). *Tradeoff:* the P5 reactive-spike answer — yanks an ally out of a one-shot window; the inverse of Shared Burden, useless against a slow grind, and narrower than Twin Spirits. `NEW: duration-vs-magnitude flip on spirit-link-hot (shorter duration, higher per-tick magnitude).`
- **Twin Spirits** — Spirit Tether links a second nearby ally at half effect (the soak and HoT split across two). *Tradeoff:* the multi-target peel for chaotic pulls where two allies are dipping; each gets less, so it loses the single-target depth of both siblings — breadth over depth. `NEW: Spirit Tether targets a 2nd ally at reduced (50%) effect (multi-target redirect/HoT).`

### Tier 5 — Capstone: the Mystic you become
The deep commitment node. Each capstone re-keys the whole build around one of the three fantasies the tree has been building toward — the mountain, the warden, or the spirit-monk executioner.

- **The Mountain Holds** — Each parry has a chance to fully reset Earthen Sweep, and Living Mountain's regen ticks for the entire fight at reduced strength. *Tradeoff:* the immovable control-tank capstone — pairs with Stone Cadence/Gathering Storm for near-permanent pack lockdown and self-sustain; gives up all party reach and all burst, you are the rock the pull breaks on. `NEW: on-parry chance to reset Earthen Sweep cooldown + a fight-long low-magnitude self-regen (extends Living Mountain).`
- **Warden of Spirits** — While you hold hard threat, your Spirit Tether can be active on two allies at once and its redirect cannot be broken — but your own outgoing damage is halved. *Tradeoff:* the pure protector capstone — the ultimate P6/P7 peel for a fragile comp; the damage halving makes it a real cost and locks you out of any kill-pressure build. `NEW: allow two concurrent Spirit Tethers + un-cleansable redirect, gated on holding hard threat; -50% self dmgPct.`
- **Hand of Stillwater** — Below 30% target HP, your Palm Strike and Zen Strike strike twice and your lifesteal doubles — you finish the pull by drinking it dry. *Tradeoff:* the lifesteal-executioner capstone — turns the soft-enrage finish into self-sustaining burst (pairs with Spirit Drinker's intake); gives up all the control and peel payoffs, the only capstone that bends the timer instead of the survival floor. `EXISTING+NEW: dmgPct with onlyIf targetHpBelowPct:30 (free) + NEW double-strike & lifesteal-doubling on the two named abilities below 30%.`

**Builds:** (1) **Stone Warden** — control-tank: Stone Cadence + Gathering Storm + Rooted Stance + Twin Spirits + The Mountain Holds = permanent pack lockdown. (2) **Lone Mountain** — selfish lifesteal-bruiser: Spirit Drinker + Iron Focus + Rooted Stance + Severing Tether + Hand of Stillwater = stand-and-drink solo survival that finishes fast. (3) **Spirit Shepherd** — protector/peel tank: Flowing Calm + Resonant Chime + Rising Breath + Shared Burden + Warden of Spirits = the answer for a fragile comp's P6/P5 problems. (4) **Kill-Window Bruiser** — control-into-throughput hybrid: Stone Cadence + Resonant Chime + Rising Breath + Severing Tether + Hand of Stillwater = lock, expose, and execute.

## Bonecaller — DPS (Necromancer)

*The back-line death-caller: it doesn't kill the pull, it lets the pull rot — feeding a stacking wasting curse, raising a skeletal servant from the fallen, and draining the spark back into itself.*

### Tier 1 — The Wasting (how your signature rot is shaped)
- **Creeping Waste** — Your wasting curse ramps faster: every refresh adds an extra stack, so a single target rots toward its cap in half the time. *Tradeoff:* the single-target / boss specialist's opener — front-loads your ramp so bosses melt sooner, but does nothing extra against a wide pull and offers no cushion. `dmgPct +18, onlyIf targetHpBelowPct 100 (always-on against the focus target — tuned as the single-target ramp lever). NEW (full fidelity): wasting-curse status applies +1 stack per reapply (faster DoT ramp on the focus).`
- **Rot on the Wind** — When you apply your wasting curse, a thinner rot also seeds the two nearest enemies. *Tradeoff:* the trash-clear answer — turns one curse into a whole rotting pack, but each seed is weaker, so it loses to Creeping Waste on a lone boss. `dmgPct +20, onlyIf enemiesAtLeast 3. NEW (full fidelity): on curse-apply, applyStatus a weaker rot DoT to 2 adjacent enemies (contagion seed).`
- **Patient Decay** — Your rot can no longer crit, but every active DoT ticks for a flat 15% more, forever. *Tradeoff:* the steady-floor pick — unconditional, reliable rot throughput that ignores enemy count and HP, but gives up the crit ceiling and the spread/ramp of its siblings. `dmgPct +12 (unconditional). NEW (full fidelity): DoT ticks +X% but cannot crit (duration-magnitude trade).`

### Tier 2 — The Servant (what you raise from the dead)
- **Raise the Barrow-Hound** — Your skeletal servant is a lean front-line hound: high attack rate, claws at the nearest enemy, and reclaims a fresh body whenever one falls so it never stays down long. *Tradeoff:* the sustained-pet-DPS build's core — a relentless extra body that snowballs through a pull, but it's fragile and contributes nothing while no corpse is available to re-raise. `dmgPct +10 (models the pet's steady extra output). NEW (full fidelity): summon a persistent fast melee pet (front band) that re-raises off the next corpse on death (pet engine, L.5).`
- **Bind the Carrion Choir** — Instead of one hound you raise a flock of weak skeletal birds that harry the back band, picking at sheltered casters you can't otherwise reach. *Tradeoff:* the reach answer — the only Bonecaller pick that pressures the back line, but the flock is individually feeble and wasted when everything dangerous is already up front. `dmgPct +12, onlyIf enemiesAtLeast 3. NEW (full fidelity): summon multiple frail flyer pets targeting the back band (reach + spread).`
- **The Servant Endures** — You raise no minion; instead the unspent grave-power hardens your own flesh and lets your rot feed you a trickle of life on every tick. *Tradeoff:* the pet-less self-sufficient line — frees you from corpse dependency and adds standalone survivability, but forfeits the entire summon axis the other two build around. `maxHpPct +10, dmgPct +4. NEW (full fidelity): self-HoT proportional to active DoT ticks (drain-to-self), in lieu of a pet.`

### Tier 3 — The Drain (how you turn their dying into your living)
- **Drink the Fading** — Your life-drain channels harder against a dying target, returning far more of the damage as healing once the enemy drops low. *Tradeoff:* the execute-sustain pick — keeps you (and your build's drain economy) alive through the kill window, but the bonus sleeps until the target is nearly dead. `dmgPct +16, onlyIf targetHpBelowPct 35. NEW (full fidelity): drain returns +X% as self-heal vs. low-HP targets (execute-keyed lifesteal).`
- **Glut of the Grave** — Each corpse that falls near you releases a burst of soul-rot, damaging everything still standing in the pack. *Tradeoff:* the AoE-on-kill snowball — converts a dying pack into chain damage that accelerates a wipe, but pays out nothing on a single boss with no adds to drop. `dmgPct +14, onlyIf enemiesAtLeast 3. NEW (full fidelity): on enemy death, applyStatus a short AoE rot pulse to remaining enemies (on-kill cascade).`
- **The Quiet Reserve** — Your drain becomes a slow, certain trickle that heals you a fixed amount on every cast regardless of the target. *Tradeoff:* the unconditional safety floor — dependable self-sustain that holds up against any pull, but it never spikes and surrenders the burst payoff of its two siblings. `intakePct -10. NEW (full fidelity): flat self-heal per cast (steady drain, no condition).`

### Tier 4 — The Curse Deepens (lean into rot, or branch toward raw death-magic)
- **Marrow-Deep Plague** — Your wasting curse gains extra duration and outlasts any cleanse window, but its ticks can no longer be amplified by your cooldowns — it is pure, patient attrition. *Tradeoff:* the contagion/attrition capstone-feeder — maximal DoT uptime that wins long, sustained fights, but caps your burst ceiling and rewards patience over tempo. `dmgPct +12 (models extended uptime). NEW (full fidelity): curse +duration, immune to one cleanse, but excluded from burst-window multipliers.`
- **Hex of the Hollow Bell** — Branch away from rot into direct death-magic: your hardest cast hits far harder against a wounded target, a clean spike instead of a slow bleed. *Tradeoff:* the burst/single-target branch — gives the curse build a real spike finisher, but abandons the spread-and-wait identity and does nothing while the target is healthy. `dmgPct +22, onlyIf targetHpBelowPct 50. NEW (full fidelity): a direct-damage nuke (not a DoT) scaling with target missing-HP.`
- **Withering Aura** — A passive miasma clings to you, dealing a small flat amount to every enemy near the front each tick — no setup, no condition, no ramp. *Tradeoff:* the zero-effort AoE floor for a pet/cleave build that wants its attention elsewhere, but the per-target damage is low and it scales poorly into single-target. `dmgPct +8 (unconditional, models the aura). NEW (full fidelity): self-anchored aura emitting a flat AoE rot tick to nearby enemies.`

### Tier 5 — The Last Toll (the deep capstone — what your build becomes)
- **The Marrow Throne** — Capstone of the pet line: while your skeletal servant lives, your own death-magic surges, and slaying an enemy immediately re-raises the servant from its corpse at full strength. *Tradeoff:* the summoner-snowball finisher — turns a timed pull into a self-feeding minion engine that accelerates through trash, but collapses to near-baseline the moment your servant is dead and no corpse is up. `dmgPct +18, onlyIf enemiesAtLeast 3. NEW (full fidelity): while pet alive, +X% caster damage; on kill, instantly re-raise the pet (pet-loop capstone).`
- **Crowned in Rot** — Capstone of the curse line: once your wasting curse reaches full stacks on a target, every other DoT you own on it detonates for a burst, then the curse resets to seed again. *Tradeoff:* the wasting-detonation finisher — a huge windowed payoff that rewards full-ramp single-target play, but demands the target survive long enough to cap and gives nothing to a build that spreads thin. `dmgPct +20, onlyIf targetHpBelowPct 50. NEW (full fidelity): at max curse stacks, detonate all DoTs on the target for burst, then reset curse stacks (resource-flip capstone).`
- **The Last Vigil** — Capstone of the sustain/utility line: you pre-place a quiet binding on one ally so that, should they fall, they rise once on their own — a self-contained battle-rez held in reserve. *Tradeoff:* the safety/utility capstone for a survival or progression build — a real wipe-insurance answer no other DPS brings, but it spends your deep node on resilience rather than the throughput the other two capstones grant. `intakePct -8, maxHpPct +6 (the throughput a survival build trades down to). NEW (full fidelity): pre-placed single-use battle-rez on a flagged ally (binds to sim.rez*; the "Calling Back the Quiet" family).`

**Builds:** **Sustained pet-master** (Raise the Barrow-Hound → The Servant Endures off-ramp → The Marrow Throne) snowballs trash with a re-raising minion; **single-target wasting/detonation** (Creeping Waste → Marrow-Deep Plague / Hex of the Hollow Bell → Crowned in Rot) ramps one target to a burst kill; **AoE rot-spreader** (Rot on the Wind → Glut of the Grave → Withering Aura) contagions a whole pull; **drain-survivalist / battle-rez utility** (The Servant Endures → The Quiet Reserve → The Last Vigil) trades damage for self-sustain and a unique party-saving pre-placed rez.

## Pallbinder — Healer (Necromancer)

*The death-priest who heals by drawing the spark from the dying — the grim mirror of the Lifebinder. Where the grove heals through nature, the Pallbinder heals through death: a bound guardian-wraith feeds anima to the party while a hoarded "Spark" resource turns the dying into someone else's second breath.*

> **Signature class-tools** (ability-side, the niche the tree bends around): the bound **Wraith** (guardian-wraith conduit that drains an enemy and pours the leeched anima into the lowest ally), **Spark** (a death-fueled healing resource banked from enemy deaths and from the drain), and the shared Necromancer **pre-placed battle-rez** (Soulstone). The third healer SHAPE: not Cleric's reactive burst, not Lifebinder's rolling HoT — a drain-conduit attrition healer who is strongest the longer the dying lasts.

### Tier 1 — The Source of Healing (where your throughput comes from)
- **Carrion Conduit** — While your Wraith is bound to an enemy, your healing output is +14%; the bond does nothing for you while no enemy is being drained. *Tradeoff:* the pet-leech identity build — huge sustain in long drawn-out pulls, but it goes flat the instant there is nothing to drain (single low-HP target, between packs). Serves the Wraith/drain build; gives up the always-on safety of the balanced pick. `dmgPct: 14, onlyIf{enemiesAtLeast: 1}` (heals fold into the output channel; gated so the bonus only lives while there is something for the Wraith to bite).
- **Spark of the Spent** — +18% healing while your focus enemy is below 35% HP (you heal hardest off the dying). *Tradeoff:* the execute-attrition build — converts low-enemy-HP windows into healing surges, perfect for the soft-enrage race, but worthless on a healthy boss or a fresh pull. Serves a kill-window-timed build; gives up sustained early-pull coverage. `dmgPct: 18, onlyIf{targetHpBelowPct: 35}`.
- **Pall of Patience** — +9% healing, always, with no condition (the steady draw). *Tradeoff:* the reliable floor — never the highest ceiling, never zero either; the pick for players who do not want their healing to blink on and off. Serves a generalist build; gives up the spikes the two conditional siblings reach. `dmgPct: 9` (default/balanced).

### Tier 2 — Wide or Deep (how the Spark spreads)
- **The Wraith's Wide Wings** — +20% healing while 3 or more enemies are up (the conduit feeds off a whole pack). *Tradeoff:* the trash-week / attrition build — turns big pulls into a rising tide of leeched anima, but folds on bosses and 1-2 enemy remainders. Serves AoE attrition; gives up single-target depth. `dmgPct: 20, onlyIf{enemiesAtLeast: 3}`.
- **The Last Vigil** — +20% healing while 2 or fewer enemies remain (everything pours into the one dying ally). *Tradeoff:* the boss / triage build — concentrates the whole draw into deep single-target healing for the tank-killer fight, but quiet during trash gauntlets. Serves single-target/boss; gives up pack coverage. `dmgPct: 20, onlyIf{enemiesAtMost: 2}`.
- **Even Tithe** — +10% healing, always (the death is owed evenly). *Tradeoff:* the safe middle — content-agnostic, neither AoE nor boss specialist; the floor pick that frees the other tiers to commit. Serves a generalist; gives up the 2x payoff the specialists get in their content. `dmgPct: 10` (default/balanced).

### Tier 3 — Tempo or Toll (sustained draw vs. death-fed spike)
- **The Slow Marrow** — +8% healing, always, plus +5% crit on your heals (a steady, slightly-streaky draw). *Tradeoff:* the tempo build — smooths the run with always-on output and crit-heal variance for occasional lucky tops; never windowed, so it cannot "save up" for the spike. Serves sustained coverage; gives up the burst the toll sibling front-loads. `dmgPct: 8, critPct: 5` (default/balanced).
- **Toll for the Dying** — +17% healing while your focus enemy is below 50% HP (the bell tolls as it falls, and your draw redoubles). *Tradeoff:* the spike build — a windowed surge that front-loads healing into every kill, ideal for catching the spike that lands as a mob dies; flat while enemies are healthy. Serves burst/kill-window healing; gives up early-pull tempo. `dmgPct: 17, onlyIf{targetHpBelowPct: 50}`.
- **The Cold Hand's Certainty** — +12% crit on your heals, no output bonus (you cannot raise the average, only the ceiling). *Tradeoff:* the high-variance build — a fat crit-heal chance that swings between overkill tops and dry stretches; rewards a busy heal cadence and punishes a sparse one. Serves a gamble/spike-via-crit build; gives up the guaranteed floor the other two carry. `critPct: 12`.

### Tier 4 — The Wraith's Other Half (what the bound guardian does for YOU)
- **Anima-Fed** — While the Wraith is bound (an enemy alive to drain), you take -12% damage — the leeched anima sheaths the binder. *Tradeoff:* the survival-via-uptime build — strong personal mitigation, but it is *conditional and active*: the moment the pack is dead and there is nothing to drain, the shield drops, so it cannot be a flat-DR crutch in the dead air between fights. Serves a self-sustain drain build; gives up raw throughput. `intakePct: -12` (note: applied while at least one enemy lives — see new engine effect; otherwise it is the existing uniform intake knob). *NEW: gate intakePct on `enemiesAtLeast` (conditional intake) so the mitigation only lives while the Wraith has a target.*
- **Spite of the Spark** — +15% healing while 3 or more enemies are up, but no personal mitigation at all (you pour everything outward and stand exposed). *Tradeoff:* the glass-conduit AoE build — maximal pack healing at the cost of your own skin; pairs with a tank who can hold and a comp that does not need you alive between hits. Serves throughput-over-self; gives up all personal defense. `dmgPct: 15, onlyIf{enemiesAtLeast: 3}`.
- **The Quiet Between** — +10% max HP (a deeper grave to draw from). *Tradeoff:* the buffer build — a flat health pad that, unlike Anima-Fed, also protects you in the gaps with nothing to drain; the safe survival pick for chaotic pulls, but the least throughput of the tier and (in the timer-bound regime) the least "active" choice. Serves a defensive generalist; gives up both the conditional shield's magnitude and the AoE healing. `maxHpPct: 10`.

### Tier 5 — The Final Communion (the capstone identity, where the build commits)
- **Glut of the Grave** — +16% healing while 3 or more enemies are up (the pack-feast — the Wraith gorges and the whole party drinks). *Tradeoff:* the deep AoE-attrition capstone — stacks with the wide-wings line into an overwhelming trash-pull tide; collapses on bosses and the last 1-2 mobs. Serves the AoE/attrition build to its ceiling; gives up the kill-race and the floor. `dmgPct: 16, onlyIf{enemiesAtLeast: 3}`.
- **Reaper's Recompense** — +20% healing while your focus enemy is below 30% HP (the deeper you let it die, the more it owes the living). *Tradeoff:* the execute-healing capstone — the strongest possible draw off the dying, the answer to the soft-enrage / kill-window race; near-dead on full-HP targets. Serves the kill-window/boss attrition build; gives up healthy-pull and trash-week coverage. `dmgPct: 20, onlyIf{targetHpBelowPct: 30}`.
- **The Even Reckoning** — +11% healing, always, and +4% crit on heals (the patient toll that never stops). *Tradeoff:* the unconditional capstone — the only Tier-5 pick that pays on a healthy boss, a fresh pull, AND a dying mob; the lowest ceiling, the highest floor, the choice for a player who wants healing they can plan around. Serves the generalist/tempo build; gives up the doubled payoff the two conditional capstones reach in their content. `dmgPct: 11, critPct: 4` (default/balanced).

**Builds:** **Wraith-conduit attrition** (Carrion Conduit → wide-wings → tempo → Anima-Fed → Glut of the Grave: a self-sustaining AoE-feast healer that snowballs the longer a pull lasts) · **Death-spark executioner** (Spark of the Spent → Last Vigil → Toll for the Dying → Anima-Fed → Reaper's Recompense: a kill-window healer who is strongest in the soft-enrage race and quiet on healthy targets) · **Glass conduit** (Carrion Conduit → wide-wings → Cold Hand's Certainty → Spite of the Spark → Glut of the Grave: maximum pack throughput, no personal defense) · **The Patient Toll generalist** (Pall of Patience → Even Tithe → Slow Marrow → The Quiet Between → The Even Reckoning: an unconditional, plannable floor-healer that never blinks off).

# Cross-cutting synthesis — the talent revamp

Grounded in the live engine (`web/src/sim/egm/combat.ts`, `stats.ts`, `engine.ts`; `web/src/content/schema.ts`). One finding reframes the whole scope and leads.

## 0. The keystone: the talent `onlyIf` path is a *duplicate, impoverished* copy of an existing condition engine

Nearly every spec lists "NEW onlyIf condition type X" as engine work. That framing inflates the cost. The reality:

- There are **two** condition evaluators in the codebase. The rich one — `condHolds()` in `combat.ts` — *already* evaluates `targetHpBelowPct`, `selfHitSinceLastAction`, `targetHasStatus`, `selfHasStatus`, `targetBand`, `targetHotStacksAtLeast`. The poor one — the inline switch in `passiveDamageMult()` — only knows `{targetHpBelowPct, enemiesAtLeast, enemiesAtMost}` and **only gates `dmgPct`**. The talent schema enum (`schema.ts`, `effects.onlyIf.type`) mirrors the poor one.
- So the foundational task is **not** "implement N new predicates." It is: **(a)** widen the talent `onlyIf` enum to the full closed set; **(b)** route the talent path through `condHolds()` instead of the duplicated switch; **(c)** make `onlyIf` gate the `intakePct` and `critPct` channels in `resolveTalents` (which today apply both unconditionally and fold intake into a single uniform self channel).

Do that one unification and you simultaneously unblock **conditional intake/crit** (asked for by 11 of the 12 specs) and **status/band/self-HP-gated dmgPct**. This is the keystone; sequence it first. Treat the per-spec "NEW onlyIf type" bullets as *consumers* of this one shared deliverable. (This is sense (1) of "sequencing" from the design contract; sense (2) is the revamp-before-P.5c roadmap order.)

Predicates genuinely absent from `condHolds` today, to add to the closed enum: `enemiesAtLeast` / `enemiesAtMost` (in the talent switch but not `condHolds`), `selfHpBelowPct` (Berserker, Mystic, Guardian "below max HP", Archer's Roll Aside threshold), `selfStacksAtLeast` / resource count (Berserker Fury, Lifebinder/Mystic HoT-stacks), `allyHpBelowPct` / `lowestAllyHpBelowPct` (Cleric, Lifebinder, Crusader), `selfHoldsHardThreat` (Guardian, Crusader, Mystic). Everything else (`targetHasStatus`, `selfHasStatus`, `targetBand`, `selfHitSinceLastAction`) is already in `condHolds` and just needs exposing to talents.

## 1. Consolidated, deduped engine-effects backlog (the real scope)

Grouped by subsystem, with which specs drive each. "(exists ability-side)" = the mechanic lives in a `PlayerAbility` special today and the work is *promoting it into a talent channel*.

### A. Condition/gating engine (the keystone above — do first)
- **Unify talent `onlyIf` onto `condHolds` + extend closed enum** — all 10.
- **Gate `intakePct` via `onlyIf`** — Guardian, Berserker, Crusader, Cleric (general absorb), Assassin, Archer, Pyromancer, Arcanist, Lifebinder, Mystic.
- **Gate `critPct` via `onlyIf`** — Assassin, Archer, Lifebinder (+ Crusader/Cleric party-crit windows adjacent).
- **New predicates:** `selfHpBelowPct`, `selfStacksAtLeast`/resourceCount, `allyHpBelowPct`/`lowestAllyHpBelowPct`, `selfHoldsHardThreat` (see §0).

### B. `abilityOverride` talent channel — **enumerated per-kind (validation strict — Umut's call)**
A talent effect that patches a named ability's params at `buildParty` time covers a startling number of bullets (Berserker scalar patches, Cleric atonement/heal overrides, Arcanist interrupt/cooldown edits, Mystic targeting widen, Archer reach-shot rewrites, Crusader/Cleric charge-model edits, Lifebinder cooldown). **Decision: keep Zod strict** — do **not** ship one loose `{abilityId, params?…}` bag (a typo'd param would silently un-gate instead of fail-closed). Enumerate each override *kind* as its own typed, closed-schema effect (`abilityScalarOverride`, `abilityTargetingOverride`, `abilityCooldownOverride`, `abilityChargeOverride`, …). More schema to write, but a typo fails validation — the same fail-closed guarantee the current closed `onlyIf` enum gives. **Persistent note:** whenever this revamp adds a new ability-patch capability, add a new enumerated effect kind; never widen into a free-form bag "to save time." This is the contract that keeps content typo-safe.

### C. Status authoring from talents (apply / tune / suppress statuses)
- **applyStatus-on-enemy (debuff rider):** Berserker (Riving Wound −50% incoming healing — the P9 anti-heal), **Archer (Serrated Shot −40% incoming healing — the second P9 answer, Umut's add)**, Pyromancer (stun on detonate), Arcanist (silence on Glacial Burst, chill on barrier-break, +damageTaken-while-chilled), Mystic (silence on interrupt, +damage-taken-while-stunned), Lifebinder (purge buff-strip), Archer (Mark `damageTaken` magnitude), Crusader (daze on Oath-expiry).
- **Per-status magnitude/duration tuning channel (distinct from dmgPct):** Berserker (Bleed/Rending Cut +mag/+dur), Cleric (Burn +tick), Assassin (poison-tick mult), Pyromancer (Burn +mag/+dur), Lifebinder (HoT +mag/+dur), Arcanist (extra chill stack), Archer (`statusMod` on Mark).
- **Per-effect crit-suppression flag on a status:** Lifebinder (Evergreen Renewal, no-crit HoT).
- **Cleanse-immunity / exclude-from-burst flags:** Mystic (un-cleansable redirect).
A shared `statusMod` + `applyStatus` talent payload covers most of this; the suppress/immune flags are small booleans on the status instance.

### D. Atonement / damage-to-heal channel (healers)
- **Per-ability atonement overrides + disable flag + target-mode (rotate/exclude-last/party-swap):** Cleric (Choir of Dawn, Kindled Offering, Piercing Ray, Crowned in Light).
- **damage-to-party-heal gated on lowest-ally-HP:** Cleric (Crowned in Light, Final Light's self-extend), Lifebinder (Verdant Reckoning — the same mechanic, deliberately echoed across the holy/nature specs). Build once.
- Note `afterHit` already has an `atonement-heal` path with an `atonement-amp` onlyIf; this is an *extension*, not greenfield.

### E. Event-trigger riders (on-kill / on-crit / on-parry / on-detonate / on-cleanse / on-expiry)
The engine already has `reset-cooldown-on-kill` and `reset-cooldown-on-crit` ability-side. New talent-attachable riders:
- **on-kill:** resource refund (Berserker +2 Fury), self-cd-reset (Berserker Rampant Slaughter, Assassin finisher, Guardian grip via The Line Breaks), re-stealth+status (Assassin Fade to Black), AoE cascade (adjacent), conditional intake window.
- **on-crit:** lifesteal/heal (Assassin Predator's Vigor), guaranteed-next-crit chain (Assassin Bladed Cascade).
- **on-parry:** named-ability cd-reduction (Mystic Spirit Drinker→Zen, Stone Cadence→Earthen Sweep), heal-lowest-ally (Mystic Flowing Calm), parry→Earthen-Sweep-reset chance (Mystic capstone).
- **on-detonate:** stun rider, splash+1, cd-refund on Emberfall, self-DR window, full-pack contagion, execute stack-refund (Pyromancer — its whole tree is detonate-riders).
- **on-cleanse:** post-cleanse buff, +count, +dispelType (Cleric, Lifebinder).
- **on-effect-outcome:** Glacial Burst resets on 2+ freezes (Arcanist), interrupt→silence (Mystic, Arcanist).
- **on-self-hit:** queued one-shot dmg buff (Guardian Mounting Fury), damage-to-ally-lifesteal (Guardian Valor Repaid).
- **on-expiry:** post-expiry self-DR window (Arcanist Frostward Veil), CC on redirect-expiry (Crusader Reckoning of the Warden).
Recommend a single **`eventRider` talent shape** keyed by trigger enum — most are the same "on X, do {applyStatus | adjustCooldown | refundResource | heal | dealDamage}" pattern.

### F. Tank-tool extensions (existing ability mechanics widened)
- **thorns-reflect** → all-front-band + reflectPct scalar (Guardian Bristling Bulwark; mechanic exists).
- **grip** → +root, +target-count, on-kill-reset, convert-to-strike (Guardian Hook and Haul fork).
- **block-reflect** → reflectPct + route reflected as real damage event (Guardian Riposting Aegis; `blockReflectPct` exists); transferable-immunity variant (Guardian Sheltering Ward, Crusader).
- **redirect-damage** → +extra DR on redirected, min-1-HP death-immunity, multi-concurrent, un-cleansable (Crusader, Mystic; mechanic exists).
- **NEW Guardian Tier-3 "Sentinel's Voice"** (replaces the deleted P7-eruption intercept fork): a 60s party rallying buff (`applyStatus party {dmgPct, shieldAbsorb}`), a locked single-target hard-taunt (taunt-cannot-be-overridden state), and an AoE front-band soften + interrupt war cry. Reuses party-buff/taunt/debuff primitives; the **locked-taunt** state and **AoE-interrupt** are the net-new bits.
- **untargetable back-band aura while holding hard-taunt** + paired self −50% dmg (Guardian Sentinel's Burden; needs threat-state read + band-targetability flag — net-new).
- **shield variants:** no-crit-while-shielded, split-shield, shield-detonation-on-consume, overheal→shield on full-HP target, conditional overheal→shield magnitude (Crusader + Cleric — build as one shield-options bag). **Note (Umut): the "DoT-immune / direct-hit-only" absorb flag is CUT** — Cleric absorbs are now general (see §G).

### G. Typed-mitigation channels — **SCOPE REDUCED (Umut: paladin DR is general, not DoT-typed)**
The original design routed Crusader's rot-carry DR and Cleric's absorbs through a **DoT/recurring-tick-only** mitigation channel, which required the engine to *tag* tick damage as a separate channel — real new plumbing. **Per Umut's call, both are now general (any-damage) mitigation kept as timed cooldown windows.** Consequence: **the DoT-tagging plumbing is no longer required by any active spec.** Crusader's Bulwark Litany / Vault of the Dawn are general party-DR *windows* (gated to a cooldown, not flat auras); Cleric's Sanctuary Bloom / Final Light absorbs soak any damage. **→ Defer the tick-damage-tagging work entirely** unless a future spec re-introduces a typed-mitigation need. This removes one of the heavier engine items from the critical path.

### H. The summon/pet engine — **re-scoped as a GENERAL system (Umut), its own milestone**
A **persistent summoned combatant** that acts on the tick loop, occupies a band, contributes output + threat, can die/expire — confirmed there is **no party-side pet today** (the only `summon*` code spawns an *enemy* guard). Umut's direction: **build this regardless of the Necromancer** — it is useful immediately for **enemy summons on trash and bosses** (boss adds, summoned wardens, etc.), and the future Bonecaller minion reuses the same system when necro returns. It touches the combatant model, threat, replay (`ReplayMob`), and the turn scheduler, so scope it as **its own milestone** decoupled from the (deferred) necro trees. The Bonecaller `dmgPct` proxies are irrelevant for now (necro deferred); the engine work is justified by enemy-side use alone.

### I. Resource economy (necro battle-rez deferred with necro)
- **Resource flags** (active): no-decay on a per-spec resource (Berserker Crimson Crescendo), resource-cost-to-heal inversion (Berserker Crimson Tithe), consume-stacks-for-burst (Pyromancer execute-refund).
- **Pre-placed battle-rez** (Bonecaller The Last Vigil) — **deferred with necro.**

### J. Direct-damage & misc primitives
- **repeatHit / echo rider** on an AoE ability (Archer Culling Storm — second 50% all-enemy hit).
- Pyromancer contagion-spread is adjacent in spirit.

**Suggested build order:** §A (keystone, unblocks all 10) → §B (`abilityOverride`, enumerated) → §C+§E (status authoring + event riders — the bulk of the DPS/healer trees) → §D (atonement) → §F (tank tools) → §H (summon engine, isolated milestone) → §J (tail primitives). **§G is now largely cut** (general paladin DR needs no tick-tagging). With §A–§E done, ~9 of 10 specs are fully expressible.

## 2. Build-diversity check

The framework — Tier 1 engine, Tier 2 shape, Tier 3 fork, Tier 4 keyed-defense, Tier 5 re-key capstone — produces 4 distinct builds per spec where the *mechanics actually diverge*. After the review pass, all **10 active specs** pass: each build changes a *verb* (reflect vs anchor vs collapse vs survival; control vs lifesteal vs peel vs execute; contagion vs detonate vs reach vs tempo; reactive-spike vs off-school vs dispel vs sustain). No false choices spotted in the active roster.

- **Pallbinder's dial-only weakness (the original §2 flag) is moot** — the Necromancer is deferred. When necro returns, push at least one Pallbinder tier to reshape the Wraith/Spark verbs (not just a `dmgPct` dial) before shipping it.
- **Safe-pick variety (Umut's ask) is done:** the repeated "+X% max HP" floor node is gone everywhere it was boilerplate — replaced with spec-flavoured, deliberately-modest options (e.g. Berserker Warbound Cadence = Fury-ramp floor; Pyromancer Banked Heat = focus-stack floor; Arcanist's tempo refund; Lifebinder Quick Bloom = one-charge HoT refresh; Archer Roll Aside = keyed once-per-fight evasive; Mystic Steadying Brew = on-demand brew). No two specs share a safe-pick mechanic now.

## 3. Naming + IP (post-rename)

The register changed from gothic to **bright Warcraft-style class-fantasy** (Umut). The rename was run per-spec and then **machine-audited** for two things:
- **Cross-spec collisions:** 3 true clashes fixed (Cleric "Last Light" → **Final Light** vs Lifebinder's; Assassin "Killing Edge"/Cold-Blood collapse → **Killer's Focus**; "Wildfire" ability vs node → ability **Emberfall**). Deliberate cross-spec motifs were kept and flagged for UI disambiguation if two trees ever render side-by-side: **Crowned in Light** (Cleric — smite-atonement; Lifebinder's nature twin is **Verdant Reckoning**), **Cleansing Litany** (typed-cleanse echo), **Evergreen Renewal** (HoT-persistence echo).
- **WoW-verbatim leakage introduced by the rename:** the pass fixed the originals (Bladestorm→Steel Cyclone, Frost Nova→Glacial Burst, Counterspell→Spellbreak, Divine Shield→Radiant Aegis, Vanish→Fade to Black, Mass Dispel→Cleansing Light, …) but slipped several new verbatim names landing on the analogous class. All were re-scrubbed: **Bloodthirst/Bloodthirsty, First Blood, Hunter's Mark, Overgrowth, Searing Light, Sunfire, Hallowed Insight, Bladestorm Cascade, Lacerate, Carnage, Festering Venom, Rolling Thunder, Berserk Frenzy, Nightfall, Shadow Slip, Wildfire, Crippling Strike** → all replaced with original names. The trees below are clean.

**Pre-existing ability-name IP-debt (NOT introduced here — flag for the IP-scrub task, not this revamp):** several *abilities* the talents reference are still WoW-adjacent and predate this pass — **Rally Cry, Holy Strike, Stalwart Defender, Light's Salvation, Emberstorm, Glacial Cataclysm, Combustion, Meteor/Skyfall** and the existing major names. They live in `abilities-player.json` / `skills.json`.

**Ability-rename map (implementation note):** the talents above already reference the *new* bright spell names (e.g. Hook and Haul, Iron Bulwark, Steel Cyclone, Emberfall, Glacial Burst, Spellbreak, Radiant Aegis, Earthen Sweep, Spirit Tether, Mending Light/Dawnward/Radiance). When the trees are wired, `abilities-player.json` + `skills.json` + the icon manifest must adopt the same names so the doc and data don't diverge. Internal ability **ids** can stay; only display names change.

## 4. Coverage of the choice axes across the roster

Mapping builds to the Phase-P problem axes (P1 interrupt, P2 resist-wall, P3 armour-wall, P4 dispel, P5 spike, P6 drowning-rot, P7 eruption-peel, P8 dangerous-add, P9 anti-heal/purge, P11 reach, P12 soft-enrage):

- **Well-covered (multiple distinct answers):** P3 (Pyromancer, Cleric, Arcanist, Lifebinder, Berserker), P8 (Arcanist, Assassin, Pyromancer, Mystic, Archer, Guardian's new war cry), P11 (Guardian-grip, Assassin-dive, Archer-native, Pyromancer-reach — four distinct mechanical answers), P12 (Berserker, Assassin, Archer, Pyromancer, Mystic), P5 (Cleric, Crusader, Mystic, Lifebinder-partial).
- **P9 anti-heal — no longer single-point (Umut's fix):** now answered by **Berserker (Riving Wound)** *and* **Archer (Serrated Shot)** — a melee and a ranged anti-heal, so a self-healing-boss week no longer hard-walls a Berserker-less group. Lifebinder/(deferred Bonecaller) offer *purge* (buff-strip), a distinct tool. Still worth a coverage check that the scheduler doesn't stack P9 against a comp with neither anti-heal spec.
- **P6 drowning-rot:** Lifebinder (native), Crusader (now a *general* timed party-DR window — still a fine rot answer because rot is "a heavy stretch you press the dome for"), Mystic (Spirit Tether). All tanks/healers — no DPS rot-survival, by design.
- **P4 dispel by type:** Cleric (Magic/Curse + Nature/Poison via Purifying Hymn) and Lifebinder (Nature/Poison) split coverage; a Magic/Curse-heavy week with a Lifebinder-only healer has a gap — worth a coverage-matrix check.
- **P7 eruption-peel:** Crusader (Aegis of the Dawnsworn) + Guardian Sheltering Ward — note P7 itself is currently *deferred* on the roadmap, which is exactly why the old Guardian Tier-3 (built on it) was remade.

Every active Phase-P axis has at least one answer, and the crowded axes (P3, P8, P11, P12) get **mechanically distinct** answers rather than reskins. Residual concentration to watch: dispel-type coverage (two specs splitting four types).
