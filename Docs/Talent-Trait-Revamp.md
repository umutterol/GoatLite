# GOAT Lite — Talent & Trait Revamp (design report)

**Status:** design ✅ (2026-06-22), execution deferred. Feeds roadmap **C.2** (per-spec talent trees). Companions: `ROADMAP.md`, `Class-Tools-System.md` (spec identities), `MMO-Nostalgia-Reference.md` (Necromancer proposal + naming voice).

> Generated via a multi-agent investigation: WoW (retail Dragonflight/War Within + Classic 51-point) talent-design research → per-spec dossier → **12 parallel tree designs** → cross-cut synthesis. Goal: talents that are **cool and actually matter** — every tier a real playstyle fork, not a stat stick.

## Design contract
- **12 specs**, each **15 nodes** = **5 tiers of 3 mutually-exclusive choices** (pick 1 of 3 per tier).
- Each tier is a real **playstyle fork** — AoE↔single-target · survival/defensive↔output · self-sustain↔raw-damage · burst↔sustained · control/utility↔damage · **lean-into↔branch-from the spec's class-tool**. The 3 options are power-equal and win in *different* content; "+5% vs +10%" is banned.
- **Earnest in-world gothic names** (the satire stays in the meta layer — never a spell name).
- **Balance-aware:** the combat regime is going **timer-bound** (P.5c), so talents favour **active throughput / utility / typed-or-conditional defensives** over generic flat survival (the anti-generic-EHP rule the C.11 audit demanded).
- Covers the **10 current specs + the planned Necromancer** (Bonecaller DPS / Pallbinder healer).
- Every node is tagged with its **engine effect** — existing vocab (`maxHpPct`/`dmgPct`/`intakePct`/`critPct`/`onlyIf`) or a **NEW** hook. The consolidated NEW-hook list (the engine work this revamp implies) is in the **synthesis** at the end.

> **Sequencing:** this revamp is done **before** P.5c (the regime tuning) — talents are sim power inputs, so the global balance is calibrated once, against the final trees (see the Open-Design-Decisions resolution in `ROADMAP.md`).

## WoW talent-design patterns (the inspiration)

A field guide for porting WoW's talent design into the drowned-barrow auto-battler. Every example name below is gothic/in-world and re-skinnable; the mechanics map to the engine's real fields (`damageType` Physical/Magic, dispel typing Magic/Curse vs Nature/Poison, per-spec resources like Rampage/Burn/Chill/Poison/HoT-stacks, bands front/back, `onlyIf` conditions, `cooldownTurns`). The satire stays in the QA wrapper — never in a spell name.

---

### 1. What makes a talent MEANINGFUL (not a stat stick)

A talent earns its slot when picking it **changes a decision you make during the run**, not just a number on a sheet. The four tests:

1. **It changes behaviour, not just output.** "+8% damage" is invisible at the table; "+22% damage *while 3+ enemies are up*" makes you read the pull. The engine already does this with `onlyIf` (`enemiesAtLeast`, `targetHpBelowPct`, `targetBand`). The *condition* is the design — the percentage is just tuning. (This is exactly the Dragonflight pivot: the revamp's stated goal was **player agency over build + meaningful rewards**, achieved by gating power behind situational triggers and choice nodes rather than flat passives.)

2. **It has an opportunity cost.** WoW's octagonal **choice nodes** give you *one of two* — you cannot have both. Taking the cleave answer means giving up the single-target answer. A talent with no cost (a free passive everyone takes) is not a choice, it's a tax. Your `talents.json` already encodes this shape: each node is a 2–3 option pick where the `"default": true` "balanced" middle option is the *deliberately weaker safe pick* you trade up from.

3. **It expresses an identity / fantasy.** Classic's 51-point capstones (Mortal Strike, Bloodthirst, Shield Slam, Shadowform) were **playstyle-defining**: reaching the bottom of a tree changed *how the class plays*, and the cost was the rest of the tree. The capstone is the punchline of a whole build. Re-skin this as a deep-commitment final node that re-keys a spec's rotation (e.g. a Pyromancer node that turns Burn from DoT into burst).

4. **It interacts with the OTHER systems you already have.** The best WoW talents reach sideways — Convection (cheaper Lightning), or talents that make your dispel also buff, your interrupt also slow. Your `mass-dispel` already does this (cleansed allies deal +10%). A meaningful talent in this game should hook the **problem taxonomy** (P1–P12): it should improve how a spec answers its dungeon problem, not just pad EHP — because the C.11 audit proved **generic EHP was the disease**.

The litmus test, in one line: *if every viable build takes it, it's not a talent — it's base kit.* (Fix: either bake it in, or give it a real rival on the same node.)

---

### 2. The recurring meaningful-choice ARCHETYPES (this-or-that shapes)

These are the reusable "shapes" — the skeleton of a choice node. Pick a shape, then re-skin the names per spec. Each is a genuine trade with no dominant pick across all content:

- **Cleave vs. Execute** — wide damage when 3+ are up *vs.* amplified damage on a low-HP target. (Trash week vs. boss week.) Engine: `enemiesAtLeast:3` vs `targetHpBelowPct`.
- **Single-target vs. AoE** — your `node-2` (Focused Strike vs. Cleaving Blow). The purest dial.
- **Proactive-absorb vs. reactive-burst** — pre-shield the predicted hit *vs.* top the spike *after* it lands. This is the **Crusader vs. Cleric P5 read** as a talent: prevention vs. reaction.
- **Sustained tempo vs. burst spike** — steady always-on output *vs.* a windowed cooldown that front-loads. (Your `node-3` Steady Hand vs. Overload.)
- **Survival vs. throughput** — +max HP *vs.* conditional damage (your `node-1`). The eternal tank/DPS lever.
- **Mobility/reach vs. control** — gain a reach/dive primitive *vs.* gain a CC. (Maps to P11 reach vs. P8 lock.)
- **Single-target reliability vs. spread/contagion** — apply a strong DoT to one *vs.* spread a weaker DoT to many. (Pyromancer Conflagration shape.)
- **Duration vs. magnitude** — a longer, gentler effect *vs.* a short, intense one. (HoT that outlasts the drowning tick vs. a burst bloom — the **Lifebinder P6 vs. P5** identity.)
- **Resource generation vs. resource spending** — build the stack faster *vs.* make spends hit harder. (Rampage / Burn / Poison economies.)
- **Typed-tool specialisation** — your dispel/shield/school becomes better *but narrower* (Magic-only vs. Nature-only; Physical-school vs. Magic-school). Turns "a cleanse" into "the *right* cleanse" — the anti-generic-EHP weapon.
- **Self vs. party** — a buff that only helps you *vs.* a weaker version that helps the group. (The Archer recut deliberately took the self-only side of this; a talent could offer the party side back at a cost.)
- **Conditional spike vs. unconditional floor** — big payoff *if a condition holds* vs. a smaller guaranteed amount. (The "gamble vs. safe" node — your `"default"` middle options are the safe floor.)
- **On-kill / on-crit chain vs. steady value** — snowball mechanics (reset a cooldown on kill/crit, à la Massacre→Blood Frenzy, Shadow Step→Camouflage) *vs.* a flat reliable bonus. Rewards execution and reading the fight.

### 3. The ANTI-PATTERNS to avoid

These are the failure modes WoW spent two decades fixing — and the ones your own C.11 audit flagged. Treat each as a hard "no":

- **The boring passive %-node.** "+5% damage / +5% HP" with no condition. Invisible, mandatory, un-fun. *Fix:* attach a trigger (`onlyIf`) so the number teaches a read, or delete it and bake it into base stats.
- **The mandatory pick (a tax, not a choice).** A node where one option is so far ahead that 100% of builds take it. The other options are decoration. *This is the most common sin.* *Fix:* if a node has an obvious answer, it isn't a node — either remove the losers or buff them into genuine rivals.
- **The false choice (illusory branch).** Two options that do almost the same thing, or one strictly dominates the other in all content. The player *feels* like they chose but didn't. *Fix:* the two sides must win in *different content* (the cleave side wins trash weeks, the execute side wins boss weeks).
- **Generic-EHP creep — the cardinal sin here.** The C.11 audit proved stacked party-DR / shields / off-heals scaled survivability against *everything*, so "bring more survival" beat "bring the counter." A talent that adds undifferentiated EHP (flat party DR, a bigger generic shield) is the disease. *Fix:* any defensive talent must be **typed or keyed** — rot-only DR, immunity to *one* event, a shield that only stops the *predicted* hit. Useless against the wrong problem by construction.
- **The trap option.** A talent that looks exciting but is mathematically never correct. Wastes the player's attention and erodes trust. *Fix:* if it's never right, it's a bug — cut it or fix it.
- **Build-locking complexity / mandatory long chains.** Classic's 31-point-deep prerequisite chains forced cookie-cutter routes. A talent buried behind five filler nodes isn't a choice, it's a toll road. *Fix:* keep prerequisite depth shallow; let capstones be reachable by more than one path.
- **The "do my whole rotation for me" passive.** Removes decisions instead of adding them. *Fix:* talents should add a decision (a new condition to watch), not automate one away.
- **Power without identity.** A raw throughput cooldown that any spec could have. *Fix:* tie every notable talent to the spec's signature verb (grip, cancel, anti-heal, HoT-coverage) so the choice reinforces *what this class is for*.

### 4. Iconic talent IDEAS by role (re-skinned to the drowned-barrow)

Each entry: an in-world name + the WoW pattern it borrows + the engine hook + the choice it creates. Names are earnest gothic, matched to the established voice.

#### TANK (Guardian / Crusader / Mystic)

- **Grave-Anchor** *(Drag-to-the-Dust upgrade — reach/threat).* Your pull also roots the dragged caster for 1 turn. *Choice vs.* **Iron Vigil**: instead, your taunt also grants the gripped target's nearest squishy −15% damage-taken. (Reposition-offence vs. peel-defence — the P11/P7 fork.)
- **Carrion Wall** *(Shield Wall reflect-build).* Your block-wall reflects +50% as Physical. *Choice vs.* **Stillwater Aegis**: the wall instead grants 1 charge of full immunity to a flagged ally (P7 negate). (Thorns vs. transfer.)
- **The Mountain Holds** *(Mystic avoidance capstone — Unmoving Mountain line).* Each parry has a chance to reset Leg Sweep. *Choice vs.* **Tidebound Calm**: parries instead heal the lowest ally (lifesteal-as-group-sustain, the P6 peel side). (Selfish uptime vs. group sustain — never both.)
- **Barrow-Keeper's Burden** *(deep capstone, identity-flipping).* While you hold hard threat, the back band cannot be targeted at all — but your own damage is halved. The wall-or-warden commitment node.

#### HEALER (Cleric / Lifebinder)

- **Lantern in the Dark** *(reactive-burst identity — Cleric P5).* Your instant heal on a target below 30% also leaves an absorb equal to 50% overheal. *Choice vs.* **Vigil's Patience**: instead, your heals on full-HP allies pre-load a small shield (proactive). (Reactive triage vs. proactive prevention — the P5 shape as one node.)
- **Unbinding Litany** *(typed-dispel specialisation — anti-generic).* Your cleanse strips +1 debuff and grants the cleansed ally +10% power *but only removes Nature/Poison* (Lifebinder). The Cleric mirror, **Censer's Word**, does the same for Magic/Curse. (Forces the *right* dispel — P4. Re-skin of your live `mass-dispel`/`unbinding-word` split.)
- **Drowned-Root Renewal** *(duration-vs-magnitude — Lifebinder P6).* Your rolling HoT gains +1 turn and outlasts the drowning tick, *but cannot crit*. *Choice vs.* **Sudden Bloom**: HoTs bloom for a burst on expiry but are short (loses to a sustained tick). (The P6-coverage vs. P5-spike identity baked into one octagon — the design says Lifebinder must be *weak at the spike by choice*.)
- **Crowned in Ash** *(deep healer capstone, smite-atonement).* While any ally is below 30%, your Magic damage heals the party for 50% — turning the healer into an off-school answer to the armour wall (P3) at the cost of pure throughput.

#### MELEE DPS (Berserker / Assassin)

- **Grave-Wound** *(Mortal Strike — the iconic healing-reduction execute; already named in the design).* Your strike applies −50% incoming healing for 2 turns. *Choice vs.* **Reaving Sweep**: instead, hit 2 adjacent for full damage (anti-heal single-target vs. cleave — the P9 vs. P2 fork; Grave-Wound is the **only** answer to a self-healing boss).
- **Coldiron Patience** *(Cold Blood crit-chain).* After a crit, your next finisher is a guaranteed crit. *Choice vs.* **Marrow-Thirst**: crits instead heal you 3% max HP (snowball burst vs. self-sustain).
- **Barrow-Step** *(reach/dive primitive — Shadowstep, already in the voice).* Your opener can reach the back band and deals +30% there. *Choice vs.* **Throat-Slitter**: instead, +100% vs. targets below 20% (reach vs. raw execute — P11 vs. P12).
- **Rampant Slaughter** *(deep capstone, resource-flip).* At max Rampage, your execute resets its own cooldown on kill (the Massacre→snowball loop) — converts the spec into a chain-killing soft-enrage answer (P12).

#### RANGED PHYSICAL DPS (Archer)

- **Quarrel-Mark** *(armor-pen / debuff-amp — Mark identity).* Your Mark also makes the target take +30% from *your* shots specifically (selfish focus-fire). *Choice vs.* **Hunter's Largesse**: the Mark instead amps the *whole party's* damage on that target by +15% (the self-vs-party node — the Archer deliberately defaults selfish, this buys back the support side at a personal-DPS cost).
- **Over-the-Barrow Volley** *(reach specialisation — P11).* Your arcing shot can pick off the sheltered caster and hits 2 in the back band. *Choice vs.* **Leaden Carillon**: instead, a heavier single aimed shot with +50% execute (back-line reach vs. single-target ceiling).
- **Cold Focus** *(burst-window cooldown — already in the kit).* Your aim window also grants +20% crit damage. *Choice vs.* **Unerring Patience**: steady passive +8% crit/power with no window (spike vs. tempo — the P12 enrage fork).
- **The Last Quarrel** *(deep capstone).* Below 35% target HP your basic shot fires twice — a pure execute-phase identity for the soft-enrage race.

#### CASTER DPS (Pyromancer / Arcanist)

- **Pyre-Vents** *(Pyromancer detonate-build — burst identity).* Detonating 4+ Burn stacks grants +25% power (your live Combustion) AND stuns the target if it had Burn — turning the detonation into a P1 *delay* / P8 *lock*. *Choice vs.* **Creeping Embers**: Burn instead spreads to 2 adjacent on every cast (burst-detonation vs. contagion-AoE — the P1/P8 vs. P3 armour-wall fork).
- **Plaguebloom** *(spread/contagion specialisation — Conflagration line).* Your spread carries *all* stacks and refreshes duration. *Choice vs.* **Smothering Ash**: instead, each stack ticks +20% harder but cannot spread (wide vs. tall DoT).
- **Unbinding Word of Frost** *(interrupt/lockdown — Arcanist P1 identity; Counterspell pattern).* Your interrupt also adds +2 turns to the target's next cast (your live Counterspell) AND chills it. *Choice vs.* **Leaden Silence**: instead, your silence becomes mass (all enemies) but shorter — the P1-cancel vs. P8-lock fork, the cleanest single-target kick vs. the add-wave lockdown.
- **Cold Focus / Frostbound Capstone — Bell-Warden** *(deep control capstone).* Your first CC each fight lasts +1 turn and, while a target is frozen/silenced, your Magic damage to it is +30% — rewarding the lockdown identity with a damage payoff (P1+P8 into P3).

# The 12 talent trees

## Guardian — Tank (warrior)

*The immovable barrow-warden. Holds hard threat so the back-line is never free-cast on, drags the sheltered caster into the dirt, and steps avoidable death off his allies — a punishing reactive bruiser who turns being hit into damage and reflect.*

### Tier 1 — The Reactive Engine (what powers the bruiser loop: vengeance, thorns, or a heavier anchor swing)
- **Vengeful Marrow** — Each time an enemy strikes you, your next Revenge gains +35% damage (stacking up to 3). The more the pull beats on you, the harder you answer. *Tradeoff:* serves the reactive-bruiser / single-target build — your output is gated behind *being hit*, so it goes quiet when the healer is keeping you topped or the pull is small. `NEW: on-self-hit, grant a stacking outgoing-dmg buff to the next Revenge cast (self-hit-counted dmg stacks).`
- **Barrow-Thorns** — Your Thorns Aura reflects to *every* melee attacker in the front band, not just the one who hit you, and the reflect scales +50%. *Tradeoff:* the AoE / passive-cleave answer — turns a packed pull into free damage with zero buttons, but does nothing on a single boss and never spikes. `NEW: widen thorns-reflect to all front-band melee attackers + reflectPct scalar (multi-target thorns).`
- **Grave-Iron Resolve** — +18% damage while 2 or fewer enemies remain; baked into your basic swing, no trigger to manage. *Tradeoff:* the unconditional single-target floor — reliable boss-week throughput that asks nothing of you, but it can't snowball off incoming hits like Vengeful Marrow or sweep a pack like Barrow-Thorns. `dmgPct: 18, onlyIf: { type: "enemiesAtMost", value: 2 }`

### Tier 2 — The Grip Fork (lean into Drag to the Dust → P11, or trade the reach for a brutal punish on whatever you've already caught)
- **Grave-Anchor** — When you grip a sheltered back-line caster to the front (Drag to the Dust), the target is also rooted for 1 turn, unable to retreat or be re-sheltered. *Tradeoff:* the dedicated P11 answer — locks the dragged caster in your face so the melee can delete it, but spends your whole identity on reach and gives no help against a telegraph or a hard hit. `NEW: grip also applies a 1-turn root (control: stun-equivalent, no-reposition) to the gripped target.`
- **Chain of the Drowned** — Drag to the Dust now hauls *two* back-line enemies forward at once instead of one. *Tradeoff:* the AoE-reach build — collapses an entire sheltered back line into melee range for a cleave kill, but the grip no longer roots, so a single priority caster can simply walk back out. `NEW: grip target count 1 → 2 (multi-target grip).`
- **Toll for the Dragged** — Skip the reach entirely: instead of gripping, your next strike on any target already in melee range deals +28% and applies a Bleed. *Tradeoff:* the punisher who gives up the signature tool — pure front-line damage for fights with no sheltered caster, but you have *no answer to P11* and the back line free-casts. `NEW: convert the grip slot into a single-target dmg+Bleed strike (on-demand bleed strike, no reposition).`

### Tier 3 — The Intercept Fork (lean into Barrow-Step → P7, or harden yourself instead of saving others)
- **Barrow-Step** — Your intercept (Barrow-Step) relocates the flagged ally *and* grants them 1 charge of full immunity to the next eruption tick. *Tradeoff:* the dedicated P7 answer — guarantees the squishy survives the telegraph even if the relocation timing is tight, but it's a single-target save that does nothing for the rest of the group. `NEW: intercept also grants the relocated ally a 1-charge immunity to the next damage event (single-event immunity charge).`
- **Warden's Shadow** — Barrow-Step instead pulls the flagged ally *behind you*, and while they shelter there they take −20% damage for 2 turns. *Tradeoff:* the peel build — a softer, longer cover that helps against sustained back-line pressure (P11 spillover), but it only *reduces* the eruption instead of negating it, so a one-shot telegraph still kills. `NEW: intercept repositions ally to self's shadow + applies a short single-ally typed DR (peel-DR buff).`
- **Tide-Bitten Skin** — Forgo saving allies: while you have taken a hit since your last action, you take −25% from the next enemy ability. *Tradeoff:* the selfish-survival build — active, conditional mitigation that keeps *you* standing through a personal spike, but the rest of the party eats their own telegraphs (no P7 cover). `NEW: conditional self-intake reduction keyed to "hit since last action" (reactive self-DR window).`

### Tier 4 — Active Mitigation (how the wall holds: reflect the spike, share it, or pre-empt it — never just flat DR)
- **Carrion Wall** — Shield Wall now reflects the blocked damage back at +90% as Physical, turning your defensive cooldown into a burst window. *Tradeoff:* the thorns / reactive-damage build — the more they hit the wall the more they bleed, but it only pays out *while you're being focused*, and offers the party nothing. `NEW: raise Shield Wall reflectPct and route reflected damage to the attacker as a damage event (reflect-on-block scalar).`
- **Stillwater Aegis** — Shield Wall instead converts into a single charge of full immunity you can hand to a flagged ally before a known hit. *Tradeoff:* the proactive-prevention / utility build — the cleanest answer to a predicted one-shot on the back-line (pairs with the intercept builds), but you lose all the personal reflect and self-block. `NEW: Shield Wall becomes a transferable single-event immunity granted to one ally (immunity transfer).`
- **Drowned Footing** — When an enemy ability would hit you while you are *not* at full HP, you take −22% from it — active because you must let yourself drop below full to arm it. *Tradeoff:* the self-sustain bruiser's floor — conditional personal DR that smooths every incoming spike once the fight is rolling, but it's selfish and does nothing at full HP or for allies. `NEW: conditional self-intake reduction keyed to "self below max HP" (below-full self-DR).`

### Tier 5 — The Warden's Oath (the deep identity capstone: total threat-lock, lifesteal-anchor, or a soft-enrage cleave finisher)
- **Barrow-Keeper's Burden** — While you hold hard threat (Challenge active), the back band cannot be targeted at all — but your own outgoing damage is halved. *Tradeoff:* the absolute threat-anchor build — the wall-or-warden commitment that makes the back-line untouchable (the ultimate answer to free-cast pressure), paid for with your own damage; useless on fights where the threat is already on you. `NEW: while self has hard-taunt up, set the entire back band untargetable + self dmgPct −50% (threat-lock untargetable aura).`
- **The Tide Repays** — Each time you are struck, heal the lowest-HP ally for a share of the blow; while you hold threat this makes you the group's sustain anchor. *Tradeoff:* the self-sustain / group-bruiser build — converts the punishment you soak into party healing (eases a healer-light comp), but every point you give back is a point you didn't put into reflect or damage. `NEW: on-self-hit, heal the lowest-HP ally for a fraction of the damage taken (damage-to-ally-lifesteal).`
- **The Mountain Falls** — +24% damage while 3 or more enemies are up, and your kills refresh the grip so you can immediately drag the next sheltered caster — a chain-collapse finisher for trash weeks. *Tradeoff:* the AoE-cleave capstone — turns the Guardian into a pack-shredding threat machine that snowballs through a wide pull, but it does nothing against a single boss and abandons the personal-survival capstones. `dmgPct: 24, onlyIf: { type: "enemiesAtLeast", value: 3 }` `NEW: on-kill, reset the grip cooldown (kill-resets-grip chain).`

**Builds:** (1) **Reactive Reflect-Bruiser** — Vengeful Marrow → Toll for the Dragged → Tide-Bitten Skin → Carrion Wall → The Tide Repays: single-target punisher that turns being hit into damage and self-sustain; (2) **Threat-Anchor Warden** — Grave-Iron Resolve → Grave-Anchor → Barrow-Step → Stillwater Aegis → Barrow-Keeper's Burden: the no-free-cast lockdown that drags, immunizes, and seals off the back-line; (3) **AoE Collapse Guardian** — Barrow-Thorns → Chain of the Drowned → Warden's Shadow → Carrion Wall → The Mountain Falls: trash-week pack-shredder that hauls back-lines into a thorns-and-cleave grinder; (4) **Selfish Survival Wall** — Grave-Iron Resolve → Toll for the Dragged → Tide-Bitten Skin → Drowned Footing → The Tide Repays: the unkillable solo anchor for healer-light comps.

## Berserker — DPS (Warrior)

*The physical executioner — the only anti-heal in the game. Pay your own blood to snowball Rampage, open wounds nothing can close, and delete the dying.*

### Tier 1 — The Price of Wrath (how you pay for power)
The Berserker's core verb is self-damage-as-fuel. This tier decides what that blood buys.
- **Marrow-Spendthrift** — Your Rampage stacks hit harder the lower your own HP: while you are below 40% HP, +18% damage. *Tradeoff:* the snowball/burst payoff for a player who lets Blood Frenzy and Reckless Frenzy bleed them low and rides the edge; gives up the safety floor entirely (you are squishiest exactly when this is live). `NEW: onlyIf condition selfHpBelowPct gating dmgPct (the engine's onlyIf currently keys only targetHpBelow / enemiesAtLeast / enemiesAtMost — a self-HP gate is new). +18% dmgPct under selfHpBelowPct:40.`
- **Clotted Vigor** — Your crit-lifesteal is doubled (crits heal 6% max HP instead of 3%) and you gain +12% max HP. *Tradeoff:* the self-sustain bruiser floor — turns the spec into a fight-forever attrition build that needs no healer babysitting; gives up the raw damage of its siblings for a fatter, self-topping body. `maxHpPct:12 (FREE) + NEW: scalar override on Bloodlust crit-lifesteal healHpPct 3→6.`
- **Hate-Forged Edge** — Flat, unconditional +10% damage and +5% crit. *Tradeoff:* the deliberately-safe balanced pick — always-on output with no HP gamble and no condition to read; the floor you trade up from, weaker than either specialist in its own lane. `dmgPct:10 + critPct:5 (FREE).`

### Tier 2 — The Shape of the Swing (where your damage lands)
The eternal cleave-vs-single-target dial, tuned to the Berserker's Bladestorm/Whirlwind AoE vs Massacre execute.
- **Reaving Sweep** — +24% damage while 3 or more enemies are up. *Tradeoff:* the trash-week AoE build's backbone, multiplying Bladestorm/Whirlwind into packs; dead weight on a lone boss. `dmgPct:24, onlyIf enemiesAtLeast:3 (FREE).`
- **Throat-Finder** — +22% damage while 2 or fewer enemies remain. *Tradeoff:* the single-target / boss-week answer that fattens every hit during a duel; near-useless mid-pull when the room is full. `dmgPct:22, onlyIf enemiesAtMost:2 (FREE).`
- **Even Temper** — +11% damage, always. *Tradeoff:* the safe generalist that never whiffs on the week's content but never spikes; you give up the high ceiling of committing to one shape. `dmgPct:11 (FREE).`

### Tier 3 — The Wound That Will Not Close (lean into the class-tool, or branch out)
This is the signature fork: do you become *the* anti-heal, or specialize your snowball elsewhere? Grave-Wound is the only answer to a self-healing boss.
- **Grave-Wound** — Your Massacre (and Bladestorm at 5 Rampage) brands the target with −50% incoming healing for 2 turns. *Tradeoff:* the P9 solver and the spec's identity capstone-early — the ONLY tool that beats a self-healing/empowered boss no purge can touch, and a top answer to P2 Magic-Resist Walls and P12 soft-enrage; gives up pure personal throughput for a fight-defining debuff your party can't get anywhere else. `NEW: talent grants an enemy-debuff rider (apply 'mortal-wound' status, -50% incoming healing) onto a flagged ability — no debuff-on-enemy / healingReduction field exists in TalentOptionSchema today; this is an ability-style effect promoted into a talent.`
- **Sundered Marrow** — When you apply or extend a Bleed, it ticks for +35% and lasts +1 turn. *Tradeoff:* the bleed-DoT build — turns Bladestorm's pack-wide Bleed and Whirlwind's extensions into the primary damage source for sustained AoE attrition; gives up the anti-heal entirely (you cannot answer a self-healing boss). `NEW: talent modifier on the 'bleed' status — +35% tick magnitude and +1 base duration applied at status-application time (no per-status tuning channel in talents today).`
- **Hungering Rampage** — +20% damage while your focus target is below 30% HP, and Massacre's kill no longer resets only Blood Frenzy — it also refunds 2 Rampage. *Tradeoff:* the execute-snowball build that branches AWAY from the anti-heal to chase the chain-kill loop; pure greed that does nothing against a healing boss or before targets drop low. `dmgPct:20, onlyIf targetHpBelowPct:30 (FREE) + NEW: on-kill Rampage refund rider (+2 stacks) added to Massacre's reset-on-kill handler.`

### Tier 4 — Surviving the Bleed-Out (active/conditional defense, never flat DR)
The Berserker pays HP to fight; this tier decides how it keeps from paying with its life — all keyed or active, none generic flat DR.
- **Second Wind** — While below 35% HP, you take −20% damage. *Tradeoff:* a conditional safety net that only arms when the self-damage build is at the cliff edge — exactly the Marrow-Spendthrift partner; does nothing while healthy, so it buys zero ceiling for a topped-up Berserker. `NEW: conditional intakePct gated by selfHpBelowPct:35 (intakePct is currently applied unconditionally — gating it by self-HP is new).`
- **Sanguine Tithe** — Your Rampage spends (Blood Frenzy, Reckless Frenzy) heal you for 8% max HP instead of damaging you. *Tradeoff:* the self-sustain bruiser's engine — flips the spec's blood-cost into blood-gain so it can spam its ramps risk-free; gives up the low-HP damage spikes the other builds weaponize (you're never low on purpose). `NEW: invert the hpPct resourceCost on Rampage-spending abilities into a heal (no resource/heal-on-spend channel in talents).`
- **Reckless Disregard** — Your major Reckless Frenzy lasts +1 turn and grants +8% crit, but its HP cost rises to 15%. *Tradeoff:* the all-in burst build's payoff — a longer, deadlier offensive window at a steeper blood price; pure offense with no defensive value at all (the anti-survival pick on the defense tier). `NEW: per-ability duration/cost override on Reckless Frenzy (+1 turn, +8% crit, cost 10%→15%); critPct on the buff is new as a talent rider.`

### Tier 5 — The Last Stroke (your finisher identity)
The capstone tier — each option re-keys the kill phase for a different build.
- **Rampant Slaughter** — At 4+ Rampage, a Massacre kill resets its OWN cooldown. *Tradeoff:* the chain-killing soft-enrage answer (P12) — strings executes back-to-back across a dying pack, the AoE/execute build's dream; worthless on a single durable boss where nothing dies to reset it. `NEW: self-stack-gated (rampage>=4) reset-cooldown-on-kill rider on Massacre targeting its own cooldown.`
- **Crimson Crescendo** — Each Rampage stack now grants +6% damage (up from Bloodlust's +4%) and your stacks no longer decay between casts. *Tradeoff:* the sustained snowball ceiling — rewards a player who never drops the rhythm with the highest steady output in the spec; gives up the kill-phase explosiveness of its siblings for a fat, ever-climbing baseline. `NEW: scalar override on Bloodlust rampage-damage-bonus perStackPct 4→6 + a no-decay flag on the rampage resource.`
- **Headsman's Certainty** — Massacre's execute threshold rises to 35% HP and its below-threshold multiplier climbs from x2 to x2.5. *Tradeoff:* the pure single-target executioner — deletes bosses far earlier and harder in the kill window, the Throat-Finder/Grave-Wound boss build's finisher; offers nothing in AoE and nothing until the target is dying. `NEW: per-ability override on Massacre's execute modifier (threshold 20→35, multiplier 2→2.5).`

**Builds:** *Bloodbound Executioner* (Throat-Finder + Grave-Wound + Headsman's Certainty — the boss-deleting anti-heal that solves P9/P2/P12); *Bleeding Reaper* (Reaving Sweep + Sundered Marrow + Rampant Slaughter — Bleed-fueled AoE trash-clear and chain-kills); *Edge-Walker* (Marrow-Spendthrift + Reckless Disregard + Second Wind — low-HP all-in burst gambler); *Undying Bruiser* (Clotted Vigor + Sanguine Tithe + Crimson Crescendo — self-topping attrition body that needs no healer).

## Crusader — Tank (paladin)

*The transferable-bubble warden: a holy front-liner who wins by spending a single, perfectly-timed negation on the right ally at the right beat. Every build is a different answer to "which incoming event do I refuse to let happen?"*

### Tier 1 — The Oath's Reach (who the bubble is FOR)
The Crusader's identity question first: does your protection follow the most fragile ally, the focused ally, or come back to harden yourself?

- **Ward of the Hollow Choir** — While Blessing of Protection / Sacred Bastion is active on an ally, that ally's incoming damage is also reduced -18% for the same window (a fraying "afterglow" once the hard negate is spent). *Tradeoff:* serves the **predictive-peel build** — your single-target bubble now also softens the aftermath, the best answer to a one-target-at-a-time spike chain (P5/P7); gives up the self-survival and party-wide options entirely. `NEW: when a single-ally peel/immunity status is active on a target, apply a typed intakePct -18% to that same target for the status duration (peel-linked intake debuff, not a flat aura).`
- **Grave-Iron Vigil** — Holy Strike's +5% armour buff is doubled to +10%, lasts 2 turns instead of 1, and stacks up to 2 — your basic attack now maintains a small rolling DR on whichever ally is taking the heat. *Tradeoff:* the **active-uptime build** — you babysit one ally with your auto-rhythm instead of a big cooldown; gives up the burst-negate amplification and the self-wall. `NEW: extend buff-nearest-ally mechanic to amountPct 10, durationTurns 2, maxStacks 2 (armour buff that rolls off the basic).`
- **The Barrow Holds** — Stalwart Defender's passive armour also grants you -10% damage taken while you hold a hard taunt; drop threat and it lapses. *Tradeoff:* the **selfish-wall build** — turns you into a conventional unkillable anchor *as long as you're actively tanking*, but spends nothing on the party and dies the instant you're not the boss's target. `NEW: conditional intakePct -10% gated on selfHasStatus 'hard-taunt-active' (self-only, threat-keyed — not a flat passive).`

### Tier 2 — Against the Drowning (the rot answer vs the burst answer)
The cardinal anti-generic-EHP fork: a rot-keyed party-DR that is *useless against bursts*, versus a sharper single-event tool that is *useless against rot*.

- **Tide-Mantle Litany** — Rally Cry additionally grants the party -12% damage taken from *damage-over-time and recurring tick effects only* for its window (the second real P6 Drowning-Tick answer, so rot weeks aren't Lifebinder-mandatory). *Tradeoff:* the **rot-week / Drowning-Tick build** — the only thing here that survives a sustained-tick week without a Lifebinder; by construction does *nothing* against a single big hit or an Eruption. `NEW: typed intakePct -12% that applies ONLY to incoming DoT/recurring-tick damage (rot-keyed party aura on Rally Cry), zero effect on burst/normal hits.`
- **Stillwater Aegis** — Sacred Bastion gains a second charge but each charge is single-ally and the cooldown lengthens; you can negate two separate predicted events per its window instead of one big one. *Tradeoff:* the **multi-spike build** — two surgical immunity-peels for a fight with two scary beats (P5 On-the-Hour Spike + a second mechanic); gives up rot coverage and any throughput. `NEW: Sacred Bastion → 2 charges, single-ally immunity-peel each, longer cooldown (charge-based negate cooldown).`
- **Censer-Light Cadence** — Rally Cry's +15% power becomes +20% and also grants the party +6% crit for the window. *Tradeoff:* the **throughput / timer-race build** — you lean *away* from the bubble fantasy to make the party kill faster, the pick when the dungeon is timer-bound rather than survival-bound; gives up both the rot DR and the spike tools. `dmgPct +5 on the existing party power buff plus critPct +6 to the party for the buff window (party offensive cooldown).`

### Tier 3 — The Shield's Discipline (how Divine Shield behaves)
Divine Shield is being gated to one charge per predictable window — this tier decides what that charge *means*.

- **Drowned-Root Bulwark** — Divine Shield's absorb is +50% larger and, while it holds, that ally cannot be critically struck. *Tradeoff:* the **anti-spike-shield build** — the surest pre-block for a known big hit (the proactive side of the P5 prevention-vs-reaction read); a fat single shield that does nothing once it pops, useless against a stream of small ticks. `NEW: shield base/scale +50% and a "no-crit-taken while shielded" flag on the shield status.`
- **Spreading Benediction** — Divine Shield splits across the **two lowest-HP allies** at 60% size each instead of one ally at full size. *Tradeoff:* the **raid-damage / AoE-pressure build** — covers a two-target soak or a spread mechanic where the whole back line is bleeding; loses the ability to fully save one focused ally. `NEW: shield targets the 2 lowest-HP allies at 0.6x magnitude each (split-shield distribution).`
- **Ashen Reprisal** — When Divine Shield expires or is consumed, it detonates for Physical damage to all enemies scaling off the absorbed amount. *Tradeoff:* the **damage-tank / timer build** — turns a defensive cooldown into a threat-and-damage swing, the pick when you must contribute to the kill clock; the shield itself is unchanged (no extra survival), so it's the weakest *defensive* of the three. `NEW: on shield consume/expire, deal Physical AoE = shieldAbsorbed × factor to all enemies (shield-detonation).`

### Tier 4 — The Redirect's Edge (what Guardian's Oath becomes)
Guardian's Oath (100% redirect to self at -20%) is the Crusader's other transfer tool. This tier is the personal-defensive vs. offence-from-defence vs. control fork.

- **Martyr's Forbearance** — While Guardian's Oath is active, the redirected damage you take is reduced an extra -25% (on top of the base -20%) and you cannot be reduced below 1 HP for the duration. *Tradeoff:* the **hard-peel / save-the-ally build** — you can eat a lethal hit *for* an ally and live, the ultimate "I take this so you don't" tool; spends your whole defensive budget on one redirect window and adds no damage. `NEW: while redirect-damage status is active on self, additional intakePct -25% on redirected damage + a death-immunity (min 1 HP) flag for the duration.`
- **Oathbound Reprisal** — While Guardian's Oath is active you deal +30% damage (you're shrugging off blows and swinging back). *Tradeoff:* the **damage-tank build** — converts your peel cooldown into a personal burst window, great on a timer; you take the redirected hits at only the base -20% with no safety net, so a misjudged redirect can kill you. `NEW: dmgPct +30% gated on selfHasStatus 'redirect-active' (conditional self-damage buff while the redirect runs).`
- **Toll of the Warden** — When Guardian's Oath ends, the enemy that dealt the most redirected damage is dazed for 1 turn (you turn the soaked punishment back as a stagger). *Tradeoff:* the **control / mechanic-delay build** — a built-in mini-interrupt/tempo tool to buy the party a beat (P1/P8 flavour); the redirect stays plain -20% with no extra mitigation or damage, so it's the softest pick if you just need to live. `NEW: on redirect-damage expiry, applyStatus 'daze' 1 turn to the enemy that contributed the most redirected damage (on-expiry crowd-control).`

### Tier 5 — Capstone: The Last Bastion (what your MAJOR is FOR)
The deep commitment node — Sacred Bastion (the single-ally immunity-peel) is re-keyed into one of three identities. This is the build's punchline.

- **Aegis of the Drowned Saint** — Sacred Bastion becomes a TRUE immunity: the chosen ally fully negates the next incoming event of any size for the window (a clean, total Eruption-negate / On-the-Hour-Spike refusal), and its cooldown is shortened. *Tradeoff:* the **predictive-negate capstone** — the purest expression of the Crusader fantasy and the definitive P7/P5 answer; it is single-ally and binary, so a missed prediction wastes your whole major and it gives nothing to a rot week. `NEW: Sacred Bastion → single-ally full event-immunity (negate next incoming event entirely) with reduced cooldown (one-charge predictive immunity major).`
- **Vault of the Tide-Bound** — Sacred Bastion instead drops a -40% damage-taken dome over the **whole party** for its window, but ONLY against recurring-tick / DoT damage (the rot disaster button). *Tradeoff:* the **rot-survival capstone** — lets a Crusader hard-carry a Drowning-Tick week solo, the strongest non-Lifebinder rot answer in the game; by construction it is *worthless* against any single burst, wall, or Eruption, so a wrong-week pick is a dead button. `NEW: Sacred Bastion → party-wide typed intakePct -40% applying ONLY to DoT/recurring-tick damage for the duration (rot-keyed party dome major).`
- **Reliquary of Wrath** — Sacred Bastion shields the ally (0.5×maxHp) AND, while that shield holds, the whole party deals +20% damage. *Tradeoff:* the **damage-tank / timer capstone** — one cooldown that both peels *and* swings the kill clock, the pick for a timer-bound clear; the peel is "just" a shield (no immunity, no rot coverage), so it's the weakest *defensive* major and rewards aggression over prediction. `NEW: Sacred Bastion shield (0.5×maxHp single ally) + while that shield is active grant party dmgPct +20% (shield-linked party damage major).`

**Builds:** (1) **Predictive Peeler** — Ward of the Hollow Choir → Stillwater Aegis → Drowned-Root Bulwark → Martyr's Forbearance → Aegis of the Drowned Saint: the definitive single-event-negate Crusader for spike/Eruption weeks (P5/P7). (2) **Tide-Warden / Rot Carry** — Grave-Iron Vigil → Tide-Mantle Litany → Spreading Benediction → Martyr's Forbearance → Vault of the Tide-Bound: the non-Lifebinder Drowning-Tick answer (P6). (3) **Wrath-Anchor / Damage-Tank** — The Barrow Holds → Censer-Light Cadence → Ashen Reprisal → Oathbound Reprisal → Reliquary of Wrath: a selfish, timer-racing tank that contributes to the kill clock. (4) **Warden of Tempo / Control** — Grave-Iron Vigil → Stillwart Aegis → Spreading Benediction → Toll of the Warden → Aegis of the Drowned Saint: a peel-and-stagger build that buys the party beats against mechanics (P1/P8 flavour).

## Cleric — Healer (paladin)

*Identity: the reactive-burst + Magic/Curse-dispel healer who heals by dealing damage (atonement). The instant spike-top — heals the spike AFTER it lands, banks overheal as a shield, and answers the Armour-Wall with off-school Smite. Weak at sustained rot by design; throughput is capped so a second healer buys safety, not ceiling.*

### Tier 1 — The Atonement Shape (how your damage heals)
- **Hand of the Choir** — Smite and Holy Fire heal the lowest-HP ally for +20% of the damage dealt (atonement healPctOfDamage 50→70%), but you cannot atonement-heal the same ally twice in a row — it always rotates to the *next* lowest. *Tradeoff:* serves the **damage-while-healing / off-school** build — biggest passive throughput floor, spread across the party — but loses the ability to pour all your smite-healing into the one person who is actually dying. `NEW: atonement healPctOfDamage override + "rotate target, exclude last-healed ally" targeting flag on atonement-heal/atonement-on-dot.`
- **Pyre-Tithe** — your Burn DoT (Holy Fire) ticks +30% harder and its atonement healing is doubled, but Smite's direct atonement is removed (basic attacks no longer heal). *Tradeoff:* the **DoT-leaning sustain** answer — converts you into a fire-and-forget atonement engine that wants Holy Fire on cooldown, the closest the Cleric gets to rot-coverage — but gives up the instant, every-GCD trickle of Smite healing. `NEW: per-status DoT magnitude multiplier + atonement-on-dot healPctOfDotDamage override + flag to disable atonement-heal on a named ability (smite).`
- **Severing Light** — Smite deals +15% Magic damage but no longer heals at all; instead every Smite reduces Flash Heal's cooldown by 1 turn. *Tradeoff:* the **reactive-spike enabler** — you stop caring about passive trickle and weaponise your filler into faster access to your instant spike heal, leaning hard into the burst identity — but you forfeit all atonement value while topped, so a calm pull heals nothing. `dmgPct: 15` (on smite) `+ NEW: "on-cast of ability X reduce cooldown of ability Y by N turns" hook + disable atonement-heal flag.`

### Tier 2 — The Spike (your signature reactive top)
- **Lantern in the Dark** — Flash Heal cast on an ally below 30% HP leaves an absorb equal to 50% of its overheal AND that absorb cannot be popped by rot/DoT damage — only by direct hits. *Tradeoff:* the **pure reactive-triage** pick — the cleanest answer to On-the-Hour Spike (P5): you let the hit land, then top + bank a typed shield against the *next* big swing — but the shield is useless against a sustained-rot fight and demands you hold Flash Heal for the spike. `NEW: conditional overheal→shield magnitude + shield "immune-to-DoT-damage / direct-hit-only" consumption flag.`
- **Vigil's Patience** — Flash Heal cast on a full-HP ally pre-loads a small proactive shield (overheal→shield works while topped, not just when healing damage). *Tradeoff:* the **prevention / Crusader-mirror** pick — lets the Cleric pre-shield a predicted hit instead of reacting, smoothing predictable damage and covering a little rot — but the shield is smaller than Lantern's reactive bloom and wastes a GCD when nobody is hurt. `NEW: allow overheal-to-shield to generate a shield on full-HP targets (proactive shield, magnitude scaled).`
- **Greater Mending** — Greater Heal's below-30% bonus rises from +50% to +90% and refunds 1 turn of its cooldown if it lands on a target below 30%. *Tradeoff:* the **emergency single-target ceiling** — your hardest non-major top gets much bigger and can chain in a crisis — but it is a slow cooldown heal, not an instant, so it loses the race against a true one-shot spike that Flash Heal would catch. `NEW: per-ability targetHpBelowPct heal multiplier override + "refund N cooldown turns if target below pct" hook.`

### Tier 3 — The Cleanse (your typed dispel niche, P4)
- **Censer's Word** — Mass Dispel strips +1 Magic/Curse debuff (3 total) and its cleansed-ally +damage buff rises to +18% for 3 turns. *Tradeoff:* the **lean-into-the-tool** pick — the definitive Spreading-Curse (P4) answer that turns a heavy dispel into a party-wide offensive swing — but it is still locked to Magic/Curse, so a Nature/Poison fight leaves it dead, and it's a cooldown you must time to the curse wave. `NEW: cleanse count override + cleansed-ally buff amountPct/duration override on a named ability.`
- **Unbinding Litany** — Mass Dispel additionally cleanses 1 Nature/Poison debuff from each ally (covers all four dispel types), but the post-cleanse damage buff is removed. *Tradeoff:* the **generalist insurance** pick — the only way the Cleric touches Nature/Poison, broadening her dispel to cover any debuff wave — but she gives up the signature offensive payoff, becoming a pure janitor instead of a janitor-who-buffs. `NEW: add extra dispelTypes coverage (Nature/Poison) to a named cleanse + remove its conditional buff effect.`
- **Searing Absolution** — Mass Dispel no longer cleanses; instead it deals Magic damage to all enemies scaled on power, and that damage feeds atonement (heals the lowest ally for 50%). *Tradeoff:* the **damage-pivot / armour-wall** pick — converts your utility cooldown into an AoE off-school nuke + group heal, brutal on trash and great vs P3 armour walls — but you throw away your dispel entirely, so a curse fight will overwhelm you. `NEW: replace cleanse with an AoE damage effect (damageType Magic, scaleStat power, pattern all-enemies) that triggers atonement-heal.`

### Tier 4 — Off-School Damage (the Smite-atonement / P3 axis)
- **Crowned in Ash** — while any ally is below 30% HP, your Magic damage heals the whole party for 50% of the damage dealt (atonement goes from single lowest-ally to party-wide during the emergency). *Tradeoff:* the **deep atonement capstone-feel** pick — in a crisis the Cleric becomes an off-school damage-as-group-healing answer to the Armour Wall (P3), enormous AoE healing while you smite — but only when someone is *already* low, so it does nothing for prevention and pure throughput is capped. `NEW: conditional atonement target-mode swap (lowest-ally → party) gated by "any ally below pct"; needs a new onlyIf scope (ally-state, not focus-target).`
- **Wrathful Devotion** — +18% Magic damage while 3 or more enemies are up; with three foes your atonement healing is enough to off-tank rot through pure smiting. *Tradeoff:* the **AoE damage-while-healing** pick — leans on the wide-pull math so Smite spam both clears trash and trickle-heals the party — but the bonus evaporates on a single boss, leaving you a weak solo-target healer. `dmgPct: 18, onlyIf { type: "enemiesAtLeast", value: 3 }` (FREE).
- **Executioner's Litany** — +20% Magic damage to your focus target while it is below 30% HP, and Divine Insight's atonement-amp also applies to *your* damage there. *Tradeoff:* the **single-target execute** pick — turns the kill window into a burst of both damage and the largest atonement heals of the fight, ideal for a boss soft-enrage — but offers nothing on full-HP targets or wide pulls. `dmgPct: 20, onlyIf { type: "targetHpBelowPct", value: 30 }` (FREE) `+ NEW: extend atonement-amp to self-damage under the same condition.`

### Tier 5 — The Major: Light's Salvation Recut (your defining cooldown)
- **Light's Salvation** — a strong instant single-target reactive burst heal (no party-shield half); on a target below 30% HP it heals for +50% and leaves a Lantern-style direct-hit-only absorb. *Tradeoff:* the **purest spike-top** pick — the recut signature: the single biggest instant save in the game for catching the On-the-Hour one-shot (P5) — but it touches only one ally, so a party-wide pulse can still drop two people at once. `NEW: single-target reactive heal with conditional below-30% multiplier + conditional direct-hit-only shield (recut of lights-salvation: remove party-pattern shield, swap to side:ally pattern:single).`
- **Pyreclysm** — Light's Salvation becomes an instant party-wide Magic nuke that feeds full atonement (heals everyone for 50% of damage dealt) and refreshes Burn on every enemy. *Tradeoff:* the **AoE damage-as-healing burst** pick — the Searing/Crowned build's payoff cooldown: a single button that nukes the pack, blankets it in DoT, and bursts the whole party up at once — but it heals *nothing* if there are no enemies in range to smite, so it whiffs on a positioning check. `NEW: major that deals AoE Magic damage (scaleStat power), triggers party-wide atonement-heal, and reapplies a named DoT to all enemies.`
- **Bell of Cleansing Light** — Light's Salvation instantly strips ALL Magic/Curse debuffs from every ally, grants the party +15% damage for 3 turns, and heals each cleansed ally for a moderate burst. *Tradeoff:* the **utility / hard-reset** pick — the dispel-build's panic button that wipes an entire curse wave (P4) and swings the party offensive in one cast — but its raw healing is the smallest of the three majors, so it's wasted if there's no debuff to clear. `NEW: major combining full-coverage cleanse (count = all, dispelTypes Magic/Curse) + per-cleansed-ally heal burst + party power buff for N turns.`

**Builds:** (1) **Reactive Spike-Top** — Severing Light → Lantern in the Dark → Censer's Word → Executioner's Litany → Light's Salvation: the instant single-target save build. (2) **Atonement Off-Healer (P3 armour-wall)** — Hand of the Choir → Greater Mending → Searing Absolution → Crowned in Ash → Pyreclysm: heal the party purely by smiting the pack. (3) **Curse-Janitor / Utility** — Pyre-Tithe → Vigil's Patience → Unbinding Litany → Wrathful Devotion → Bell of Cleansing Light: the dispel-and-prevent generalist for debuff-heavy weeks. (4) **DoT-Sustain hybrid** — Pyre-Tithe → Greater Mending → Censer's Word → Wrathful Devotion → Pyreclysm: the closest the Cleric comes to covering sustained rot.

## Assassin — DPS (rogue)

*The melee priority-delete and the only melee reach — a stealth-and-poison erasing rogue who chains crits and executes one target into the dust.*

### Tier 1 — The Opener (what your stealth strike is FOR)
- **Marrow-Pierce** — Your stealth-empowered opener (Camouflage / Killing Edge follow-up) deals +25% to a target above 80% HP, but nothing extra once it's wounded. *Tradeoff:* the pure burst-window build — front-loads the kill on a fresh priority add (P8) and rewards reading which mob to delete first; gives up the execute carry of Throat-Reaver and the reach of Barrow-Pierce. `NEW: dmgPct gated by targetHpAbovePct (new onlyIf type — current enum only has targetHpBelowPct).`
- **Barrow-Pierce** — Your opener can land on the BACK band and deals +30% there (folds the dive into the stealth-open). *Tradeoff:* the dive/reach build — the melee-only answer to the Sheltered Caster (P11); pays for it with no above/below-HP amplifier, so it under-burns when the back caster is already dead. `NEW: the reach-on-opener relies on the dive primitive (ability special); the +30%-vs-back is dmgPct gated by a targetBand onlyIf (new condition type).`
- **Throat-Reaver** — Your opener deals +30% to a target below 35% HP instead. *Tradeoff:* the execute build — converts the cheap stealth strike into a second finisher for the soft-enrage race (P12); loses the fresh-target alpha and the back-line reach. `dmgPct + onlyIf{targetHpBelowPct:35}.`

### Tier 2 — Single-target vs. the wave (priority-delete vs. clearing the room)
- **Singular Malice** — +22% damage while 2 or fewer enemies remain. *Tradeoff:* the boss/priority-kill build — leans all the way into single-target erase for the Dangerous Add (P8) and bosses; dead weight on a packed trash pull. `dmgPct + onlyIf{enemiesAtMost:2}.`
- **Reaving Whisper** — Your strikes spill 35% of their damage onto one adjacent enemy while 3+ are up. *Tradeoff:* the only AoE answer in the tree — keeps the Assassin from drowning on trash weeks (P2/wide pulls); a flat downgrade in any 1-2 target window where the spec wants to focus. `NEW: cleave-splash on basic/finisher hits (splashPct + splashCount on the spec's attacks), gated to enemiesAtLeast:3.`
- **Patient Blade** — +9% damage at all times, no condition. *Tradeoff:* the safe floor — never wrong, never exciting; the pick for a player who doesn't want to commit to trash-vs-boss and just wants reliable uptime. `dmgPct.`

### Tier 3 — The Poison economy (build the stacks vs. spend them harder)
- **Weeping Wound** — Your poison applications land +1 extra stack and last longer; poison ticks +20% harder. *Tradeoff:* the sustained-DoT build — ramps damage across a long single-target fight and feeds a fatter Eviscerate; weak in the first few seconds before stacks exist, so it loses burst openers. `NEW: poison apply grants +1 stack & +1 duration, and a poison-DoT magnitude multiplier (status-tick scaling — no talent field today).`
- **Venom-Glut** — Each poison stack on your target adds +4% to your finisher (Eviscerate) damage, on top of its base per-stack bonus. *Tradeoff:* the burst-payoff build — turns the poison ramp into one explosive spend instead of steady ticks; does nothing if you don't reach the finisher window, punishing sloppy play. `NEW: finisher gains +X% per stack of poison on target (per-stack damage scaling keyed to a status count — abilities have perStackOf, talents do not).`
- **Cold Patience** — +6% damage and +5% crit chance, always. *Tradeoff:* the generalist poison-agnostic pick — steady value that ignores the stack economy entirely; gives up both the long-fight ramp and the finisher spike. `dmgPct + critPct.`

### Tier 4 — The crit-chain (snowball execution vs. raw conversion vs. staying alive)
- **Cold Cascade** — When you crit, your next strike is a guaranteed crit (chaining off Cold Blood and Shadow Step's resets). *Tradeoff:* the snowball build — the highest skill-ceiling, highest-ceiling damage line that rewards keeping the crit chain alive and Camouflage resetting; offers zero defense and falls off if the chain breaks. `NEW: on-crit "guarantee next crit" flag (status-driven crit guarantee — no talent crit-state field exists).`
- **Killing Frost** — +12% crit chance and +20% crit damage, flat. *Tradeoff:* the reliable-crit build — converts the spec's crit synergies into raw, unconditional throughput without needing a chain to hold; gives up the explosive snowball ceiling of Cold Cascade. `NEW: critPct exists; the +20% crit DAMAGE has no talent field today (critMult is a buff stat, not a talent effect).`
- **Marrow-Thirst** — Your crits heal you for 4% of your max HP. *Tradeoff:* the self-sustain bruiser line — buys the lone-melee Assassin enough lifesteal to survive a dive onto the back band without a healer babysitting it; pure-offense builds rightly skip it as a damage loss. `NEW: heal-on-crit (lifesteal keyed to crit events — no talent healing/lifesteal field).`

### Tier 5 — The capstone (re-key the spec: chain-killer vs. assassin's mark vs. ghost)
- **Unending Slaughter** — When you kill an enemy, your finisher's cooldown resets and your next strike is empowered. *Tradeoff:* the soft-enrage chain-kill capstone (P12) — converts the spec into a wave-erasing executioner that snowballs through low-HP mobs; useless on a single fat boss where there's nothing to kill mid-pull. `NEW: reset-finisher-cooldown-on-kill + empower-next (on-kill hook — no talent kill-trigger field).`
- **Sever the Thread** — Your opener and finisher gain full back-band reach AND deal +40% to the sheltered back caster; while a back-band enemy lives, your single-target damage to it is +15%. *Tradeoff:* the dedicated dive capstone (P11) — makes the Assassin the definitive melee answer to the Sheltered Caster, erasing the protected mob the rest of the party can't touch; pours everything into reach and is flat on a fight with no back line. `NEW: grant dive-reach to multiple abilities + a band-keyed damage amp (targetBand onlyIf + reach primitive — neither is a talent field).`
- **The Vanishing** — After scoring a kill, you re-enter stealth automatically (one free empowered opener) and take -50% damage for 1 turn. *Tradeoff:* the survival/execution capstone — lets a healer-light comp run the Assassin as a self-resetting ghost that vanishes between kills; the conditional, kill-gated defensive (not flat DR) keeps it timer-regime-safe, but it sacrifices the raw chain throughput of Unending Slaughter. `NEW: on-kill re-stealth + a 1-turn keyed intake reduction (on-kill status grant — no talent status/defensive-window field).`

**Builds:** (1) **Dive Assassin** — Barrow-Pierce → Singular Malice → Venom-Glut → Cold Cascade → Sever the Thread: the melee P11 specialist who erases the back caster. (2) **Execute Chain-Killer** — Throat-Reaver → Reaving Whisper → Weeping Wound → Cold Cascade → Unending Slaughter: a P12 soft-enrage wave-deleter. (3) **Alpha Burster** — Marrow-Pierce → Singular Malice → Venom-Glut → Killing Frost → Sever the Thread: the priority-delete P8 specialist. (4) **Lone-Knife Bruiser** — Throat-Reaver → Patient Blade → Cold Patience → Marrow-Thirst → The Vanishing: the self-sustaining, healer-light dive survivor.

## Archer — DPS (rogue), Back line

*Identity: a selfish ranged physical sharpshooter who Marks a target, reaches over the front rank to pick the sheltered back-line caster, and steadies their own shot to focus-fire it dead. Every tier asks which problem the bow answers: the reach pick, the resist-wall ceiling, the execute race, or the trash hail.*

### Tier 1 — The Quarrel's Purpose (what your Mark is FOR)
This fork re-keys the spec's signature debuff: is the Mark a selfish focus-fire amp, a wide opener, or a steadier line?
- **Quarrel-Mark** — While your focus target is below 50% HP, your shots hit +14% harder (the Mark becomes a personal focus-fire kill stamp). *Tradeoff:* serves the single-target / executioner build; pure selfish ceiling, gives up any AoE or survival the siblings offer. `dmgPct: 14, onlyIf: { type: "targetHpBelowPct", value: 50 }`
- **Hail-Marked** — +16% damage while 3 or more enemies are up (you spray Marks across the pack instead of one). *Tradeoff:* the AoE / trash-clear opener; dead weight on a lone boss, sacrifices the execute payoff. `dmgPct: 16, onlyIf: { type: "enemiesAtLeast", value: 3 }`
- **Steady Quarrel** — +8% damage at all times (a level, always-on draw). *Tradeoff:* the safe balanced floor — wins in no single situation but never goes dark; gives up the higher conditional ceilings on both sides. `dmgPct: 8`

### Tier 2 — The Reach (lean INTO the signature class-tool, or branch away)
The Archer's Phase-P niche is NATIVE RANGED REACH — picking off the sheltered back-line caster. This tier decides whether you double down on it or trade it for raw single-target ceiling.
- **Over-the-Barrow Volley** — Over the Barrow can pick off TWO sheltered targets in the back band and its back-band bonus rises to +60%. *Tradeoff:* the dedicated reach / P11 back-line-assassination build; the strongest answer to the protected caster, but wasted when there is no back rank to reach. `NEW: per-ability override — extend over-the-barrow targeting to pattern "adjacent" count 2 within band "back", and raise its existing targetBand:"back" multiplyDamage from 1.3 to 1.6 (no current talent field touches a specific ability's targeting/band modifier).`
- **Leaden Carillon** — Over the Barrow becomes a single heavy aimed shot: drop the reach, gain +50% damage versus targets below 35% HP (a second execute hammer). *Tradeoff:* the single-target ceiling build that abandons the reach niche entirely — best raw boss DPS, worst answer to a back-line problem. `NEW: per-ability override — strip over-the-barrow's targetBand back modifier and add a modifier when targetHpBelowPct 35 → multiplyDamage 1.5 (talents cannot rewrite an ability's modifier set).`
- **Long Draw** — +12% damage while 2 or fewer enemies remain (you favour the patient line over the crowd, reach intact but unimproved). *Tradeoff:* the generalist boss-lean middle — keeps your base reach shot and rewards thinning the pack, but improves neither the reach nor the execute as sharply as its siblings. `dmgPct: 12, onlyIf: { type: "enemiesAtMost", value: 2 }`

### Tier 3 — The Aim Window (burst spike vs steady tempo vs the kill race)
Shapes how your damage is delivered — front-loaded through Cold Focus, smoothed across the fight, or sharpened for the soft-enrage execute.
- **Cold Focus** — A flat +6% crit chance at all times that sharpens your aim window (passive precision that stacks under Cold Focus's crit buff). *Tradeoff:* the sustained-tempo / crit build; reliable every turn, but no conditional spike when it matters most. `critPct: 6`
- **Frostbound Loosing** — +18% damage while your focus target is below 30% HP (you save your steadiest shots for the kill). *Tradeoff:* the burst / soft-enrage executioner spike — huge on the kill, invisible until the target is bloodied. `dmgPct: 18, onlyIf: { type: "targetHpBelowPct", value: 30 }`
- **Unerring Patience** — +4% crit and +7% damage, always (a measured blend). *Tradeoff:* the balanced metronome floor — never the highest spike nor the purest crit engine, but always contributing. `dmgPct: 7, critPct: 4`

### Tier 4 — Keeping the Line (active/conditional survival, never flat passive DR)
A back-line DPS still eats spillover. This fork is your one defensive choice — all three are keyed or conditional, none is a flat damage sponge.
- **Carrion-Walker's Footing** — While 3 or more enemies are up, you take 14% less damage (you read the pull and stay out of the hail). *Tradeoff:* the trash-survival / AoE-build defensive — keeps you alive through pack pulls, but does nothing on a lone boss where you most want output. `intakePct: -14` *(applied with an enemiesAtLeast:3 gate)* `NEW: extend onlyIf to gate intakePct, not only dmgPct (resolveTalents currently applies intakePct unconditionally; conditional intake requires a combat.ts change).`
- **Grave-Iron Resolve** — While your focus target is below 35% HP, you take 12% less damage (you can plant your feet for the kill without dying to the enrage). *Tradeoff:* the executioner-build defensive — survives the soft-enrage race, but offers nothing during the long opening of a fight. `intakePct: -12` *(applied with a targetHpBelowPct:35 gate)* `NEW: same conditional-intake hook as above (gate intakePct by onlyIf).`
- **Quarried Stillness** — +10% max HP (a deeper buffer you carry into any fight). *Tradeoff:* the unconditional safety floor — the only always-on survival pick, but the timer-bound regime punishes raw EHP, so it is the deliberately weakest-against-the-clock choice. `maxHpPct: 10`

### Tier 5 — The Last Quarrel (capstone — the build's punchline)
The commitment node that re-keys your endgame: pure execute, the trash-clearing storm, or a focus-fire amp that finally reaches back to the party.
- **The Last Quarrel** — While your focus target is below 35% HP, your damage spikes +28% (every shot becomes a finisher in the kill window). *Tradeoff:* the single-target / soft-enrage capstone — the highest boss-and-execute ceiling in the spec, but flatly dead until the target is bleeding. `dmgPct: 28, onlyIf: { type: "targetHpBelowPct", value: 35 }`
- **Culling Storm** — While 3 or more enemies are up, +24% damage AND your Carrion Hail / Culling Rain strike a second time at 50% (the pack-clearing crescendo). *Tradeoff:* the AoE trash-clear capstone — owns multi-pull weeks, but every point is wasted on a single target. `dmgPct: 24, onlyIf: { type: "enemiesAtLeast", value: 3 }` plus `NEW: per-ability rider — Carrion Hail and Culling Rain deal a second 50% all-enemy hit (a "repeat-strike at pct" mechanic; no talent field repeats an ability's hit).`
- **Hunter's Largesse** — Your Mark also amplifies the WHOLE party's damage on that target by +12% (a second damage taken increase on the Marked enemy), at the cost of −6% to your own output. *Tradeoff:* buys back the support side the recut removed — turns the selfish Archer into a focus-fire enabler for the group, surrendering personal ceiling for party throughput; the only co-op capstone, useless solo. `NEW: amplify the Mark status's damageTaken magnitude by +12 (party-facing, distinct from the self-only dmgPct knob) AND apply a self −6% dmgPct (dmgPct: -6 is expressible; the Mark-magnitude buff is NOT — no talent field edits a status's statMods).`

**Builds:** (1) **Back-line Executioner** — Quarrel-Mark → Leaden Carillon → Frostbound Loosing → Grave-Iron Resolve → The Last Quarrel: single-target P2 resist-wall / P8 kill / P12 soft-enrage; (2) **Reach Sharpshooter** — Quarrel-Mark/Steady → Over-the-Barrow Volley → Cold Focus → Grave-Iron Resolve → The Last Quarrel: the P11 back-line-caster answer; (3) **Carrion Hailer (AoE)** — Hail-Marked → Long Draw → Cold Focus → Carrion-Walker's Footing → Culling Storm: trash-clear; (4) **Party Focus-Fire Marker** — Steady Quarrel → Long Draw → Unerring Patience → Quarried Stillness → Hunter's Largesse: a teamwide kill-target enabler.

## Pyromancer — DPS (mage)

*Identity:* the burn-stack AoE detonator — paint the pack in Burn, then explode it. Every tier asks the same question a different way: do you stack WIDE (contagion across the pack), tall (fewer targets, hotter ticks), or do you trade the slow-burn for a controlled detonation that delays a cast and locks an add. The signature fork is contagion-AoE (P3 armour wall) vs. detonation-control (P1 delay / P8 lock).

### Tier 1 — The Ember's Nature (how Burn behaves on the pack)
- **Creeping Embers** — Every offensive cast also spreads 1 Burn stack to an enemy adjacent to your target. *Tradeoff:* the contagion / P3-armour-wall build — Burn blankets the whole pack with no extra GCDs, so Ignite/Emberstorm detonations hit everyone. Gives up the focused single-target ramp and the control payoff of the other two. `NEW: on every offensive cast, apply 1 stack of the 'burn' status to one enemy adjacent to the primary target (auto-spread rider).`
- **Smothering Ash** — Burn cannot spread, but each of your Burn stacks ticks +25% harder and lasts +1 turn. *Tradeoff:* the tall single-target / boss build (P3 against a lone armoured target, P12 ramp) — fewer targets but a far hotter bar. Gives up pack coverage; weak when 4+ are up. `NEW: increase Burn DoT tick magnitude by +25% and Burn duration by +1 turn for Burn applied by this caster; suppress all Burn spread mechanics.`
- **Kindled Patience** — Burn you apply starts at 2 stacks instead of 1 the first time it lands on a fresh target. *Tradeoff:* the balanced ramp pick — faster initial stacking on a clean pull without committing to spread or tall ticks, but no ceiling on either axis; the safe middle. `NEW: when applying Burn to a target with no existing Burn, apply +1 additional initial stack (first-application bonus only).`

### Tier 2 — The Detonation (what an explosion is for)
- **Pyre-Vents** — Detonating 4+ Burn stacks grants +25% power for 2 turns (your Combustion payoff) AND, if the detonated target had Burn, stuns it for 1 turn. *Tradeoff:* the detonation-control build — turns Ignite/Emberstorm into a P1 cast-*delay* and P8 add-*lock* while still spiking your damage. Gives up raw splash width; you must set up the stack before it pays. `NEW: on self-detonation of >=4 Burn stacks, apply 'stun' (1 turn) to detonated targets that had Burn, in addition to the existing +25% power buff.`
- **Cinderburst** — Your detonations splash to +1 additional adjacent target and deal +20% detonation damage. *Tradeoff:* the AoE-payoff build — the widest possible explosion across a stacked pack (P3 trash, P12 burst), no control attached. Gives up the stun (no P1/P8) and the execute focus. `NEW: increase detonation splashCount by +1 and detonation damage by +20% (Ignite / Emberstorm / Meteor detonate channel).`
- **Hollow Coals** — Detonations refund 1 cooldown turn on Ignite per 4 stacks consumed. *Tradeoff:* the tempo / sustained-cycle build — detonate more often for steady throughput rather than one big bloom; serves a long boss timer. Gives up the burst spike and the control of its siblings. `NEW: on self-detonation, reduce Ignite's remaining cooldown by floor(stacksConsumed / 4) turns.`

### Tier 3 — Where the Fire Reaches (range profile vs. the pack)
- **Far-Cast Ember** — +18% damage while 3 or more enemies are up; your Burn application range extends to the back band, letting you light the sheltered caster. *Tradeoff:* the reach / P11 + AoE build — answers the back-line caster AND the trash wall at once. Gives up single-target ceiling; the bonus evaporates on a lone boss. `dmgPct: 18, onlyIf { enemiesAtLeast: 3 }` plus `NEW: this caster's Burn-applying abilities may target the back band (reach flag for P11).`
- **Focused Pyre** — +20% damage when 2 or fewer enemies remain. *Tradeoff:* the single-target / boss build (P3 lone armour wall, P12) — pure ceiling on the kill target. Gives up all pack/reach value; dead weight on heavy trash. `dmgPct: 20, onlyIf { enemiesAtMost: 2 }`
- **Steady Flame** — +10% damage, always. *Tradeoff:* the unconditional floor — never the best in any single fight, never zero either; the safe pick for a player who doesn't want to read the pull. Gives up the high end of both forks. `dmgPct: 10`

### Tier 4 — Ward of the Flame (active/conditional defense, not a flat wall)
- **Ashen Veil** — When you detonate 4+ Burn stacks, you take −30% damage for 2 turns. *Tradeoff:* the active-mitigation defensive — survival is *earned* by playing the detonation rotation, so it never beats reading the fight; pairs with any detonate build. Gives up the throughput of Emberglass and the burst of Searing Resolve. `NEW: on self-detonation of >=4 Burn stacks, apply a 2-turn self status granting intakePct −30% (conditional, detonation-gated mitigation).`
- **Searing Resolve** — +14% damage while your focus target is below 30% HP. *Tradeoff:* the no-defense execute build — pure offense for the soft-enrage race (P12), trusting the timer to end before you're hit. Gives up all mitigation. `dmgPct: 14, onlyIf { targetHpBelowPct: 30 }`
- **Emberglass** — −8% damage taken and +4% damage. *Tradeoff:* the balanced floor — a thin always-on cushion plus a little output, for a player who wants neither a rotation-gated ward nor a glass-cannon gamble. Gives up the high end of both siblings. `intakePct: -8, dmgPct: 4`

### Tier 5 — The Last Coal (capstone — re-keys the build)
- **Conflagrant Bloom** — While 3 or more enemies are up, +16% damage; capstone: your Burn spreads from any detonated target to ALL surviving enemies in the pack. *Tradeoff:* the contagion-AoE capstone — converts the whole pack into one shared Burn pool that every detonation feeds; the definitive P3 trash-melter. Gives up single-target and control entirely. `dmgPct: 16, onlyIf { enemiesAtLeast: 3 }` plus `NEW: on self-detonation, spread the pre-detonation Burn stacks of the detonated target to ALL living enemies (full-pack contagion capstone).`
- **Crown of Cinders** — +18% damage while your focus target is below 35% HP; capstone: detonating Burn on a target below 35% HP refunds half the stacks consumed, letting you re-detonate the execute target. *Tradeoff:* the burst/execute capstone (P12 soft-enrage) — a chain-detonation kill engine on the boss. Gives up all pack coverage and control. `dmgPct: 18, onlyIf { targetHpBelowPct: 35 }` plus `NEW: when detonating Burn on a target below 35% HP, refund floor(stacksConsumed / 2) Burn stacks to that target (execute-phase stack refund).`
- **Bell-Warden's Ember** — capstone: your first detonation-stun each fight lasts +1 turn, and while a target is stunned or Burn-locked your Magic damage to it is +30%. *Tradeoff:* the control capstone — pays off the Pyre-Vents delay/lock identity (P1 + P8) with a damage spike on the locked target, fusing control into throughput. Requires the Pyre-Vents path to function; gives up raw AoE and execute ceiling. `NEW: extend the first detonation-stun of each encounter by +1 turn; while a target has the 'stun' status, this caster's Magic damage to it is +30% (control-into-damage capstone).`

**Builds:** (1) Contagion AoE-melter — Creeping Embers → Cinderburst → Far-Cast Ember → Ashen Veil → Conflagrant Bloom (P3 armour wall + P11 reach across a pack). (2) Detonation-control — Kindled Patience → Pyre-Vents → Steady Flame → Ashen Veil → Bell-Warden's Ember (P1 cast-delay + P8 add-lock with a payoff). (3) Tall single-target / execute — Smothering Ash → Hollow Coals/Cinderburst → Focused Pyre → Searing Resolve → Crown of Cinders (P3 lone wall + P12 soft-enrage). (4) Tempo sustained — Kindled Patience → Hollow Coals → Steady Flame → Emberglass → Conflagrant Bloom (long-timer steady detonation cycle).

### Tier 1 — The Garden's Reach (how wide does the green grow?)
- **Spreading Roots** — Your rolling HoTs (Regrowth/Blossom/Nature's Breath) tick for +18% while 3 or more enemies are up, but lose that bonus on a near-cleared pull. *Tradeoff:* the trash-week / drowning-tick build — maximum coverage HPS when the room is full; gives up the focused output of the boss-week sibling and the personal floor of the survival sibling. `dmgPct: 18, onlyIf { enemiesAtLeast: 3 }` (folds into the healing output channel — FREE).
- **Last Light** — +16% healing (and damage) while the focus enemy is below 40% HP — your green surges as the pull dies. *Tradeoff:* the boss-week / execute-window build, pairing with the burst forks below; weak during the long full-room stretch where Spreading Roots shines. `dmgPct: 16, onlyIf { targetHpBelowPct: 40 }` (FREE).
- **Deeprooted** — +12% max HP. A quiet personal buffer so the back line survives a stray cleave or a missed interrupt. *Tradeoff:* the safety pick for new/under-geared rosters; the only Tier-1 option that does nothing for throughput, so it falls off hard once the timer (not survival) is the wall. `maxHpPct: 12` (FREE).

### Tier 2 — The Unbinding Word (lean into the typed dispel, or branch toward output)
- **Unbinding Litany** — Your Nature/Poison cleanse strips +1 debuff and grants each cleansed ally +10% power for 2 turns. *Tradeoff:* the anti-Creeping-Rot specialist — turns the Mire's P4 answer into a party damage swing; useless on a pull with nothing to dispel, where the other two siblings always work. `NEW: talent that modifies the lifebinder's cleanse ability — +1 cleanse count and an on-cleanse power buff (no talent field exists for cleanse-count or post-cleanse buffs today).`
- **Quickened Sap** — Your cleanse comes off cooldown 1 turn faster (cd3 to cd2), so the rot never escalates past you. *Tradeoff:* the throughput-of-utility pick — more dispel uptime against fast-stacking rot, but no power swing and no extra strip; loses to Litany on a single big curse and to Wild Growth on a clean pull. `NEW: talent that reduces a specific ability's cooldownTurns (talents cannot touch ability cooldowns today).`
- **Wild Growth** — +10% healing output, always. You walk away from the dispel's depth for a flat, unconditional green floor. *Tradeoff:* the generalist / clean-week pick — the safe choice when the affix isn't rot; gives up every dispel upgrade, so the Mire run punishes it. `dmgPct: 10` (FREE).

### Tier 3 — Duration vs. Magnitude (the P5/P6 identity, baked into one octagon)
- **Drowned-Root Renewal** — Your rolling HoTs gain +1 tick of duration and tick +10% harder, but can never crit — a longer, gentler green that OUTLASTS the drowning tick. *Tradeoff:* the P6 coverage build — the pre-blanket that survives the long submersion; explicitly surrenders the spike (no crit, no burst) so it loses the single P5 hammer by design. `NEW: talent that extends HoT duration by +1 tick AND adds a flat HoT-magnitude bonus AND suppresses crit on HoTs (no duration, HoT-specific magnitude, or crit-suppression field exists in talents).`
- **Sudden Bloom** — Your Blossom blooms for +35% burst on trigger/expire, and +15% crit while the focus enemy is below 50% HP — short, intense green for the kill window. *Tradeoff:* the burst-triage build, buying back a piece of the P5 spike the spec normally lacks; the HoTs are shorter and gamble on the bloom landing on time, so they're worse at the sustained drowning tick. `critPct: 15` is FREE but conditional crit needs `NEW: gate critPct behind onlyIf { targetHpBelowPct } (onlyIf currently gates ONLY dmgPct), plus NEW: talent that amplifies Blossom's bloom-heal magnitude.`
- **Even Tending** — +7% healing and +5% crit, unconditionally. Neither longest nor sharpest — a steady middle that asks nothing of your timing. *Tradeoff:* the low-skill-floor balanced pick; never the best at coverage OR at the spike, so it's the deliberately-weak safe fork you trade up from. `dmgPct: 7, critPct: 5` (FREE).

### Tier 4 — Vital Surge's Tribute (what does consuming the garden buy?)
- **Verdant Reclamation** — When Vital Surge consumes 3+ HoT stacks, it also strips 1 Nature/Poison debuff from the target. *Tradeoff:* the burst-and-cleanse fusion for the rot fight — folds emergency triage and a dispel into one button; wasted when there's nothing to cleanse, where the damage sibling always pays out. `NEW: talent that adds a conditional cleanse rider to a specific ability, gated on consumed-HoT-stacks (no cleanse rider or self-stack condition in talents).`
- **Thornward Vigor** — +14% damage while 3 or more enemies are up — your Smite-equivalent and bloom backlash actually hurt the pack, letting you contribute to the trash race while healing. *Tradeoff:* the damage-while-healing / fast-clear build for over-geared weeks; pure offence, so it adds nothing on a single-target boss or a dispel-heavy room. `dmgPct: 14, onlyIf { enemiesAtLeast: 3 }` (FREE).
- **Sheltering Bloom** — −10% damage taken for any ally currently carrying one of your HoTs. *Tradeoff:* the active-mitigation coverage pick — keyed to your own pre-blanket so it's only EHP where you've already planted, never undifferentiated party DR; gives up Vital Surge's cleanse and the trash damage, and does nothing on an un-HoTed ally caught flat-footed. `NEW: keyed intakePct that applies only to allies who currently have a Lifebinder HoT (talent intakePct is uniform-self today and cannot read ally HoT status).`

### Tier 5 — The Deep Commitment (the capstone fork that re-keys the build)
- **Crowned in Ash** — While any ally is below 30% HP, your Magic damage also heals the party for 50% of the damage dealt — an off-school answer to the armour wall (P3) that turns triage into smite-atonement. *Tradeoff:* the damage-dealing-healer capstone for armour weeks; demands you spend GCDs on damage instead of pure heals, so it loses raw throughput exactly when the group is already low. `NEW: smite-atonement mechanic gated on lowest-ally-HP (no damage-to-party-heal conversion or ally-HP condition in talents).`
- **The Long Tide** — Your rolling HoTs no longer expire on their own while the bearer is below 50% HP — the green keeps tending the wounded until they're safe. *Tradeoff:* the ultimate P6 coverage capstone, the answer to a sustained drowning tick that never lets up; pure prevention, it does nothing for a healthy party and cannot save a one-shot spike. `NEW: HoT-persistence mechanic — suppress HoT expiry while the bearer is below an HP threshold (no HoT-lifetime control or bearer-HP condition in talents).`
- **Severing Frost** — Your enemy purge (P9) also rips a buff from every adjacent enemy and you gain +12% crit for 2 turns after a purge. *Tradeoff:* the utility/anti-buff capstone — leans the spec into offensive dispel for buff-heavy packs and rewards it with a personal crit window; the weakest pick on a fight with nothing to purge, where the two healing capstones always work. `critPct: 12` is FREE as a flat passive, but the purge-AoE and on-purge trigger need `NEW: talent that adds an enemy buff-strip rider + an on-purge self-crit-buff to the lifebinder's purge ability.`

**Builds:** (1) **Rolling-HoT Drowning-Tick coverage** — Spreading Roots into Drowned-Root Renewal into The Long Tide: the pure P6 pre-blanket that outlasts the submersion. (2) **Rot-cleanse Mire specialist** — Unbinding Litany into Verdant Reclamation, with Quickened Sap uptime: the P4 Creeping-Rot answer with a party power swing. (3) **Burst-triage / off-school smiter** — Last Light into Sudden Bloom into Crowned in Ash: buys back a slice of the P5 spike and pays armour-wall (P3) weeks in damage-as-healing. (4) **Damage-while-healing fast-clear** — Spreading Roots into Thornward Vigor into Severing Frost: a contributor on over-geared trash and buff-heavy purge weeks.

## Mystic — Tank (Sage)

*The control tank: you don't raise a wall, you stand in the pack and turn its blows back on it — parry, counter, kill the cast, stun the add, bleed it dry while you drink. Survival here is earned with timing, never bought with a slab of EHP.*

### Tier 1 — The Counter (what your parry is FOR)
The Mystic's whole defense is avoidance + counter. This tier decides what a successful parry pays out — selfish bleed-back, group sustain, or a faster control loop.

- **Marrow-Drinker** — While Earthen/Unmoving stance is up, your counter-hits steal +50% more life back to you, and parrying refunds a sliver of Zen Strike's cooldown. *Tradeoff:* serves the lone lifesteal-bruiser build (stand-and-drink solo survival); gives up any group benefit and any extra control the siblings offer. `NEW: on-parry, reduce a named ability's remaining cooldown by N turns + scale the existing lifesteal mechanic's pct by the talent.`
- **Tidebound Calm** — Each parry also heals the lowest-HP ally for a small amount (your counter becomes a trickle of party sustain). *Tradeoff:* the soft-support tank — props up a thin healer through trash, but the heal is small and you keep none of the self-bleed; loses Marrow-Drinker's solo wall and Stonebound's tempo. `NEW: on-parry heal-lowest-ally hook (extends the existing on-parry-heal mechanic's target to lowest-hp-ally).`
- **Stonebound Rhythm** — Every parry shaves a turn off Leg Sweep's cooldown; the more they swing, the more you sweep. *Tradeoff:* the control engine — turns a busy pack into near-permanent AoE stun uptime; gives up the self-heal of Marrow-Drinker and the group heal of Tidebound, betting that locking the pack down beats out-sustaining it. `NEW: on-parry, reduce Leg Sweep cooldown by N turns (parry→CC reset loop).`

### Tier 2 — Single Lock vs. Sweeping Lock (your control's shape)
The Mystic answers P8 (add lock) with Leg Sweep and P1 (kill-cast) with Zen Strike. This tier is where your CC commits to a shape — one hard answer, or wide soft pressure.

- **Iron Discipline** — Zen Strike's interrupt also leaves the silenced target unable to cast for an extra turn (the kick lands harder and buys real time). *Tradeoff:* the dedicated P1 cancel — best on dangerous-cast bosses where one clean kick matters; gives up the AoE reach of the other two against trash waves. `NEW: interrupt also applies a 1-turn silence status to the interrupted target.`
- **Sweeping Tempest** — Leg Sweep hits the whole front band instead of just 2 adjacent, and its stun catches everything it touches. *Tradeoff:* the P8 add-wave answer — locks an entire pack at once; gives up Iron Discipline's surgical kick-extend and Hollow Toll's payoff, and does nothing extra to a single hard caster. `NEW: widen Leg Sweep targeting pattern from adjacent/2 to all-front-band.`
- **Hollow Toll** — Enemies you stun take +20% damage from the party while stunned (your lockdown becomes the party's kill window). *Tradeoff:* the control-into-throughput bridge — makes the DPS's job easier on every Leg Sweep; needs allies to capitalize, and gives up the raw single-target lock (Iron Discipline) and the full-pack lock (Sweeping Tempest). `NEW: stunned enemies take +X% damage (a vulnerability debuff applied alongside the existing stun).`

### Tier 3 — Active Mitigation (how you survive the spike, without a wall)
Pure timer-bound design: no flat %DR brick here. Each option is an *active* or *conditional* survival lever tied to your stance/health, never an always-on slab.

- **Unyielding Footing** — While your stance (Earthen/Unmoving) is active, you take meaningfully less damage; the instant it drops, so does the mitigation. *Tradeoff:* rewards perfect stance uptime — strong for the pilot who plays the cooldown, dead weight in the gaps; gives up the always-on cushion of Living Bulwark and the burst-recovery of Second Wind. `NEW: conditional intakePct that applies ONLY while a named self-buff (stance) is active (current intakePct is unconditional).`
- **Living Bulwark** — A modest, always-on reduction to damage taken — the closest thing the Mystic has to a passive cushion, deliberately small. *Tradeoff:* the safety pick for an unsteady pilot or a thin healer; it's the *weakest* survival ceiling because it's unconditional (and the design wants conditional to win at high keys). The honest floor. `EXISTING: intakePct (small negative).`
- **Second Wind** — When you drop below 35% HP, your next stance grants a burst of self-regen on top of its parry. *Tradeoff:* the comeback lever — saves you from the spike that would have killed you, then idles when healthy; gives up Unyielding's sustained mitigation and Bulwark's reliability, betting on the one moment that matters. `NEW: onlyIf self-HP-below threshold → on next stance cast, add a regen-HoT (new self-low-HP condition + stance-keyed regen).`

### Tier 4 — The Peel (Spirit Link's identity — P6 drowning answer)
Spirit Link is the Mystic's one reach into ally survival, deliberately capped so it can't become generic off-heal EHP. This tier shapes that single peel — bigger soak, broader reach, or duration-vs-magnitude.

- **Shared Burden** — Spirit Link redirects more of the ally's damage onto you and the HoT outlasts the drowning tick (a long, gentle save). *Tradeoff:* the P6 sustained-rot answer — the *right* tool against a drowning/rot tick that grinds an ally; gives up Severing Link's emergency magnitude and Bound Spirits' coverage. `NEW: scale damage-redirect's redirectPct + extend the spirit-link-hot duration via the talent.`
- **Severing Link** — Spirit Link's HoT front-loads into a short, intense burst (a hard spike-soak now, gone fast). *Tradeoff:* the P5 reactive-spike answer — yanks an ally out of a one-shot window; the inverse of Shared Burden, useless against a slow grind, and narrower than Bound Spirits. `NEW: duration-vs-magnitude flip on spirit-link-hot (shorter duration, higher per-tick magnitude).`
- **Bound Spirits** — Spirit Link tethers a second nearby ally at half effect (the soak and HoT split across two). *Tradeoff:* the multi-target peel for chaotic pulls where two allies are dipping; each gets less, so it loses the single-target depth of both siblings — breadth over depth. `NEW: Spirit Link targets a 2nd ally at reduced (50%) effect (multi-target redirect/HoT).`

### Tier 5 — Capstone: the Mystic you become
The deep commitment node. Each capstone re-keys the whole build around one of the three fantasies the tree has been building toward — the wall, the warden, or the executioner-monk.

- **The Mountain Holds** — Each parry has a chance to fully reset Leg Sweep, and Unmoving Mountain's regen ticks for the entire fight at reduced strength. *Tradeoff:* the immovable control-tank capstone — pairs with Stonebound/Sweeping for near-permanent pack lockdown and self-sustain; gives up all party reach and all burst, you are the rock the pull breaks on. `NEW: on-parry chance to reset Leg Sweep cooldown + a fight-long low-magnitude self-regen (extends Unmoving Mountain).`
- **Warden of the Drowned** — While you hold hard threat, your Spirit Link can be active on two allies at once and its redirect cannot be broken — but your own outgoing damage is halved. *Tradeoff:* the pure protector capstone — the ultimate P6/P7 peel for a fragile comp; the damage halving makes it a real cost and locks you out of any kill-pressure build. `NEW: allow two concurrent Spirit Links + un-cleansable redirect, gated on holding hard threat; -50% self dmgPct.`
- **Hand of Stillwater** — Below 30% target HP, your Palm Strike and Zen Strike strike twice and your lifesteal doubles — you finish the pull by drinking it dry. *Tradeoff:* the lifesteal-executioner capstone — turns the soft-enrage finish into self-sustaining burst (pairs with Marrow-Drinker/Hand-of-Stillwater intake); gives up all the control and peel payoffs, the only capstone that bends the timer instead of the survival floor. `EXISTING+NEW: dmgPct with onlyIf targetHpBelowPct:30 (free) + NEW double-strike & lifesteal-doubling on the two named abilities below 30%.`

**Builds:** (1) **Stonebound Warden** — control-tank: Stonebound Rhythm + Sweeping Tempest + Unyielding Footing + Bound Spirits + The Mountain Holds = permanent pack lockdown. (2) **Lone Mountain** — selfish lifesteal-bruiser: Marrow-Drinker + Iron Discipline + Unyielding Footing + Severing Link + Hand of Stillwater = stand-and-drink solo survival that finishes fast. (3) **Drowned Shepherd** — protector/peel tank: Tidebound Calm + Hollow Toll + Second Wind + Shared Burden + Warden of the Drowned = the answer for a fragile comp's P6/P5 problems. (4) **Kill-Window Bruiser** — control-into-throughput hybrid: Stonebound Rhythm + Hollow Toll + Second Wind + Severing Link + Hand of Stillwater = lock, expose, and execute.

## Bonecaller — DPS (Necromancer)

*The back-line death-caller: it doesn't kill the pull, it lets the pull rot — feeding a stacking wasting curse, raising a skeletal servant from the fallen, and draining the spark back into itself.*

### Tier 1 — The Wasting (how your signature rot is shaped)
- **Creeping Waste** — Your wasting curse ramps faster: every refresh adds an extra stack, so a single target rots toward its cap in half the time. *Tradeoff:* the single-target / boss specialist's opener — front-loads your ramp so bosses melt sooner, but does nothing extra against a wide pull and offers no cushion. `dmgPct +18, onlyIf targetHpBelowPct 100 (always-on against the focus target — tuned as the single-target ramp lever). NEW (full fidelity): wasting-curse status applies +1 stack per reapply (faster DoT ramp on the focus).`
- **Rot on the Wind** — When you apply your wasting curse, a thinner rot also seeds the two nearest enemies. *Tradeoff:* the trash-clear answer — turns one curse into a whole rotting pack, but each seed is weaker, so it loses to Creeping Waste on a lone boss. `dmgPct +20, onlyIf enemiesAtLeast 3. NEW (full fidelity): on curse-apply, applyStatus a weaker rot DoT to 2 adjacent enemies (contagion seed).`
- **Patient Decay** — Your rot can no longer crit, but every active DoT ticks for a flat 15% more, forever. *Tradeoff:* the steady-floor pick — unconditional, reliable rot throughput that ignores enemy count and HP, but gives up the crit ceiling and the spread/ramp of its siblings. `dmgPct +12 (unconditional). NEW (full fidelity): DoT ticks +X% but cannot crit (duration-magnitude trade).`

### Tier 2 — The Servant (what you raise from the dead)
- **Raise the Barrow-Hound** — Your skeletal servant is a lean front-line hound: high attack rate, claws at the nearest enemy, and reclaims a fresh body whenever one falls so it never stays down long. *Tradeoff:* the sustained-pet-DPS build's core — a relentless extra body that snowballs through a pull, but it's fragile and contributes nothing while no corpse is available to re-raise. `dmgPct +10 (models the pet's steady extra output). NEW (full fidelity): summon a persistent fast melee pet (front band) that re-raises off the next corpse on death (pet engine, L.5).`
- **Bind the Carrion Choir** — Instead of one hound you raise a flock of weak skeletal birds that harry the back band, picking at sheltered casters you can't otherwise reach. *Tradeoff:* the reach answer — the only Bonecaller pick that pressures the back line, but the flock is individually feeble and wasted when everything dangerous is already up front. `dmgPct +12, onlyIf enemiesAtLeast 3. NEW (full fidelity): summon multiple frail flyer pets targeting the back band (reach + spread).`
- **The Servant Endures** — You raise no minion; instead the unspent grave-power hardens your own flesh and lets your rot feed you a trickle of life on every tick. *Tradeoff:* the pet-less self-sufficient line — frees you from corpse dependency and adds standalone survivability, but forfeits the entire summon axis the other two build around. `maxHpPct +10, dmgPct +4. NEW (full fidelity): self-HoT proportional to active DoT ticks (drain-to-self), in lieu of a pet.`

### Tier 3 — The Drain (how you turn their dying into your living)
- **Drink the Fading** — Your life-drain channels harder against a dying target, returning far more of the damage as healing once the enemy drops low. *Tradeoff:* the execute-sustain pick — keeps you (and your build's drain economy) alive through the kill window, but the bonus sleeps until the target is nearly dead. `dmgPct +16, onlyIf targetHpBelowPct 35. NEW (full fidelity): drain returns +X% as self-heal vs. low-HP targets (execute-keyed lifesteal).`
- **Glut of the Grave** — Each corpse that falls near you releases a burst of soul-rot, damaging everything still standing in the pack. *Tradeoff:* the AoE-on-kill snowball — converts a dying pack into chain damage that accelerates a wipe, but pays out nothing on a single boss with no adds to drop. `dmgPct +14, onlyIf enemiesAtLeast 3. NEW (full fidelity): on enemy death, applyStatus a short AoE rot pulse to remaining enemies (on-kill cascade).`
- **The Quiet Reserve** — Your drain becomes a slow, certain trickle that heals you a fixed amount on every cast regardless of the target. *Tradeoff:* the unconditional safety floor — dependable self-sustain that holds up against any pull, but it never spikes and surrenders the burst payoff of its two siblings. `intakePct -10. NEW (full fidelity): flat self-heal per cast (steady drain, no condition).`

### Tier 4 — The Curse Deepens (lean into rot, or branch toward raw death-magic)
- **Marrow-Deep Plague** — Your wasting curse gains extra duration and outlasts any cleanse window, but its ticks can no longer be amplified by your cooldowns — it is pure, patient attrition. *Tradeoff:* the contagion/attrition capstone-feeder — maximal DoT uptime that wins long, sustained fights, but caps your burst ceiling and rewards patience over tempo. `dmgPct +12 (models extended uptime). NEW (full fidelity): curse +duration, immune to one cleanse, but excluded from burst-window multipliers.`
- **Hex of the Hollow Bell** — Branch away from rot into direct death-magic: your hardest cast hits far harder against a wounded target, a clean spike instead of a slow bleed. *Tradeoff:* the burst/single-target branch — gives the curse build a real spike finisher, but abandons the spread-and-wait identity and does nothing while the target is healthy. `dmgPct +22, onlyIf targetHpBelowPct 50. NEW (full fidelity): a direct-damage nuke (not a DoT) scaling with target missing-HP.`
- **Withering Aura** — A passive miasma clings to you, dealing a small flat amount to every enemy near the front each tick — no setup, no condition, no ramp. *Tradeoff:* the zero-effort AoE floor for a pet/cleave build that wants its attention elsewhere, but the per-target damage is low and it scales poorly into single-target. `dmgPct +8 (unconditional, models the aura). NEW (full fidelity): self-anchored aura emitting a flat AoE rot tick to nearby enemies.`

### Tier 5 — The Last Toll (the deep capstone — what your build becomes)
- **The Marrow Throne** — Capstone of the pet line: while your skeletal servant lives, your own death-magic surges, and slaying an enemy immediately re-raises the servant from its corpse at full strength. *Tradeoff:* the summoner-snowball finisher — turns a timed pull into a self-feeding minion engine that accelerates through trash, but collapses to near-baseline the moment your servant is dead and no corpse is up. `dmgPct +18, onlyIf enemiesAtLeast 3. NEW (full fidelity): while pet alive, +X% caster damage; on kill, instantly re-raise the pet (pet-loop capstone).`
- **Crowned in Rot** — Capstone of the curse line: once your wasting curse reaches full stacks on a target, every other DoT you own on it detonates for a burst, then the curse resets to seed again. *Tradeoff:* the wasting-detonation finisher — a huge windowed payoff that rewards full-ramp single-target play, but demands the target survive long enough to cap and gives nothing to a build that spreads thin. `dmgPct +20, onlyIf targetHpBelowPct 50. NEW (full fidelity): at max curse stacks, detonate all DoTs on the target for burst, then reset curse stacks (resource-flip capstone).`
- **The Last Vigil** — Capstone of the sustain/utility line: you pre-place a quiet binding on one ally so that, should they fall, they rise once on their own — a self-contained battle-rez held in reserve. *Tradeoff:* the safety/utility capstone for a survival or progression build — a real wipe-insurance answer no other DPS brings, but it spends your deep node on resilience rather than the throughput the other two capstones grant. `intakePct -8, maxHpPct +6 (the throughput a survival build trades down to). NEW (full fidelity): pre-placed single-use battle-rez on a flagged ally (binds to sim.rez*; the "Calling Back the Quiet" family).`

**Builds:** **Sustained pet-master** (Raise the Barrow-Hound → The Servant Endures off-ramp → The Marrow Throne) snowballs trash with a re-raising minion; **single-target wasting/detonation** (Creeping Waste → Marrow-Deep Plague / Hex of the Hollow Bell → Crowned in Rot) ramps one target to a burst kill; **AoE rot-spreader** (Rot on the Wind → Glut of the Grave → Withering Aura) contagions a whole pull; **drain-survivalist / battle-rez utility** (The Servant Endures → The Quiet Reserve → The Last Vigil) trades damage for self-sustain and a unique party-saving pre-placed rez.

## Pallbinder — Healer (Necromancer)

*The death-priest who heals by drawing the spark from the dying — the grim mirror of the Lifebinder. Where the grove heals through nature, the Pallbinder heals through death: a bound guardian-wraith feeds anima to the party while a hoarded "Spark" resource turns the dying into someone else's second breath.*

> **Signature class-tools** (ability-side, the niche the tree bends around): the bound **Wraith** (guardian-wraith conduit that drains an enemy and pours the leeched anima into the lowest ally), **Spark** (a death-fueled healing resource banked from enemy deaths and from the drain), and the shared Necromancer **pre-placed battle-rez** (Soulstone). The third healer SHAPE: not Cleric's reactive burst, not Lifebinder's rolling HoT — a drain-conduit attrition healer who is strongest the longer the dying lasts.

### Tier 1 — The Source of Healing (where your throughput comes from)
- **Carrion Conduit** — While your Wraith is bound to an enemy, your healing output is +14%; the bond does nothing for you while no enemy is being drained. *Tradeoff:* the pet-leech identity build — huge sustain in long drawn-out pulls, but it goes flat the instant there is nothing to drain (single low-HP target, between packs). Serves the Wraith/drain build; gives up the always-on safety of the balanced pick. `dmgPct: 14, onlyIf{enemiesAtLeast: 1}` (heals fold into the output channel; gated so the bonus only lives while there is something for the Wraith to bite).
- **Spark of the Spent** — +18% healing while your focus enemy is below 35% HP (you heal hardest off the dying). *Tradeoff:* the execute-attrition build — converts low-enemy-HP windows into healing surges, perfect for the soft-enrage race, but worthless on a healthy boss or a fresh pull. Serves a kill-window-timed build; gives up sustained early-pull coverage. `dmgPct: 18, onlyIf{targetHpBelowPct: 35}`.
- **Pall of Patience** — +9% healing, always, with no condition (the steady draw). *Tradeoff:* the reliable floor — never the highest ceiling, never zero either; the pick for players who do not want their healing to blink on and off. Serves a generalist build; gives up the spikes the two conditional siblings reach. `dmgPct: 9` (default/balanced).

### Tier 2 — Wide or Deep (how the Spark spreads)
- **The Wraith's Wide Wings** — +20% healing while 3 or more enemies are up (the conduit feeds off a whole pack). *Tradeoff:* the trash-week / attrition build — turns big pulls into a rising tide of leeched anima, but folds on bosses and 1-2 enemy remainders. Serves AoE attrition; gives up single-target depth. `dmgPct: 20, onlyIf{enemiesAtLeast: 3}`.
- **The Last Vigil** — +20% healing while 2 or fewer enemies remain (everything pours into the one dying ally). *Tradeoff:* the boss / triage build — concentrates the whole draw into deep single-target healing for the tank-killer fight, but quiet during trash gauntlets. Serves single-target/boss; gives up pack coverage. `dmgPct: 20, onlyIf{enemiesAtMost: 2}`.
- **Even Tithe** — +10% healing, always (the death is owed evenly). *Tradeoff:* the safe middle — content-agnostic, neither AoE nor boss specialist; the floor pick that frees the other tiers to commit. Serves a generalist; gives up the 2x payoff the specialists get in their content. `dmgPct: 10` (default/balanced).

### Tier 3 — Tempo or Toll (sustained draw vs. death-fed spike)
- **The Slow Marrow** — +8% healing, always, plus +5% crit on your heals (a steady, slightly-streaky draw). *Tradeoff:* the tempo build — smooths the run with always-on output and crit-heal variance for occasional lucky tops; never windowed, so it cannot "save up" for the spike. Serves sustained coverage; gives up the burst the toll sibling front-loads. `dmgPct: 8, critPct: 5` (default/balanced).
- **Toll for the Dying** — +17% healing while your focus enemy is below 50% HP (the bell tolls as it falls, and your draw redoubles). *Tradeoff:* the spike build — a windowed surge that front-loads healing into every kill, ideal for catching the spike that lands as a mob dies; flat while enemies are healthy. Serves burst/kill-window healing; gives up early-pull tempo. `dmgPct: 17, onlyIf{targetHpBelowPct: 50}`.
- **The Cold Hand's Certainty** — +12% crit on your heals, no output bonus (you cannot raise the average, only the ceiling). *Tradeoff:* the high-variance build — a fat crit-heal chance that swings between overkill tops and dry stretches; rewards a busy heal cadence and punishes a sparse one. Serves a gamble/spike-via-crit build; gives up the guaranteed floor the other two carry. `critPct: 12`.

### Tier 4 — The Wraith's Other Half (what the bound guardian does for YOU)
- **Anima-Fed** — While the Wraith is bound (an enemy alive to drain), you take -12% damage — the leeched anima sheaths the binder. *Tradeoff:* the survival-via-uptime build — strong personal mitigation, but it is *conditional and active*: the moment the pack is dead and there is nothing to drain, the shield drops, so it cannot be a flat-DR crutch in the dead air between fights. Serves a self-sustain drain build; gives up raw throughput. `intakePct: -12` (note: applied while at least one enemy lives — see new engine effect; otherwise it is the existing uniform intake knob). *NEW: gate intakePct on `enemiesAtLeast` (conditional intake) so the mitigation only lives while the Wraith has a target.*
- **Spite of the Spark** — +15% healing while 3 or more enemies are up, but no personal mitigation at all (you pour everything outward and stand exposed). *Tradeoff:* the glass-conduit AoE build — maximal pack healing at the cost of your own skin; pairs with a tank who can hold and a comp that does not need you alive between hits. Serves throughput-over-self; gives up all personal defense. `dmgPct: 15, onlyIf{enemiesAtLeast: 3}`.
- **The Quiet Between** — +10% max HP (a deeper grave to draw from). *Tradeoff:* the buffer build — a flat health pad that, unlike Anima-Fed, also protects you in the gaps with nothing to drain; the safe survival pick for chaotic pulls, but the least throughput of the tier and (in the timer-bound regime) the least "active" choice. Serves a defensive generalist; gives up both the conditional shield's magnitude and the AoE healing. `maxHpPct: 10`.

### Tier 5 — The Final Communion (the capstone identity, where the build commits)
- **Glut of the Grave** — +16% healing while 3 or more enemies are up (the pack-feast — the Wraith gorges and the whole party drinks). *Tradeoff:* the deep AoE-attrition capstone — stacks with the wide-wings line into an overwhelming trash-pull tide; collapses on bosses and the last 1-2 mobs. Serves the AoE/attrition build to its ceiling; gives up the kill-race and the floor. `dmgPct: 16, onlyIf{enemiesAtLeast: 3}`.
- **Reaper's Recompense** — +20% healing while your focus enemy is below 30% HP (the deeper you let it die, the more it owes the living). *Tradeoff:* the execute-healing capstone — the strongest possible draw off the dying, the answer to the soft-enrage / kill-window race; near-dead on full-HP targets. Serves the kill-window/boss attrition build; gives up healthy-pull and trash-week coverage. `dmgPct: 20, onlyIf{targetHpBelowPct: 30}`.
- **The Even Reckoning** — +11% healing, always, and +4% crit on heals (the patient toll that never stops). *Tradeoff:* the unconditional capstone — the only Tier-5 pick that pays on a healthy boss, a fresh pull, AND a dying mob; the lowest ceiling, the highest floor, the choice for a player who wants healing they can plan around. Serves the generalist/tempo build; gives up the doubled payoff the two conditional capstones reach in their content. `dmgPct: 11, critPct: 4` (default/balanced).

**Builds:** **Wraith-conduit attrition** (Carrion Conduit → wide-wings → tempo → Anima-Fed → Glut of the Grave: a self-sustaining AoE-feast healer that snowballs the longer a pull lasts) · **Death-spark executioner** (Spark of the Spent → Last Vigil → Toll for the Dying → Anima-Fed → Reaper's Recompense: a kill-window healer who is strongest in the soft-enrage race and quiet on healthy targets) · **Glass conduit** (Carrion Conduit → wide-wings → Cold Hand's Certainty → Spite of the Spark → Glut of the Grave: maximum pack throughput, no personal defense) · **The Patient Toll generalist** (Pall of Patience → Even Tithe → Slow Marrow → The Quiet Between → The Even Reckoning: an unconditional, plannable floor-healer that never blinks off).

# Cross-cutting synthesis — the 12-spec talent revamp

I read the live engine to ground this (`web/src/sim/egm/combat.ts`, `stats.ts`, `engine.ts`; `web/src/content/schema.ts`). One finding reframes the whole scope and I lead with it.

## 0. The headline scope finding: the talent `onlyIf` path is a *duplicate, impoverished* copy of an existing condition engine

Nearly every spec lists "NEW onlyIf condition type X" as engine work. That framing inflates the cost. The reality:

- There are **two** condition evaluators in the codebase. The rich one — `condHolds()` in `combat.ts:63-73` — *already* evaluates `targetHpBelowPct`, `selfHitSinceLastAction`, `targetHasStatus`, `selfHasStatus`, `targetBand`, `targetHotStacksAtLeast`. The poor one — the inline switch in `passiveDamageMult()` at `combat.ts:318-324` — only knows `{targetHpBelowPct, enemiesAtLeast, enemiesAtMost}` and **only gates `dmgPct`**. The talent schema enum (`schema.ts:205`) mirrors the poor one.
- So the foundational engine task is **not** "implement N new predicates." It is: **(a) widen the talent `onlyIf` enum in `schema.ts:205` to the full closed set; (b) route the talent path through `condHolds()` instead of the duplicated switch; (c) make `onlyIf` gate the `intakePct` and `critPct` channels in `resolveTalents` (`stats.ts:112-125`, which today applies both unconditionally and folds intake into a single uniform self channel at `stats.ts:157`).**

Do that one unification and you simultaneously unblock **conditional intake/crit** (asked for by Guardian, Berserker, Crusader, Cleric, Assassin, Archer, Pyromancer, Arcanist, Lifebinder, Mystic, Pallbinder — i.e. **11 of 12 specs**) and **status/band/self-HP-gated dmgPct**. This is the keystone; sequence it first. I'd treat the per-spec "NEW onlyIf type" bullets as *consumers* of this one shared deliverable, not 11 separate features.

The predicates that are genuinely absent from `condHolds` today and must be added to the closed enum: `enemiesAtLeast` / `enemiesAtMost` (present in the talent switch but not in `condHolds`), `selfHpBelowPct` (Berserker, Mystic, Guardian-via-"below max HP", Pallbinder), `selfStacksAtLeast` / resource count (Berserker rampage, Lifebinder/Mystic HoT-stacks, Bonecaller curse-stacks), `allyHpBelowPct` / `lowestAllyHpBelowPct` (Cleric, Lifebinder, Crusader, Pallbinder), `selfPetAlive` (Bonecaller), `selfHoldsHardThreat` (Guardian, Crusader, Mystic, Pallbinder-proxied). Everything else (`targetHasStatus`, `selfHasStatus`, `targetBand`, `selfHitSinceLastAction`) is already in `condHolds` and just needs exposing to talents.

## 1. Consolidated, deduped engine-effects backlog (this is the real scope)

Grouped by subsystem, with which specs drive each. "(exists ability-side)" means the mechanic lives in a `PlayerAbility` special today and the new work is *promoting it into a talent channel*.

### A. Condition/gating engine (the keystone above — do first)
- **Unify talent `onlyIf` onto `condHolds` + extend closed enum** — all 12.
- **Gate `intakePct` via `onlyIf`** — Guardian, Berserker, Crusader, Cleric(shield), Assassin, Archer, Pyromancer, Arcanist, Lifebinder, Mystic, Pallbinder (11).
- **Gate `critPct` via `onlyIf`** — Assassin, Archer, Lifebinder (and Crusader/Pallbinder party-crit windows lean adjacent).
- **New predicates:** `selfHpBelowPct`, `selfStacksAtLeast`/resourceCount, `allyHpBelowPct`/`lowestAllyHpBelowPct`, `selfPetAlive`, `selfHoldsHardThreat` (see §0).

### B. `abilityOverride` talent channel (patch a named ability's params at `buildParty` time)
A single typed talent effect `abilityOverride: { abilityId, params?, targeting?, modifiers?, cooldownTurns?, charges? }` covers a startling number of bullets:
- **Scalar/param patches:** Berserker (Bloodlust 3→6, perStack 4→6, Massacre 20/2→35/2.5, Reckless Frenzy dur/cost/crit), Cleric (atonement healPctOfDamage, Greater Mending +50→+90, Light's Salvation), Arcanist (Counterspell cd 3→2 + addTurns 2→3, Frost Nova cd-1, Cold Focus crit-dmg mult), Mystic (lifesteal pct, redirectPct, spirit-link HoT dur), Lifebinder (Quickened Sap cd), Crusader (Holy Strike buff amount/dur/stacks).
- **Targeting/modifier rewrites:** Archer (Over-the-Barrow → adjacent count 2 band back, x1.3→x1.6; Leaden Carillon strip-band + add execute modifier), Mystic (Leg Sweep adjacent/2 → all-front-band), Arcanist (extend Arcane Bolt's CC modifier to chill), Crusader (split-shield to N lowest allies), Cleric (Light's Salvation pattern swap party→single).
- **Cooldown/charge model:** Crusader & Cleric (Sacred Bastion 2 charges), Arcanist (Counterspell/Frost Nova cd edits).
This is one engine feature with broad reach — flag it as the second-biggest lever after §A.

### C. Status authoring from talents (apply / tune / suppress statuses)
- **applyStatus-on-enemy from a talent (debuff rider):** Berserker (mortal-wound −50% healing — the P9 anti-heal, identity-critical), Pyromancer (stun on detonate), Arcanist (silence on Frost Nova, chill on barrier-break, +damageTaken-while-chilled), Mystic (silence on interrupt, +damage-taken-while-stunned), Bonecaller (rot contagion seed, on-death AoE rot pulse), Lifebinder (purge buff-strip rider), Archer (Mark `damageTaken` magnitude +12), Crusader (daze on Oath-expiry).
- **Per-status magnitude/duration tuning channel (distinct from dmgPct):** Berserker (Bleed +35%/+1t), Cleric (Burn +30% tick), Assassin (poison-tick mult), Pyromancer (Burn +25%/+1t), Lifebinder (HoT +mag/+1 tick/+dur), Bonecaller (DoT +15% / curse +dur), Arcanist (extra chill stack), Archer (`statusMod` on Mark).
- **Per-effect crit-suppression flag on a status:** Lifebinder (Drowned-Root, no-crit HoT), Bonecaller (Patient Decay), Cleric-adjacent.
- **Cleanse-immunity / exclude-from-burst flags on a status:** Bonecaller (Marrow-Deep Plague), Mystic (un-cleansable redirect).
A shared `statusMod` + `applyStatus` talent payload covers most of this; the suppress/immune flags are small booleans on the status instance.

### D. Atonement / damage-to-heal channel (healers)
- **Per-ability atonement overrides + disable flag + target-mode (rotate/exclude-last/party-swap):** Cleric (Hand of the Choir, Pyre-Tithe, Severing Light, Crowned in Ash).
- **damage-to-party-heal gated on lowest-ally-HP:** Cleric (Crowned in Ash, Executioner's self-extend), Lifebinder (Crowned in Ash). Same mechanic, two specs — build once.
Note `afterHit` already has an `atonement-heal` path with an `atonement-amp` onlyIf (`combat.ts:339-346`); this is an *extension*, not greenfield.

### E. Event-trigger riders (on-kill / on-crit / on-parry / on-detonate / on-cleanse / on-expiry)
The engine already has `reset-cooldown-on-kill` (`combat.ts:362`) and `reset-cooldown-on-crit` (`combat.ts:347`) ability-side. New talent-attachable riders needed:
- **on-kill:** resource refund (Berserker +2 Rampage), self-cd-reset target (Berserker Rampant Slaughter, Assassin finisher, Guardian grip), re-stealth+status (Assassin), instant pet re-raise (Bonecaller), AoE cascade (Bonecaller, Crusader-adjacent), conditional intake window (Assassin The Vanishing).
- **on-crit:** lifesteal/heal (Assassin, Guardian-adjacent), guaranteed-next-crit chain (Assassin Cold Cascade).
- **on-parry:** named-ability cd-reduction (Mystic Marrow-Drinker→Zen, Stonebound→Leg Sweep), heal-lowest-ally extend (Mystic Tidebound), parry→Leg-Sweep-reset chance (Mystic capstone).
- **on-detonate:** stun rider, splash+1, cd-refund on Ignite, self-DR window, full-pack contagion, execute stack-refund (Pyromancer — its whole tree is detonate-riders).
- **on-cleanse:** post-cleanse buff, +count, +dispelType (Cleric, Lifebinder).
- **on-effect-outcome:** Frost Nova resets on 2+ freezes (Arcanist), interrupt→silence (Mystic, Arcanist).
- **on-self-hit:** queued one-shot dmg buff (Guardian Vengeful Marrow), damage-to-ally-lifesteal (Guardian The Tide Repays).
- **on-expiry:** post-expiry self-DR window (Arcanist Stillwater Veil), CC on redirect-expiry (Crusader).
Recommend a single **`eventRider` talent shape** keyed by trigger enum — most of these are the same "on X, do {applyStatus | adjustCooldown | refundResource | heal | dealDamage}" pattern.

### F. Tank-tool extensions (existing ability mechanics widened)
- **thorns-reflect** → all-front-band + reflectPct scalar (Guardian; mechanic exists `combat.ts:250`).
- **grip** → +root, +target-count, on-kill-reset, convert-to-strike (Guardian).
- **intercept/Barrow-Step** → +immunity charge, +shadow-DR (Guardian).
- **Shield Wall block-reflect** → reflectPct + route reflected as real damage event (Guardian; `blockReflectPct` exists `combat.ts:484`); transferable-immunity variant (Guardian, Crusader).
- **redirect-damage** → +extra DR on redirected, min-1-HP death-immunity, multi-concurrent instances, un-cleansable (Crusader, Mystic; mechanic exists `combat.ts:230-238, 493-497`).
- **untargetable back-band aura while holding hard-taunt** + paired self −50% dmg (Guardian; needs threat-state read + a band-targetability flag — net-new).
- **shield variants:** no-crit-while-shielded, split-shield, shield-detonation-on-consume, DoT-immune absorb, overheal→shield on full-HP target, conditional overheal→shield magnitude (Crusader + Cleric — heavily overlapping shield-flag set, build as one shield-options bag).

### G. Typed-mitigation channels (require the engine to *tag* damage)
- **DoT/recurring-tick-only intakePct** (rot-keyed party DR) — Crusader (Tide-Mantle, Vault), Cleric/Lifebinder-adjacent. Requires tagging tick damage and a separate mitigation channel. This is real new plumbing distinct from §A's gating.
- **direct-hit-only absorb** (shield immune to DoT) — Cleric (Lantern, Light's Salvation), Crusader-adjacent. Same tag, applied to absorbs.

### H. The pet engine (largest single net-new system)
- **Persistent party-side summoned combatant** that acts on the tick loop, occupies a band, contributes output + threat, can re-raise off a corpse — Bonecaller (core), with a flyer-flock back-band variant. Confirmed there is **no party-side pet today**; the only `summon*` code (`engine.ts:192-208`) spawns an *enemy* guard. This is the one item that touches the engine's combatant model, threat, replay (`ReplayMob`), and turn scheduler. Scope it as its own milestone; the spec's `dmgPct` proxies are the fallback if the pet engine slips.

### I. Battle-rez / resource economy
- **Pre-placed single-use battle-rez bound to an ally**, wiring into `sim.rez*` — Bonecaller (The Last Vigil). Shared with the Pallbinder Soulstone family (ability-side). One mechanic, two necromancer specs.
- **Resource flags:** no-decay on a per-spec resource (Berserker Crimson Crescendo), resource-cost-to-heal inversion (Berserker Sanguine Tithe), DoT-detonation at a stack threshold (Bonecaller, Pyromancer execute-refund — same "consume-stacks-for-burst" primitive).

### J. Direct-damage & misc primitives
- **Direct (non-DoT) nuke from a DoT spec** scaling on missing HP (Bonecaller Hex of the Hollow Bell).
- **Self-anchored AoE damage aura** (Bonecaller Withering Aura).
- **repeatHit / echo rider** on an AoE ability (Archer Culling Storm — second 50% all-enemy hit). Pyromancer's contagion-spread is adjacent in spirit.

**Suggested build order:** §A (keystone, unblocks 11 specs) → §B (`abilityOverride`, broad) → §C+§E (status authoring + event riders, the bulk of DPS/healer trees) → §D (atonement) → §F+§G (tank tools + typed mitigation) → §H (pet, isolated milestone) → §I/§J (necromancer-specific + tail primitives). With §A–§E done, ~9 of 12 specs are fully expressible.

## 2. Build-diversity check (does every spec offer genuinely distinct builds?)

Mostly strong. The framework — Tier 1 engine, Tier 2 shape, Tier 3 fork, Tier 4 keyed-defense, Tier 5 re-key capstone — produces 4 distinct builds per spec where the *mechanics actually diverge*. Where I'd push back:

- **Pallbinder — flagged.** This is the weakest tree for genuine choice. Tiers 1, 2, 3, 5 are almost entirely `dmgPct` with different `onlyIf` gates (`enemiesAtLeast:3` / `targetHpBelowPct:35` / unconditional / crit). The four "builds" are really one healer with the AoE-vs-execute-vs-flat dial set four ways — there is exactly **one** mechanically novel node in the entire tree (Tier 4 Anima-Fed conditional intake). Compare to its mirror Lifebinder, which has HoT-persistence, cleanse-riders, atonement-conversion, ally-keyed DR — genuinely different *verbs*. Pallbinder's distinctive class-tools (Wraith conduit, Spark resource) are described as living entirely ability-side, so the *talents* never get to reshape them. **Recommendation:** push at least one of Wraith-retarget / Spark-spend-mode / drain-tag into the talent layer so a Pallbinder build *changes how the conduit works*, not just its multiplier. As written it risks being the "false choice" spec.

- **Pallbinder Tier 1 vs Tier 2 vs Tier 5 are structurally redundant** — three separate tiers each offering {AoE / execute / flat}. A player picks the same axis three times. Consider making one of those tiers about *Wraith behaviour* (who it drains, what the leech does) and another about *Spark* (bank/spend), so the axes are orthogonal.

- **Cleric Tier 5 "DoT-Sustain hybrid"** is explicitly acknowledged as a deliberate non-specialist ("weak at P6 by design"). That's honest, but it means build #4 is a watered blend of builds #2 and #3 rather than a fourth identity. Acceptable as designed, but it is the least distinct of the Cleric four.

- **Bonecaller, Mystic, Guardian, Crusader, Pyromancer, Arcanist, Assassin** — all pass cleanly: each build changes a *verb* (pet vs curse-detonate vs rot-spread vs drain; control vs lifesteal vs peel vs execute; reflect vs anchor vs collapse vs survival). No false choices spotted.

- **One cross-spec watch-item:** the Tier-4 "keyed defense, never flat DR" rule is well-honoured *except* where specs offer a `maxHpPct` option (Archer Quarried Stillness, Berserker Clotted Vigor, Lifebinder Deeprooted, Bonecaller, Pallbinder The Quiet Between). Each correctly self-labels these as "deliberately weak in the timer regime," which is consistent — but five specs leaning on the same "+10-12% maxHP = the weak safe pick" makes that node feel like boilerplate. It reads as the intended floor, so I'd keep it, just be aware it's the most-repeated single node in the roster.

The gothic/Ashveil voice ("barrow," "marrow," "tide," "drowned," "carrion," "rot," "bell/toll," "cinder/ember," "frost/cold") is strong and consistent. Collisions and drifts:

- **Reused node names across specs (intentional cross-spec motif or accidental collision?):**
  - **"Crowned in Ash"** — Cleric Tier 4 *and* Lifebinder Tier 5. Both are the same mechanic (smite-atonement gated on lowest-ally-HP), so the shared name actually signals a shared engine effect — fine, but confirm it's deliberate.
  - **"Unbinding Litany"** — Cleric Tier 3 *and* Lifebinder Tier 2. Both are cleanse-upgrade nodes — again a deliberate-looking cross-healer echo, but two healers sharing an exact node name may confuse the UI.
  - **"Grave-Iron Resolve"** — Guardian Tier 1 *and* Archer Tier 4. Different mechanics (Guardian dmg floor vs Archer conditional intake). This one is a genuine collision worth renaming one side.
  - **"Stillwater Aegis"** — Guardian Tier 4 *and* Crusader Tier 2. Both are the immunity/charge tank tool — plausibly intentional (two paladins/warriors), but identical names on two tank specs in the same screen is risky.
  - **"Second Wind"** — Berserker Tier 4 *and* Mystic Tier 3. Both are low-HP comeback levers; very generic name, appears twice.
  - **"Killing Frost"** — Assassin Tier 4 *and* Arcanist Tier 1. Assassin's is thematically odd (a rogue with "Frost"? the spec is stealth/poison) — that one drifts; rename the Assassin node to something blade/cold-knife flavoured if you want, but "Frost" on a non-mage reads as a leftover.
  - **"The Last Vigil"** — Bonecaller Tier 5 (battle-rez) *and* Pallbinder Tier 2 (a +healing dmgPct node). Same name, wildly different weight (one is a unique mechanic, one is a stat stick). Rename the Pallbinder one.
  - **"Cold Focus"** — Archer Tier 3, Arcanist Tier 4, Assassin's "Cold Patience/Cold Cascade" cluster. "Cold" is doing heavy lifting across three rogue/mage specs.
  - **"Even Temper / Even Tending / Even Tithe / Even Reckoning"** — the "Even ___" balanced-pick naming convention is used in Berserker, Lifebinder, Pallbinder. This is a *nice* convention if intentional (signals "the safe node"), but make it deliberate and complete or drop it.
  - **"Bell-Warden('s Ember)"** — Arcanist capstone *and* Pyromancer capstone, plus "Bell of Cleansing Light" (Cleric), "Hex of the Hollow Bell" (Bonecaller), "Bell-Warden" (Arcanist). The bell motif is everywhere; fine thematically but "Bell-Warden" specifically appears on two DPS capstones.

- **Voice drift:** "Hate-Forged Edge," "Even Temper," "Patient Blade," "Steady Quarrel," "Steady Flame," "Resonant Cadence," "Wild Growth" are flatter/more-generic than the surrounding gothic names. They're all the "safe balanced pick" nodes, so the blandness correlates with the boring-by-design node — arguably appropriate, but a couple ("Wild Growth," "Killing Frost" on the Assassin) read as borrowed-from-WoW and brush the IP concern flagged in CLAUDE.md.

The memory file flags WoW-trademark scrubbing as a hard pre-launch gate. Several of these are *ability* names the talents reference rather than talent names themselves, but the revamp is a good moment to rename the abilities too. Flagging because adding 12 new trees is exactly when new WoW-verbatim strings creep in.

## 4. Coverage of the choice axes across the roster

Mapping builds to the Phase-P problem axes (P1 interrupt, P2 resist-wall, P3 armour-wall, P4 dispel, P5 spike, P6 drowning-rot, P7 eruption-peel, P8 dangerous-add, P9 anti-heal/purge, P11 reach, P12 soft-enrage):

- **Well-covered (multiple specs answer):** P3 armour-wall (Pyromancer, Cleric, Arcanist, Lifebinder, Berserker), P8 dangerous-add (Arcanist, Assassin, Pyromancer, Mystic, Archer), P11 reach (Guardian-grip, Assassin-dive, Archer-native, Pyromancer-Far-Cast, Bonecaller-flock — five distinct mechanical answers, excellent), P12 soft-enrage (Berserker, Assassin, Archer, Pyromancer, Pallbinder, Mystic, Bonecaller), P5 spike (Cleric, Crusader, Mystic, Lifebinder-partial).
- **Covered but thin:** P6 drowning-rot — answered by Lifebinder (native), Crusader (Tide-Warden), Mystic (Shared Burden), Pallbinder (attrition). Good *that* it's no longer Lifebinder-mandatory (the Crusader rot-keyed party-DR explicitly exists to break that). But all four are tanks/healers — no DPS rot-survival answer, which is by design.
- **Single-point-of-failure axes (only ONE spec answers):**
  - **P9 anti-heal / self-healing boss** — **only Berserker (Grave-Wound).** This is called out as "the ONLY tool that beats a self-healing boss no purge can touch." Lifebinder/Bonecaller offer *purge* (buff-strip), which the dossier distinguishes from anti-heal. If a group has no Berserker and the week is a self-healing boss, there is no answer. That's a deliberate class-matters spike, but it's the sharpest single-point dependency in the roster — worth confirming the dungeon scheduler never forces P9 in a way that hard-walls Berserker-less groups.
  - **P1 interrupt as a *tight-loop tyrant*** — Arcanist (The Long Winter) is the cleanest, with Mystic (Iron Discipline) and Pyromancer/Crusader (delay, not true kick) as softer answers. Reasonably covered.
  - **P4 dispel by type** — Cleric (Magic/Curse + Nature/Poison via Unbinding) and Lifebinder (Nature/Poison) split the dispel-type coverage. A Magic/Curse-heavy week with a Lifebinder-only healer has a gap. Worth a coverage matrix check.
- **Axis the roster is rich in:** *transferable single-event negation* (Crusader's entire identity, Guardian Stillwater Aegis, Cleric Lantern, Mystic Severing Link) — P5/P7 is over-served relative to P9. Not a problem, but note the asymmetry.

Overall the roster delivers on "class choice matters (+3-5 keys, diverse answers)": every Phase-P axis has at least one answer, and the *crowded* axes (P3, P8, P11, P12) each get **mechanically distinct** answers rather than reskins. The risk concentrations are P9 (one spec) and dispel-type coverage (two specs splitting four types).

## 5. Open questions for the user

2. **`abilityOverride` shape — one generic patch effect, or per-param typed effects?** A single `abilityOverride: { abilityId, params? targeting? modifiers? cooldownTurns? charges? }` is DRY but loosens Zod validation (a typo'd param wouldn't fail-closed the way the current closed enum does). Do you want the validation strictness preserved (→ enumerate each override kind) or the flexibility (→ one bag)?
3. **Pallbinder build-diversity — acceptable as a "dial-only" tree, or push Wraith/Spark into talents?** As written it's the one spec whose four builds share a single axis (see §2). Do you want me to redesign 1-2 tiers to reshape the conduit/resource, or is a deliberately-simple necromancer-healer intended?
4. **P9 single-point dependency — is Berserker-only anti-heal intended to be a hard wall?** If a self-healing-boss week can be scheduled, a group with no Berserker has no counter. Confirm the dungeon scheduler gates around this, or whether a second (e.g. Pallbinder/Bonecaller) anti-heal tool should exist.
5. **Pet engine — its own milestone, or block Bonecaller on it?** It's the only item touching the combatant model + threat + replay. Do you want Bonecaller shipped on `dmgPct` proxies first (the spec provides them) with the real pet as a follow-up, or hold the spec until the pet engine lands?
